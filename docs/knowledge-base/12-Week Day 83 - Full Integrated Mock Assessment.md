## Purpose

Complete an original full mock that integrates source navigation, bounded design, transparent calculations, inspection-record interpretation, verification planning and fault reasoning within one controlled evidence system.

## Practical applications

- defining installation, equipment, circuit, source-state, operating-state, time, evidence, authority and requested-decision boundaries;
- coordinating interacting written, design, calculation, inspection, verification and diagnostic workstreams;
- preserving source identity, currency, scope, applicability and calculation provenance;
- separating literal evidence, derived facts, interpretations, assumptions, contradictions and conclusions;
- maintaining competing hypotheses with falsifiable predictions and discriminating evidence;
- stopping at the first unsupported transition and assigning an evidence owner plus recheck trigger;
- reopening complete dependency chains after two sequential material changes; and
- packaging an untouched submission for evidence-led review.

## Assessment relevance

The simulation creates structured evidence of integrated learning performance. The independent `secure`, `developing`, `unsupported` and `stop-required` states are educational review states, not official grades, competency decisions, technical findings or permission for unsupervised work. A non-compensatory blocker prevents a `secure` learning-readiness summary even when unrelated parts of the response are strong.

## References and review boundary

Exact technical requirements, clauses, limits, methods, source values, acceptance decisions, role permissions and official assessment conditions require current authorised sources, relevant RTO instructions and qualified review.
