# 12-Week Day 03 - Roles Authority Supervision and Practical Stop Conditions

## Purpose

This note connects Day 3 to the wider Capstone knowledge graph. It separates technical capability from permission, makes authority evidence inspectable and treats stop conditions as part of competent performance.

## Core concepts

- **Role:** recognised function in a learning or work system; it does not independently authorise every task.
- **Competence:** demonstrated task-specific capability; it does not automatically establish permission.
- **Task authority:** explicit permission from the correct person or system for a defined activity under stated conditions.
- **Authority evidence:** information supporting that permission is direct and current, indirect, incomplete or unsupported.
- **Scope:** the outer limit of permitted action created by legal, licence, training, workplace, task, method and supervision boundaries.
- **Supervision:** arranged direction, monitoring, communication and intervention matched to the task and learner.
- **Stop condition:** a circumstance that suspends the task and requires authorised direction.
- **Re-authorisation trigger:** a material change that makes the earlier permission incomplete or unreliable.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest point where a proposed action moves beyond the evidence-backed authority envelope.

## Reasoning workflow

Use **A-U-T-H-O-R-I-T-Y**:

1. Activity
2. User role
3. Task authority and evidence
4. Hazards and controls
5. Oversight
6. Rules and method
7. In-scope boundary
8. Triggers to stop
9. Yield and escalate

Apply the workflow before a scenario crosses from discussion, observation or documentation into practical interaction, and repeat it after any material change.

## Evidence model

Authority evidence is classified for learning purposes as:

- **direct and current:** issued by the authorised person or controlling system for the current task and conditions;
- **indirect:** relayed or inferred from custom, roster or previous work;
- **incomplete:** the source may be valid but task, limits, method, supervision or conditions are missing;
- **unsupported:** based on confidence, urgency, silence, habit or assumed seniority.

These are educational categories, not legal classifications. Exact legal, licence, RTO and workplace boundaries remain `reference_check_required`.

## Practical application

The learner completes an authority evidence ledger recording:

- the exact activity verb and equipment or location identifier;
- requester, claimed authoriser and evidence of authority;
- role and relevant capability evidence;
- approved method and supervision arrangement;
- permitted and excluded actions;
- hazards, missing or contradictory evidence and re-authorisation triggers;
- the first unsupported transition;
- a specific stop statement, escalation owner and recheck condition.

A changed-condition re-attempt checks whether the learner rebuilds the authority envelope rather than carrying permission forward automatically.

## Assessment relevance

A secure response:

- separates facts, inferences, gaps and assumptions;
- ranks authority evidence rather than accepting a relayed instruction at face value;
- identifies the first unsupported transition;
- explains why the supervision arrangement is or is not adequate for the exact task;
- states a specific stop and escalation route;
- transfers the reasoning to changed conditions.

A correct stop reached for an unsupported reason is safer than proceeding but remains incomplete evidence. A technically plausible action outside supported scope is unsatisfactory.
