## Core concepts

- **Role:** the recognised function held by a person in a learning or work system.
- **Competence:** demonstrated task-specific capability; it does not automatically establish permission.
- **Task authority:** explicit permission from the correct person or system for a defined activity under stated conditions.
- **Scope:** the limit of permitted action created by legal, licence, training, workplace, task and supervision boundaries.
- **Supervision:** an arranged control with defined direction, monitoring, communication and intervention.
- **Stop condition:** a circumstance that suspends the task and requires authorised direction.
- **Re-authorisation trigger:** a material change that makes the original permission incomplete or unreliable.

## Reasoning workflow

Use **A-U-T-H-O-R-I-T-Y**:

1. Activity
2. User role
3. Task authority
4. Hazards and controls
5. Oversight
6. Rules and method
7. In-scope boundary
8. Triggers to stop
9. Yield and escalate

The workflow should be applied before a scenario crosses from discussion, observation or documentation into practical interaction.

## Practical application

The learner completes an authority-boundary worksheet that records:

- the exact activity verb;
- the source of authority;
- role and capability evidence;
- supervision arrangements;
- permitted and excluded actions;
- hazards and controls;
- re-authorisation triggers;
- stop and escalation points.

A changed-condition re-attempt checks whether the learner recognises that authority must be reviewed when equipment, energy sources, environment, personnel, procedure or supervision changes.

## Assessment relevance

A strong response separates technical knowledge from authority and identifies the first unsupported transition. It does not receive credit for a technically plausible action that falls outside stated scope or bypasses supervision and stop conditions.

## Safety boundary

This note authorises no switching, isolation, testing, opening of equipment, resetting, disconnection, alteration, repair, rescue, energisation or verification. Licensing, legal duties, supervision requirements, RTO conditions and workplace responsibilities require confirmation from current authorised sources.
