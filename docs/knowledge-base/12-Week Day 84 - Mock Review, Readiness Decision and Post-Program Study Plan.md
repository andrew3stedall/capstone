# 12-Week Day 84 - Mock Review, Readiness Decision and Post-Program Study Plan

## Purpose

Review the untouched integrated mock through explicit evidence and dependency controls, make a bounded learning-readiness decision and create an evidence-led four-week study plan without claiming formal competency, technical approval or practical authority.

## Practical applications

- preserving authentic performance evidence and recording integrity limits;
- reconstructing installation, equipment, circuit, source-state, operating-state, time, evidence, authority and requested-decision boundaries;
- separating literal evidence, derived facts, interpretations, assumptions and contradictions;
- identifying the first unsupported transition and reopening dependent conclusions;
- assigning independent `secure`, `developing`, `unsupported` and `stop-required` states;
- prioritising safety-critical, authority and dependency-blocking errors;
- limiting remediation to observable root mechanisms;
- assigning evidence owners and recheck triggers;
- testing repairs in changed contexts; and
- planning ongoing retrieval, application, recovery and qualified escalation.

## Assessment relevance

The review converts an original mock into a bounded learning plan. It does not reproduce official assessment material, establish an RTO result, declare competency, technically accept an installation or authorise unsupervised work. A correct result, high confidence, good presentation or completion within a learner-selected time limit cannot compensate for an unresolved blocker.

## Readiness model

Each criterion is reviewed independently:

- `secure` means complete, traceable and supported within the stated boundary;
- `developing` means a bounded weakness remains without an unsafe or unsupported propagated conclusion;
- `unsupported` means the reasoning exceeds evidence, omits a dependency or cannot be reproduced; and
- `stop-required` means a safety-critical, authority, evidence-integrity or prerequisite blocker prevents a defensible readiness decision.

The readiness categories are educational only: ready with maintenance, ready with targeted repair, or not yet ready. They are not grades, pass marks, competency decisions or legal conclusions.

## Post-program evidence

A defensible four-week plan records no more than three remediation targets. Each target includes the observable behaviour, root mechanism, affected dependencies, evidence owner, recheck trigger, changed-context transfer task, closure evidence, recovery control and any qualified-review requirement.

## References and review boundary

Exact technical acceptance, official assessment conditions, source values, competency requirements, role permissions and practical authority require current authorised sources, relevant RTO instructions and qualified review. Automated content remains `review-required`, `reference_check_required`, safety-critical and not `technically-reviewed`.

The module authorises no site access, opening, switching, isolation, proving de-energised, testing, measurement, instrument use, alteration, repair, energisation, commissioning, acceptance, certification, verification or field fault finding.
