# 12-Week Day 39 - Accessibility Labelling and Original Defect-Recognition Scenarios

## Purpose

Develops evidence-controlled observation, consequence reasoning and bounded defect-candidate reporting for fictional accessibility and identification scenarios.

## Core concepts

- **Accessibility:** ability of the intended authorised person to reach, identify or operate equipment for a defined purpose under defined conditions.
- **Identification:** information intended to connect equipment, circuits, sources or functions to an understandable reference.
- **Viewing boundary:** the part of a scene or record actually available.
- **Evidence boundary:** the point beyond which available records cannot support a reliable claim.
- **Authority boundary:** the limit of permitted inspection, decision or recommendation.
- **Defect candidate:** a condition requiring verification and consequence assessment, not an automated non-compliance declaration.
- **Consequence pathway:** the credible link between a condition and an affected safety, isolation, protection, identification or operating function.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest unsupported rung in a claim chain; dependent conclusions stop there.
- **Change propagation:** reopening all conclusions that depend on a changed condition.

## Evidence states

Use these labels explicitly:

- **stated fact**
- **derived fact**
- **supported inference**
- **assumption**
- **contradiction**
- **evidence gap**

For unresolved matters, retain **competing interpretations**, identify an **evidence owner**, and set a **recheck trigger**.

## Workflow

**L-A-B-E-L-S**:

1. **Limit** the decision, viewing, evidence and authority boundaries.
2. **Annotate** direct observations, evidence states and provenance.
3. **Bind** each item to an affected function and consequence pathway.
4. **Examine** current source applicability, contradictions and competing interpretations.
5. **Locate** the first unsupported transition and stop dependent claims.
6. **State** the bounded candidate, missing evidence, owner, trigger, escalation and prohibited actions.

## Practical applications

- analyse original fictional accessibility and identification scenarios;
- distinguish current evidence from historical or cropped records;
- build claim ladders from evidence identity to bounded defect candidate;
- preserve conflicting records rather than selecting convenient evidence;
- assign evidence owners and recheck triggers;
- propagate at least two material scenario changes; and
- write bounded escalation questions without declaring compliance.

## Assessment relevance

Observable evidence includes the learner's ability to:

- control viewing, evidence and authority boundaries;
- classify evidence and cite provenance;
- connect observations to affected switchboard functions;
- identify credible consequence pathways;
- preserve contradictions and competing interpretations;
- stop at the first unsupported transition;
- design proportionate escalation; and
- transfer reasoning under changed conditions.

Use **secure**, **developing**, **unsupported** and `stop-required` as independent learning-planning states. They are not official assessment grades, competency decisions or technical approvals.

## Blocking conditions

A secure outcome is blocked by:

- invented dimensions, source paths, label content, operating states or current conditions;
- treating labels, photographs or drawings as proof without corroboration;
- ignored contradictions;
- conclusions carried beyond the first unsupported transition;
- appearance used as a compliance test;
- failure to reopen dependencies after material change; or
- unauthorised practical or compliance claims.
