# 12-Week Day 55 - Mixed Special-Location Scenario Workshop

## Purpose

Integrate location applicability, source-state, design-intent and inspection-observation reasoning without collapsing distinct problems into one checklist or proceeding beyond unsupported evidence.

## Learning module

- [Day 55 — Mixed Special-Location Scenario Workshop](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-55-mixed-special-location-scenario-workshop.md)

## Core method

Use **L-A-Y-E-R-S**:

1. locate and state all material boundaries;
2. audit evidence states, provenance and source applicability;
3. yield separate location, source, design and inspection layers;
4. expose dependencies and first unsupported transitions;
5. retain competing interpretations and assign resolution ownership; and
6. state bounded outcomes, then stress-test after two material changes.

## Core concepts

- **Boundary:** explicit limit of location, activity, user, equipment, supply, operating state, evidence, authority or decision scope.
- **Evidence state:** stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction or evidence gap.
- **Provenance:** source, date or revision, issuer and scenario connection.
- **Reasoning layer:** one analytical view retained separately from other views.
- **Claim chain:** ordered path from evidence identity to a bounded conclusion.
- **First unsupported transition:** earliest claim step lacking adequate evidence.
- **Competing interpretations:** plausible readings retained until evidence resolves them.
- **Evidence owner:** authorised source, person or qualified reviewer responsible for a gap.
- **Recheck trigger:** evidence or change requiring dependent reasoning to reopen.
- **Material change:** condition capable of changing classification, source coverage, controls, isolation or acceptance reasoning.

Confidence is recorded separately as guessing, unsure, reasonably confident or certain.

## Practical applications

- build separate location/applicability, source/state, design-intent and inspection-observation layers;
- classify evidence and record provenance before evaluating claims;
- trace claim chains to their first unsupported transition;
- retain competing interpretations for contradictions;
- assign evidence owners and recheck triggers;
- communicate supported and unsupported claims separately; and
- rebuild dependencies after two material changes.

## Assessment relevance

Evaluate boundary control, evidence discipline, layer separation, dependency control, resolution planning, two-change transfer and safety communication independently.

Use **secure**, **developing**, **unsupported** and `stop-required` only as educational planning states. They are not official grades, competency decisions, defect classifications, compliance decisions or technical approvals.

## Blocking conditions

Progression is blocked by an omitted material boundary, hidden contradiction, assumption presented as fact, collapsed design and inspection evidence, omitted source state, reasoning beyond the first unsupported transition, unresolved blocker without ownership, cosmetic-only transfer or implied practical or compliance authority.
