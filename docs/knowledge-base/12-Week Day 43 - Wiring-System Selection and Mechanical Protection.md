# 12-Week Day 43 - Wiring-System Selection and Mechanical Protection

## Purpose

Builds evidence-controlled reasoning for selecting a complete wiring-system concept across a segmented route without converting paper analysis into an installation instruction or compliance conclusion.

## Core method

Use **R-O-U-T-E**:

1. restrict and record the boundaries, complete route and interfaces;
2. observe and organise route segments, influences, provenance and contradictions;
3. use current authorised and manufacturer evidence;
4. test each candidate function against every material influence and stop at the first unsupported transition; and
5. explain the bounded recommendation, evidence owners, recheck triggers and change propagation.

## Core concepts

- **Wiring system:** conductors, insulation, sheath, containment, supports, entries and associated protection considered together.
- **Route envelope:** the complete path plus interfaces, surrounding conditions and foreseeable material influences.
- **Condition segment:** a route part within which material influences are reasonably consistent.
- **Mechanical influence:** impact, crushing, abrasion, penetration, movement, vibration or strain exposure.
- **Mechanical-protection concept:** paper-level proposal for reducing an evidenced exposure, not a construction instruction.
- **Evidence provenance:** source, date, described condition and applicability of evidence.
- **Competing interpretations:** plausible explanations retained until evidence resolves them.
- **First unsupported transition:** earliest point where a claim exceeds its support; dependent claims stop there.
- **Evidence owner:** person, current document set, authorised source or reviewer expected to resolve a gap.
- **Recheck trigger:** new evidence or changed conditions requiring earlier conclusions to reopen.
- **Bounded recommendation:** result limited to the evidence, scenario, authority and unresolved constraints.

## Practical applications

- map a complete fictional route and its interfaces;
- segment the route by evidenced conditions;
- classify facts, inferences, assumptions, contradictions and gaps;
- compare conductor, containment, support, entry and protection functions;
- preserve conflicting current and historic route records;
- assign evidence owners and recheck triggers;
- stop whole-route claims when one segment remains unsupported; and
- propagate at least two material scenario changes.

## Assessment relevance

Evaluate boundary control, route completeness, segmentation, terminology, evidence classification, candidate comparison, first-unsupported-transition control, change propagation, evidence ownership and safety communication independently.

Use **secure**, **developing**, **unsupported** and `stop-required` as educational planning states. They are not official assessment grades, competency outcomes, defect classifications, compliance decisions or technical approvals. No aggregate score or unofficial pass threshold applies.

## Blocking conditions

Secure readiness is blocked by invented route conditions or construction, endpoint-only reasoning, treating containment as universal protection, unsupported current-condition claims, concealed contradictions, whole-route suitability claims with unresolved segments, missing evidence owners or recheck triggers, incomplete change propagation, transfer using fewer than two material changes, or unauthorised practical activity.
