## Core concepts

- **Baseline diagnostic:** a low-stakes starting sample used to locate strengths, misconceptions and process gaps; it is not an official competency decision.
- **Confidence calibration:** comparison between certainty and checked answer quality, with high-confidence errors prioritised.
- **Authorised-source map:** a record of source family, title, version, jurisdiction, access, intended use, applicability and unresolved checks.
- **Error type:** knowledge, navigation, process, applicability or unsafe-assumption failure.
- **M-A-P-S:** Match the source family; Authenticate access and currency; Prove applicability; State the evidence boundary.

## Practical application

The learner creates three retained artefacts:

1. an eight-domain closed-note baseline record;
2. an authorised-source map;
3. a prioritised error log with one selected remediation target.

These artefacts should be revisited at weekly consolidation blocks and during mock-review work to show changes in retrieval, process and confidence rather than page completion alone.

## Assessment relevance

The module supports source selection, evidence trails, confidence calibration and explicit stop conditions. It does not claim an official pass mark, permitted-resource rule, competency threshold or universal Capstone format.

## Safety boundary

This note and linked module do not authorise practical electrical work. Any practical activity remains subject to legislation, licence and training limits, supervision, approved workplace procedures and direction from an authorised competent person.

## References and currency

Exact technical, legal, jurisdiction-specific and assessment claims require verification against current authorised sources. Automated content remains `review-required`, `reference_check_required` and not `technically-reviewed`.
