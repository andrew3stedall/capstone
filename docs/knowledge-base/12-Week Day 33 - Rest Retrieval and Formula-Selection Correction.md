# 12-Week Day 33 - Rest Retrieval and Formula-Selection Correction

## Purpose

Provides a bounded recovery block for confidence-calibrated retrieval, formula-selection error diagnosis, causal correction, fatigue management and readiness checking without adding new electrical theory.

## Core concepts

- **Confidence calibration:** compare confidence recorded before checking with the quality of the checked answer.
- **Formula-selection error:** a relationship does not match the required quantity, boundary, variables or authorised conditions.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest point where supported reasoning becomes unsupported.
- **Downstream consequence:** a result or conclusion dependent on an earlier step.
- **Evidence owner:** the person or role responsible for resolving a named gap.
- **Recheck trigger:** the event that requires dependent work to reopen.
- **Correction limit:** no more than three repairs or 30 minutes.
- **Stop condition:** fatigue, repeated guessing, frustration, missing authorised evidence or safety uncertainty.

## Evidence labels

Use stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction and evidence gap. A numerical result is not a supported conclusion when its boundary, inputs, applicability or method provenance remain unresolved.

## R-E-S-E-T workflow

1. **Retrieve and rate confidence** for V-O-L-T-S, L-O-O-P-S and C-H-A-I-N-S.
2. **Examine** quantity, boundary, inputs, method, result and conclusion separately.
3. **Sort** the earliest error as input, boundary, selection, unit, applicability, arithmetic, evidence or conclusion.
4. **Edit** from the first unsupported transition and reopen downstream consequences.
5. **Terminate and transfer** after three repairs, 30 minutes or a stop condition; record an evidence owner, recheck trigger and materially changed transfer case.

## Practical application

- Complete a six-minute closed-note retrieval set.
- Redraw a voltage boundary, conceptual fault loop and protection chain.
- Select no more than three high-value errors, prioritising safety or authority overreach and upstream errors with broad consequences.
- Repair from the earliest causal point rather than polishing downstream arithmetic.
- Change at least two material scenario conditions and rebuild affected reasoning.

## Assessment relevance

Record each criterion independently as **secure**, **developing**, **unsupported** or `stop-required` for workflow retrieval, confidence calibration, error classification, first-unsupported-transition control, downstream reopening, evidence restraint and stop-rule compliance.

These are educational planning states only. They are not official grades, competency decisions or authority to perform electrical work.

## Blocking conditions

Invented formulas or values, ignored contradictions, hidden assumptions, unsupported acceptance claims, work beyond a stop condition and unauthorised practical actions cannot be offset by stronger performance elsewhere.

## Safety boundary

Written recovery only. No switching, isolation, opening, measuring, testing, fault injection, energisation, commissioning, certification or field work is authorised. Missing source evidence or safety uncertainty requires a bounded stop rather than guessing.
