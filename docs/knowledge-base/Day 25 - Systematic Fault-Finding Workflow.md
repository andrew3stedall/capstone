## Core concept

Fault finding is controlled uncertainty reduction. A symptom defines a search boundary but does not prove a cause. The learner must preserve the distinction between reported symptoms, confirmed observations, operating states, hypotheses, discriminating evidence, contradictions and bounded conclusions.

The **F-A-U-L-T** workflow is:

1. **Frame the symptom and boundary**.
2. **Account for hazards and sources**.
3. **Understand the system and generate hypotheses**.
4. **Locate discriminating evidence**.
5. **Test the explanation on paper and transfer the result**.

A high-value evidence question safely separates leading hypotheses while preserving the original condition and evidence provenance.

## Practical application

Use a hypothesis matrix to compare supply, protection, control, load, connection and environmental explanations. Record evidence supporting and contradicting each explanation, then identify the smallest authorised evidence question that would remove the most uncertainty.

The final handover must state the symptom, operating state, boundary, known sources, evidence quality, live hypotheses, contradictions, stop conditions and qualified next action. It must not turn an incomplete diagnosis into a repair instruction.

## Assessment relevance

This block prepares the learner to structure a fault scenario, resist premature component replacement, rank evidence questions, explain diagnostic uncertainty and produce a traceable handover. It interleaves source awareness, test-purpose reasoning and result interpretation from Days 20C–24.
