# 12-Week Day 76 - Timed Integrated Scenario with Worked-Example Fading Removed

## Purpose

Test whether the learner can independently reconstruct the integrated Capstone workflow under a controlled educational time limit while preserving evidence, dependency, time and authority boundaries.

## Learning module

- [Day 76 — Timed Integrated Scenario with Worked-Example Fading Removed](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-76-timed-integrated-scenario-with-worked-example-fading-removed.md)

## Core method

Use **P-E-R-F-O-R-M**: Protect the review reserve and boundaries; Extract literal evidence; Route dependencies; Form bounded claims; Observe time gates; Reopen after change; Make the final integrated review.

## Core concepts

- **Scenario boundary:** the defined installation, equipment, circuit, source, operating state, time, evidence, authority and requested decision.
- **Independent reconstruction:** rebuilding the integrated workflow without worked prompts or hidden hints.
- **Review reserve:** time protected from the start for coverage, contradiction, change, traceability and safety checks.
- **Time gate:** a planned point for bounded completion or an explicit limitation before moving to the next deliverable.
- **Bounded completion:** finishing the defensible portion of an artefact while preserving unresolved dependencies.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest unsupported step in a claim chain; later dependent conclusions remain blocked.
- **Evidence owner:** the authorised person, source or record responsible for resolving an open gap.
- **Recheck trigger:** the new evidence event that permits reconsideration.
- **Material change:** a change to identity, source, state, route, equipment, environment, protective arrangement or evidence that can alter prior reasoning.
- **Non-compensatory blocker:** a safety, identity, source-state, evidence or authority weakness that cannot be averaged away.
- **Educational readiness states:** `secure`, `developing`, `unsupported` and `stop-required`; these are not official grades or competency decisions.

## Practical applications

Prepare and complete a fictional timed integrated response that:

- defines nine scenario boundaries before solving;
- protects a final review reserve;
- separates literal facts, derived facts, supported inferences, assumptions, contradictions and evidence gaps;
- classifies evidence as verified, supported but limited, conflicting, stale or superseded, not applicable or missing;
- creates all nine required design, inspection, verification, diagnostic and documentation artefacts;
- stops at first unsupported transitions;
- assigns evidence owners and recheck triggers;
- reopens dependencies after two sequential material changes; and
- preserves the timed attempt for Day 77 rather than rewriting it.

## Assessment relevance

Review boundary control, time and completion control, evidence discipline, dependency and change control, integrated reasoning, and communication and escalation independently. A `stop-required` criterion, or an `unsupported` non-compensatory blocker, prevents a readiness recommendation regardless of stronger performance elsewhere.

The original 90-minute structure is an educational pacing design only. It is not an official RTO, regulator or licensing assessment duration.

## Blocking conditions

Progress is blocked by unidentified boundaries, lost review reserve combined with guessed blockers, fabricated or silently transferred evidence, stale evidence treated as current after a material change, dependent conclusions beyond an unsupported transition, invented exact requirements or procedures, hidden safety limitations, and claims of competency, compliance, successful correction or technical approval.
