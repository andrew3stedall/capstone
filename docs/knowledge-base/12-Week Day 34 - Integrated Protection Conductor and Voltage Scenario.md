## Core concepts

Integrated scenario; design gate; dependency; reopening trigger; evidence register; evidence owner; competing interpretation; first unsupported transition; bounded conclusion.

Evidence is classified as a stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction or evidence gap. These states control how far a conclusion may progress.

## Workflow

**I-N-T-E-G-R-A-T-E** defines the boundary and operating cases, names evidence needs, traces cross-domain dependencies, checks source applicability, gates claims, reopens affected work, audits contradictions, transfers reasoning under changed conditions and limits the final claim.

The first unsupported transition is the earliest point where evidence no longer supports the next claim. It blocks all dependent conclusions until an identified evidence owner resolves the gap and the recorded trigger causes a recheck.

## Practical applications

- build an evidence register before calculations or selections;
- preserve conflicting route, conductor, device and operating-case records as competing interpretations;
- map conductor, voltage, fault-return and protection dependencies;
- reopen affected checks after at least two material scenario changes;
- explain why unaffected checks remain closed; and
- defend a bounded candidate without presenting it as approval.

## Assessment relevance

Learners produce a traceable written artefact, explain the reasoning chain, identify the first unsupported transition, respond to changed conditions and record criterion-level readiness as **secure**, **developing**, **unsupported** or `stop-required`.

These are educational planning states, not official grades, competency decisions or technical approval. They are not combined into an aggregate score because a safety or evidence failure cannot be offset by strength elsewhere.

## Safety boundary

Written educational reasoning only. Missing evidence, unresolved source applicability, contradictory records, unclear authority or any implied field action requires a bounded stop and escalation. No switching, isolation, opening, measuring, testing, adjustment, installation, energisation, commissioning, certification or verification is authorised.
