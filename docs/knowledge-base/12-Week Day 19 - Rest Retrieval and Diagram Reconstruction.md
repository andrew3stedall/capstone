## Core concepts

- **Closed-note retrieval:** recall before rereading.
- **Diagram reconstruction:** rebuild a model from memory and then compare it with the source module.
- **Misconception:** a stable but incorrect mental model.
- **Error mechanism:** the reason an error occurred, such as term substitution, role confusion, path omission, unsupported inference, overconfidence or safety-boundary drift.
- **Confidence calibration:** comparing confidence with correctness and evidence quality; a correct guess is not secure knowledge.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest link where a reconstructed explanation moves beyond the available evidence.
- **Blocking condition:** a path, evidence, authority or safety error that cannot be averaged away.
- **Evidence owner:** the authorised person, record or source needed to resolve a material gap.
- **Recheck trigger:** the evidence or corrected response that causes the readiness decision to be reconsidered.
- **Catch-up triage:** selecting one small prerequisite gap rather than attempting all unfinished work.
- **Readiness statement:** a supported criterion-level decision to progress, remediate or seek help.

## R-E-D-R-A-W workflow

1. **Retrieve** without notes and record confidence.
2. **Examine** differences against the completed modules and label the evidence.
3. **Diagnose** the error category, mechanism and first unsupported transition.
4. **Repair** one high-value misconception.
5. **Attempt again** using a varied prompt with at least two changed conditions.
6. **Withdraw on time** at the session limit or stop condition.

## Evidence labels

Use stated fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction and evidence gap. A high-confidence unsupported answer receives priority over a low-confidence omission.

## Practical application

The learner reconstructs the Week 3 terminology map and separate normal-current and conceptual fault-current models, identifies the first unsupported transition, repairs two high-priority misconceptions, completes one changed-context transfer, performs at most one bounded catch-up task and records criterion-level readiness for Day 20.

## Assessment relevance

Observable evidence includes:

- accurate separation of normal-current and conceptual fault-current paths;
- recognition that a conceptual diagram is not verification evidence;
- correction of high-confidence unsupported answers;
- transfer under at least two changed conditions;
- explicit evidence owners and recheck triggers for unresolved criteria;
- limited catch-up scope and time discipline; and
- refusal to turn a recovery task into unsupervised practical work.

Use **secure**, **developing**, **unsupported** and `stop-required` for each criterion independently. Strong terminology recall cannot cancel a blocking path, evidence, authority or safety error.

## Safety boundary

This recovery block authorises no switching, isolation, opening, proving, tracing, measurement, testing, fault creation, disconnection, reconnection, alteration, repair, energisation, commissioning, certification or verification. Stop at 30 minutes, when concentration falls, when guessing begins, when a practical action would be required, or when an immediate hazard is described.
