## Core concepts

- **Closed-note retrieval:** recall before rereading.
- **Diagram reconstruction:** rebuild a model from memory and then compare it with the source module.
- **Misconception:** a stable but incorrect mental model.
- **Error log:** a record of the error, cause, correction and future cue.
- **Catch-up triage:** selecting one small prerequisite gap rather than attempting all unfinished work.
- **Readiness statement:** a supported decision to progress, remediate or seek help.

## R-E-D-R-A-W workflow

1. **Retrieve** without notes.
2. **Examine** differences against the completed modules.
3. **Diagnose** the error type.
4. **Repair** one high-value misconception.
5. **Attempt again** using a varied prompt.
6. **Withdraw on time** at the session limit or stop condition.

## Practical application

The learner reconstructs the Week 3 terminology map and both current-path models, repairs two high-priority misconceptions, performs at most one bounded catch-up task and records a readiness decision for Day 20.

## Assessment relevance

Observable evidence includes accurate path reconstruction, high-confidence-error correction, limited catch-up scope, time discipline, and refusal to turn a recovery task into unsupervised practical work.
