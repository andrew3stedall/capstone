# 12-Week Day 64 - Continuity Evidence and Common Interpretation Errors

## Purpose

Provide a document-only method for deciding what continuity evidence can and cannot support by controlling nine boundaries, path identity, six evidence states, confidence, dependencies, competing interpretations, ownership and change propagation.

## Learning module

- [Day 64 — Continuity Evidence and Common Interpretation Errors](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-64-continuity-evidence-and-common-interpretation-errors.md)

## Core method

Use **C-O-N-T-I-N-U-E**: Clarify the evidence question; Outline every boundary; Name the intended complete path; Test identity, provenance and applicability; Identify alternate explanations and paths; Note claim states and dependencies; Understand the limited meaning; Express ownership, triggers and a bounded conclusion.

## Core concepts

- **Nine boundaries:** installation, circuit, conductor, endpoint, state, time, evidence, authority and decision.
- **Continuity:** evidence that a conductive path exists between defined points under stated conditions.
- **Path identity:** evidence that the observed route is the intended complete path rather than a partial, wrong or parallel path.
- **Connection integrity:** the condition and reliability of joins, terminations and interfaces; continuity alone does not establish every aspect.
- **Evidence states:** stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction and evidence gap.
- **Confidence calibration:** low, medium or high confidence recorded separately from correctness and evidence quality.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest unsupported arrow in a claim chain; dependent claims beyond it remain unsupported.
- **Competing interpretations:** plausible explanations retained without promoting either to fact.
- **Evidence owner:** the authorised source, custodian or qualified person responsible for resolving a blocker.
- **Recheck trigger:** a new record or material change that reopens affected reasoning.
- **Educational readiness states:** secure, developing, unsupported and `stop-required`; not official grades or technical decisions.

## Practical applications

For a fictional evidence pack:

- define the bounded question and all nine boundaries;
- map the intended complete path separately from partial, wrong and parallel paths;
- classify claims and record confidence independently;
- check identity, provenance, date, version, state and applicability;
- retain competing interpretations;
- stop at the first unsupported transition;
- assign evidence owners and recheck triggers;
- reopen dependencies after two sequential material changes; and
- communicate a bounded conclusion without pass/fail, compliance, acceptance or certification claims.

## Assessment relevance

Evaluate boundary control, path identity, evidence classification, confidence calibration, provenance, dependency reasoning, interpretation limits, ownership, two-change transfer and safety communication independently. There is no aggregate score. An unsupported blocking criterion or any `stop-required` state cannot be offset by stronger work elsewhere.

## Blocking conditions

Progress is blocked by invented or altered evidence, concealed contradictions, unidentified endpoints treated as traceable, credible alternate paths ignored, continued reasoning beyond the first unsupported transition, unresolved blockers without owners or triggers, ignored material changes, practical testing directions, or unsupported connection-integrity, safe-condition, compliance, acceptance, certification or technical-approval claims.
