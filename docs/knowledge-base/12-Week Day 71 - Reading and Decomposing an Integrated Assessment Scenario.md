# 12-Week Day 71 - Reading and Decomposing an Integrated Assessment Scenario

## Purpose

Convert a dense integrated scenario into a controlled, auditable brief before technical solving begins. The learner separates tasks, deliverables, boundaries, evidence states, contradictions, workstreams, dependencies and blocked claim chains.

## Learning module

- [Day 71 — Reading and Decomposing an Integrated Assessment Scenario](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-71-reading-and-decomposing-an-integrated-assessment-scenario.md)

## Core method

Use **D-E-C-O-M-P-O-S-E**: Define nine boundaries; Extract tasks and deliverables; Classify six evidence states; Open contradiction, assumption and gap registers; Map workstreams; Place claim chains in dependency order; Outline authorised sources and evidence owners; Sequence by critical path; End with bounded outputs and recheck triggers.

## Core concepts

- **Nine boundaries:** installation, equipment, circuit, source, operating state, time, evidence, authority and requested decision.
- **Six evidence states:** stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction and evidence gap.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest unsupported step in a claim chain; dependent reasoning remains unsupported until repaired.
- **Non-compensatory blocker:** a material weakness that cannot be averaged away by stronger work elsewhere.
- **Evidence owner:** the authorised source, custodian or qualified person responsible for resolving a gap or contradiction.
- **Recheck trigger:** evidence or a material change that reopens affected reasoning.
- **Change propagation:** reopening all dependent claims after a material change.
- **Confidence calibration:** confidence remains separate from correctness and evidence quality.
- **Educational readiness states:** `secure`, `developing`, `unsupported` and `stop-required`; these are not official grades or competency decisions.

## Practical applications

Prepare a fictional scenario decomposition pack that:

- extracts task verbs and distinct deliverables;
- defines all nine boundaries;
- preserves literal evidence before interpretation;
- classifies every item into one of six evidence states;
- separates design, inspection, verification, fault and documentation workstreams;
- traces every requested claim to its first unsupported transition;
- assigns owners and recheck triggers;
- sequences work by critical path;
- keeps confidence, correctness and evidence quality separate; and
- reopens affected dependencies after two sequential material changes.

## Assessment relevance

Assess boundary control, task/deliverable control, evidence control, dependency control, workstream integration, ownership/change control and calibration/conclusion independently. There is no aggregate score. An `unsupported` non-compensatory blocker or any `stop-required` state blocks progression regardless of stronger performance elsewhere.

## Blocking conditions

Progress is blocked by invented or altered evidence, hidden contradictions, unsupported identity or source transfer, reasoning beyond the first unsupported transition, confidence treated as proof, material changes ignored, practical directions or unsupported competency, compliance, verification, acceptance, certification, root-cause or technical-approval claims.
