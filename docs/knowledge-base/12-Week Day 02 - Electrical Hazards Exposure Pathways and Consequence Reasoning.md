# 12-Week Day 02 - Electrical Hazards Exposure Pathways and Consequence Reasoning

## Purpose

This note links the Day 2 hazard-chain model to later Capstone topics. It is an original navigation and retrieval aid, not a substitute for authorised safety sources or workplace procedures.

## Core model

Use the sequence:

**hazard → exposure pathway → exposure event → consequence → matched control → evidence check**

The module's **H-A-Z-A-R-D** workflow is:

- **H:** Highlight energy and other harm sources.
- **A:** Analyse who or what could be exposed.
- **Z:** Zoom in on the pathway.
- **A:** Ask what evidence is missing.
- **R:** Relate each control to a specific link.
- **D:** Decide whether reasoning may continue or must stop and escalate.

## Practical application

Use written or trainer-approved scenarios to:

1. separate observed facts from assumptions;
2. identify more than one plausible pathway where the evidence supports it;
3. match each proposed control to the part of the chain it changes;
4. record the evidence needed before relying on a critical control;
5. state a clear stop and escalation point.

Do not use this note as authority to approach, operate, isolate, test, reset, repair or energise equipment.

## Assessment relevance

The model supports:

- accurate safety terminology;
- scenario decomposition;
- evidence-quality reasoning;
- control selection explanations;
- recognition of unsafe assumptions;
- defensible stop decisions.

A response remains incomplete when it lists a hazard and control but does not explain the exposure pathway or evidence boundary.
