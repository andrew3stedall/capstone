# 12-Week Day 02 - Electrical Hazards Exposure Pathways and Consequence Reasoning

## Purpose

This note connects the Day 2 hazard-chain model to later Capstone topics. It is an original navigation and retrieval aid, not a substitute for authorised safety sources, emergency training or workplace procedures.

## Core model

Use the sequence:

**hazard → exposed target → exposure pathway → exposure event → immediate and secondary consequences → matched control → evidence check → bounded decision**

The module's **H-A-Z-A-R-D** workflow is:

- **H:** Highlight harm sources.
- **A:** Analyse exposed targets.
- **Z:** Zoom in on each pathway.
- **A:** Ask what evidence is missing.
- **R:** Relate each control to an exact link and evidence requirement.
- **D:** Decide the supported boundary or stop point.

## Evidence discipline

Keep these statements separate:

- **fact:** directly supplied by the scenario or an authorised record;
- **report:** attributed information that has not yet been independently established;
- **inference:** a plausible explanation clearly labelled as unverified;
- **evidence gap:** a missing fact, record, source or authorised confirmation;
- **stop-required uncertainty:** missing evidence affecting energy state, source, exposure, authority or a critical control.

A control must not move directly from “named” to “effective.” Check whether it is present, applicable to the stated pathway and supported by authorised evidence.

## Practical application

Use written or trainer-approved scenarios to:

1. separate facts, reports and inferences;
2. identify the exposed person, property or system;
3. trace more than one plausible pathway where evidence supports it;
4. distinguish immediate from secondary consequences;
5. match each proposed control to the exact link it changes;
6. record the evidence and owner needed before relying on a critical control;
7. state a clear stop and escalation point; and
8. repeat the reasoning in a changed context rather than copying the first answer.

Do not use this note as authority to approach, operate, isolate, test, reset, repair, rescue or energise equipment.

## Assessment relevance

The model supports observable evidence of:

- accurate category use;
- complete hazard-chain construction;
- separation of evidence from assumption;
- control-mechanism and evidence-owner reasoning;
- immediate and secondary consequence discrimination;
- recognition of stop-required uncertainty;
- transfer to a changed scenario; and
- confidence calibration, especially for high-confidence errors.

A response remains incomplete when it lists a hazard and control but omits the target, pathway, exposure event, evidence boundary or control-effectiveness basis.
