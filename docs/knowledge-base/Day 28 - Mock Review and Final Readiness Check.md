## Core concept

Day 28 converts the Day 27 mock into evidence-based remediation and readiness decisions. It preserves the original attempt, reviews performance across result, process, safety, source discipline, communication and time, and avoids reducing readiness to one total score.

The **R-E-V-I-E-W** workflow is:

1. **Retain the original**.
2. **Evaluate by dimension**.
3. **Verify exact claims**.
4. **Identify the error mechanism**.
5. **Establish the smallest correction**.
6. **Write the readiness decision**.

## Practical application

The learner classifies each substantial error by mechanism and consequence, gives priority to high-confidence critical errors, verifies exact claims against current authorised sources, completes a varied re-attempt and records each capability as ready, conditionally ready or not yet ready.

The immediate remediation queue is limited to three repeated patterns. This prevents the final review becoming another broad rereading exercise.

## Assessment relevance

A correct answer can still be produced by unsafe, accidental or untraceable reasoning. Day 28 therefore separates technical result from process, safety, source discipline, communication, confidence and time. Readiness must be supported by observable mock and re-attempt evidence rather than effort or an average score.
