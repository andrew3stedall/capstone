# 12-Week Day 56 - Week 8 Cumulative Design and Inspection Checkpoint

## Purpose

Demonstrate cumulative Week 8 reasoning by preserving location, source, operating-state, evidence, authority and decision boundaries through an integrated fictional dossier.

## Learning module

- [Day 56 — Week 8 Cumulative Design and Inspection Checkpoint](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-56-week-8-cumulative-design-and-inspection-checkpoint.md)

## Core method

Use **C-H-E-C-K-S**:

1. construct all material boundaries;
2. hold evidence apart by state and provenance;
3. engage only the workflow suited to each question;
4. connect claim and dependency chains;
5. keep competing interpretations, owners and recheck triggers visible; and
6. stress-test after two sequential changes, state bounded outcomes and set remediation.

## Core concepts

- **Evidence state:** stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction or evidence gap.
- **Provenance:** source, issuer, date or revision and scenario connection.
- **Dependency chain:** ordered evidence and reasoning supporting a later conclusion.
- **First unsupported transition:** earliest chain step without adequate evidence or applicability.
- **Competing interpretations:** plausible alternatives retained pending resolution.
- **Evidence owner:** authorised source, person or qualified reviewer responsible for resolving a gap.
- **Recheck trigger:** evidence or change requiring dependent reasoning to reopen.
- **Blocking condition:** an error that prevents progression regardless of stronger work elsewhere.

Confidence is recorded separately as guessing, unsure, reasonably confident or certain.

## Practical applications

- build a boundary register and evidence ledger;
- separate location, source-state, design-intent and inspection-observation maps;
- classify evidence and record provenance;
- trace claims to the first unsupported transition;
- retain competing interpretations;
- assign evidence owners and recheck triggers;
- reopen dependencies after two material changes; and
- communicate supported, unresolved and prohibited claims separately.

## Assessment relevance

Evaluate boundary control, evidence discipline, workflow selection, dependency control, resolution planning, two-change transfer and safety communication independently.

Use **secure**, **developing**, **unsupported** and `stop-required` only as educational planning states. They are not official grades, competency decisions, defect classifications, compliance decisions or technical approvals.

## Blocking conditions

Progression is blocked by an omitted material boundary, hidden contradiction, assumption presented as fact, merged design and inspection evidence, reasoning beyond the first unsupported transition, unresolved blocker without ownership, cosmetic-only transfer, invented official content or implied practical or compliance authority.
