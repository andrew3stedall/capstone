# 12-Week Day 56 - Week 8 Cumulative Design and Inspection Checkpoint

## Purpose

Demonstrate cumulative Week 8 reasoning by preserving location, source, evidence and decision boundaries through an integrated fictional dossier.

## Core workflow

Use **C-H-E-C-K-S**:

- **C** — Construct boundaries.
- **H** — Hold evidence apart.
- **E** — Engage the right workflow.
- **C** — Connect dependencies.
- **K** — Keep claim grades visible.
- **S** — Stress-test and set remediation.

## Key distinctions

- A proposed design is not evidence of installed condition.
- A visual observation is not proof of hidden construction.
- A supported conclusion remains bounded by its evidence and operating state.
- A material change reopens dependent conclusions.
- A prohibited claim exceeds evidence or learner authority.

## Practical application

Complete a cumulative boundary map, evidence ledger, workflow selection, source-state map, dependency diagram, graded conclusions, staged-change response and targeted remediation statement.

## Assessment relevance

The learner should demonstrate integrated reasoning, workflow selection, evidence discipline, dependency control, change propagation and safety communication before progressing to verification principles.

## Safety boundary

This note authorises no site classification, design approval, access, switching, isolation, testing, installation, alteration, energisation, commissioning, certification or verification.
