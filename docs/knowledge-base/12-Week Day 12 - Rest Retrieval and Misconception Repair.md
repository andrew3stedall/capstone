# 12-Week Day 12 - Rest Retrieval and Misconception Repair

## Purpose

This note connects the Week 2 protection modules to a deliberate recovery block. Day 12 uses closed-note retrieval, evidence labelling, confidence calibration, misconception diagnosis, changed-context re-attempts, bounded catch-up and explicit stop conditions before Day 13 application work.

## Core concepts

- **Retrieval:** producing knowledge or a reasoning process without looking at the source.
- **Misconception:** a stable but incorrect mental model that transfers into multiple scenarios.
- **Confidence calibration:** comparing certainty with accuracy and supporting evidence.
- **Evidence labels:** stated fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction and evidence gap.
- **Error category:** terminology, current path, protection role, evidence, confidence or safety boundary.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest step where reasoning leaves supported evidence.
- **Varied re-attempt:** testing the repair after at least two material scenario conditions change.
- **Evidence owner:** the person, source or authorised process responsible for resolving a gap.
- **Recheck trigger:** the event that requires the conclusion to be reviewed.
- **Readiness decision:** a bounded learning decision, not a competency or technical-approval claim.

## R-E-P-A-I-R workflow

1. **Retrieve** without notes.
2. **Examine** evidence labels and confidence.
3. **Pinpoint** the error category and first unsupported transition.
4. **Amend** the mental model with a bounded correction.
5. **Interleave** a scenario with at least two material changes.
6. **Record** criterion state, evidence owner, recheck trigger and readiness.

## Misconception priorities

Repair no more than three items in one recovery block, in this order:

1. safety-boundary errors;
2. high-confidence incorrect answers;
3. prerequisite errors that would block Day 13; and
4. lower-confidence terminology errors.

The block repairs the smallest unsupported reasoning link rather than reteaching all protection content.

## Assessment relevance

Observable evidence includes accurate closed-note distinctions, evidence labelling, identification of the first unsupported transition, corrected mental models, transfer under two changed conditions, calibrated confidence, separation of protection roles and claims, named evidence owners, recheck triggers and compliance with time, fatigue and authority boundaries.

Use these educational planning states:

- **secure:** accurate, evidenced, transferable and within authority;
- **developing:** substantially correct with a named support need;
- **unsupported:** conclusion exceeds evidence or transfer is absent;
- **`stop-required`:** safety, contradiction, authority or material evidence gap blocks progression.

A secure result in one criterion cannot cancel a `stop-required` state in another. These states are not official grades or competency decisions.

## Practical application

The learner completes a maximum 30-minute protocol: closed-note retrieval, misconception sorting, up to three repair records, one changed-context scenario, criterion-level review and a bounded readiness decision. Missed work is triaged rather than compressed into an extended session.
