# 12-Week Day 12 - Rest Retrieval and Misconception Repair

## Purpose

This note connects the Week 2 protection modules to a deliberate recovery block. Day 12 uses closed-note retrieval, confidence calibration, misconception classification, varied re-attempts, bounded catch-up and explicit stop conditions before Day 13 application work.

## Core concepts

- **Retrieval:** producing knowledge or a reasoning process without looking at the source.
- **Misconception:** a stable but incorrect mental model that may transfer into several scenarios.
- **Confidence calibration:** comparing certainty with demonstrated accuracy and evidence.
- **Error category:** the specific reasoning link that failed: terminology, current path, protection role, evidence, confidence or safety boundary.
- **Varied re-attempt:** testing the repaired reasoning in a changed scenario rather than repeating the original answer.
- **Catch-up triage:** selecting missed work by safety significance and prerequisite value.
- **Stop condition:** a predefined signal that further study or action is becoming unreliable or outside authority.
- **Readiness decision:** a bounded choice to progress, progress with support or complete targeted remediation first.

## R-E-P-A-I-R workflow

1. **Retrieve** without notes.
2. **Examine** accuracy and confidence.
3. **Pinpoint** the error category and broken reasoning link.
4. **Amend** the mental model with a bounded correction.
5. **Interleave** a varied scenario.
6. **Record** readiness, support needs and stop conditions.

## Misconception priorities

Repair no more than three items in one recovery block, in this order:

1. safety-boundary errors;
2. high-confidence incorrect answers;
3. prerequisite errors that would block Day 13; and
4. lower-confidence terminology errors.

The block does not attempt to reteach all protection content. It repairs the smallest reasoning link that can be tested through transfer.

## Practical application

The learner completes a maximum 30-minute protocol: closed-note retrieval, misconception sorting, three targeted repair records, one varied fictional scenario, and an evidence-based readiness decision. Missed work is triaged rather than compressed into an extended session.

## Assessment relevance

Observable evidence includes accurate closed-note distinctions, correct error classification, revised mental models, transfer to a changed scenario, calibrated confidence, separation of protection functions, explicit missing-evidence statements and compliance with time, fatigue and authority boundaries.
