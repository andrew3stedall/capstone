# 12-Week Day 23 - Design Current Protective-Device Rating and Conductor Capacity

## Purpose

Supports evidence-bounded coordination among design current, protective-device rating and conductor capacity without treating one comparison as a complete or approved cable selection.

## Core concepts

- **Design current:** current used as a defined design input for a bounded operating case.
- **Nominal device rating:** stated device identifier, not proof of protective suitability or operation.
- **Base capacity:** authorised capacity before applicable adjustments.
- **Corrected capacity:** capacity after every verified applicable influence is applied once.
- **Coordination relationship:** comparison among load, device and conductor evidence while other design checks remain open.
- **First unsupported transition:** earliest point where the evidence no longer supports the next claim.
- **Evidence owner:** source or responsible party that can resolve a material gap or contradiction.
- **Recheck trigger:** changed or newly verified information requiring affected reasoning to be repeated.

## R-A-T-I-N-G workflow

Record the design case; assign the protective function; trace conductor conditions; identify authorised values; normalise units and apply factors once; gate the conclusion.

The gate requires evidence labels, competing interpretations where sources conflict, the first unsupported transition, an evidence owner and a recheck trigger.

## Evidence and learning states

Inputs and conclusions are labelled as stated facts, derived facts, supported inferences, assumptions, contradictions or evidence gaps.

Each assessment criterion is recorded separately as secure, developing, unsupported or `stop-required`. These are educational planning states, not official grades or competency decisions. Strong performance in one criterion cannot cancel a safety, authority, contradiction or evidence failure elsewhere.

## Practical application

The learner compares fictional design candidates, audits correction factors, preserves conflicting terminal or equipment records, and rebuilds the reasoning after at least two material conditions change. The learner explains why one current-rating relationship does not complete cable selection.

## Assessment relevance

Observable evidence includes:

- value provenance and units;
- device identity and protective-role separation;
- installation-condition tracing;
- factor applicability and single application;
- contradiction and competing-interpretation control;
- identification of the first unsupported transition;
- bounded conclusions, evidence owners and reopening triggers; and
- changed-context transfer without practical-authority overreach.
