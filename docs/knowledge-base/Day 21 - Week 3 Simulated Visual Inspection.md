## Core concept

A simulated visual inspection is a bounded evidence exercise. It begins by defining scope and prohibited actions, then scans the installation by functional zone. Observations, missing evidence, potential concerns and verified conclusions must remain separate so that a photograph or neat appearance is not mistaken for proof of compliance.

## Evidence workflow

Use **I-N-S-P-E-C-T**:

1. **Identify scope** — define boundaries, evidence, operating state and prohibited actions.
2. **Note sources and functions** — map energy paths and the purpose of each area.
3. **Scan by zone** — review supply, switchboard, wiring, equipment, special-location and documentation zones.
4. **Pinpoint observations** — record precise visible conditions without inventing causes.
5. **Evaluate evidence** — identify what is shown, missing and subject to authorised verification.
6. **Classify response provisionally** — choose escalation, blocking gap, priority concern or routine clarification without official defect codes.
7. **Transfer the record** — hand over traceable findings, limitations and exact verification needs.

## Practical application

Review a fictional image and document pack for a community workshop and amenities block. Produce a scope statement, zone scan, three objective finding records and a bounded handover. The output is not a repair list, test sequence or compliance certificate.

## Assessment relevance

The exercise integrates Week 3 content: wiring systems, circuit hierarchy, wet and special locations, fixed appliances, motors and multiple supplies. It tests whether the learner can inspect systematically, resist over-interpretation and escalate incomplete or unsafe evidence.

## Safety boundary

This note does not authorise approaching, opening, touching, operating, isolating, locking, testing, proving de-energised, repairing or altering equipment. Immediate-danger indicators, unclear sources, conflicting records or insufficient geometry require escalation.
