# Six-Week Day 15 - Load Identification and Maximum-Demand Workflow

## Purpose

Build a traceable load model before using an authorised maximum-demand method. Separate connected load, assessed contribution and design margin, and keep phase, operating-case, evidence and control dependencies visible.

## Core workflow

**L-O-A-D-S:** List the complete scope → Obtain the governing method → Align units, evidence and assumptions → Determine group and phase contributions → Stress-test and state the bounded result.

## Evidence and claim control

### Evidence grades

- **E1 supplied:** present in a source but not independently corroborated.
- **E2 corroborated:** supported by a second consistent source or cross-check.
- **E3 derived:** calculated transparently from supported inputs.
- **E4 assumed:** introduced for training or because evidence is missing.
- **E5 missing or conflicting:** unavailable, ambiguous, stale or inconsistent.

### Claim grades

- **C1 descriptive:** records what a source says.
- **C2 provisional model:** suitable for learning or further investigation.
- **C3 supported paper conclusion:** traceable to authorised sources and supported inputs, subject to qualified review.
- **C4 authorised verification:** practical conclusion through an authorised process; this module cannot award C4.

An E4 assumption may support a C2 training model but not a C3 practical design conclusion.

## Demand-dependency ledger

Record the load and scope, rating basis, evidence grade, demand category, authorised source, control or coincidence dependency, phase, source, assessed contribution, unresolved information, reopening trigger and claim grade.

Reopen the affected assessment when a load, rating basis, control, phase allocation, source arrangement, authorised method or credible operating case changes, or when evidence becomes missing or conflicting.

## Practical application

Create a fictional load register, grade each input, compare three operating cases and revise the model when a control, phase allocation or rating basis changes. Do not invent missing ratings, phase data, controls or demand allowances without labelling them as assumptions.

## Assessment relevance

The learner must classify loads, select the authorised source family, preserve units and assumptions, test phase and operating cases, control dependencies and state an evidence-bounded design input. Critical errors include treating connected load as maximum demand, hiding an assumption, mixing units without a basis, relying on an undocumented control or presenting a provisional model as approved design evidence.
