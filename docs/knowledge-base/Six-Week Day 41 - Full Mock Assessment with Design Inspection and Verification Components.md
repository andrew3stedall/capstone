# Six-Week Day 41 - Full Mock Assessment with Design Inspection and Verification Components

## Purpose

Original cumulative paper mock using **C-A-P-S-T-O-N-E**: confirm scope; audit sources; plan dependencies; solve design tasks; trace inspection evidence; organise verification evidence; note changes and uncertainties; end with bounded conclusions.

## Evidence and claim model

- Evidence grades: **stated**, **indicated**, **corroborated**, **transferred**, **unresolved**.
- Claim grades: **assumption**, **provisional educational conclusion**, **supported educational conclusion**, **authorised technical determination**.
- Integrated evidence register: item → source/revision → provenance → evidence grade → dependent decisions → uncertainty → claim grade → change notice → reopening action → bounded conclusion → escalation.
- Evidence with uncertain identity, provenance, conditions or authority is quarantined rather than used as proof.

## Practical application

Complete a timed fictional dossier that integrates source and authority control, design dependencies, inspection evidence, verification purpose, supplied-result interpretation, competing fault hypotheses and change propagation. Finish with a bounded conclusion and escalation list rather than a compliance or practical-readiness declaration.

## Assessment relevance

Observable evidence includes:

- independent scope and source audit;
- traceable design sequencing without invented values;
- observation/inference separation;
- result-provenance and evidence-quarantine decisions;
- competing diagnostic hypotheses and distinguishing evidence;
- selective reopening after changed conditions; and
- claim grades aligned with evidence and authority.

The six-category 12-point rubric and critical-error gates are original educational tools. They are not an official RTO pass mark, qualification result or technical approval.

## Reopening triggers

Reopen affected rows when scope, source revision, document identity, equipment identity, route, environmental condition, supply state, automatic restoration, stored energy, evidence provenance, instrument status, supplied result, acceptance criterion, responsible role or authority changes.
