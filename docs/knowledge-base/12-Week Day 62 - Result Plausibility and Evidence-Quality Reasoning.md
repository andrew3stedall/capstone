# 12-Week Day 62 - Result Plausibility and Evidence-Quality Reasoning

## Purpose

Provide a paper-only framework for reviewing fictional result records through explicit boundaries, evidence classification, confidence calibration and unsupported-transition control without turning plausibility into compliance or providing test instructions.

## Learning module

- [Day 62 — Result Plausibility and Evidence-Quality Reasoning](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-62-result-plausibility-and-evidence-quality-reasoning.md)

## Core method

Use **R-E-S-U-L-T-S**: Read literally; Establish boundaries; Sort evidence states; Use provenance and relationships; Locate the first unsupported transition; Track ownership and triggers; State criterion-level readiness.

## Core concepts

- **Literal result:** the record reproduced without correction or added meaning.
- **Plausibility:** compatibility with stated context and related evidence; not compliance.
- **Evidence states:** stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction and evidence gap.
- **Confidence calibration:** low, medium or high confidence recorded separately from correctness and evidence quality.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest unsupported arrow in a result-to-conclusion claim chain.
- **Evidence owner:** the authorised source or qualified person responsible for resolving a blocker.
- **Recheck trigger:** new evidence or a material change requiring affected reasoning to reopen.
- **Educational readiness states:** secure, developing, unsupported and `stop-required`; not official grades or technical decisions.

## Practical applications

For each fictional result:

- preserve the literal record;
- define installation, circuit, source, state, time, evidence, authority and decision boundaries;
- classify material claims and record confidence separately;
- check provenance, completeness, currency, consistency and logical relationships;
- stop at the first unsupported transition;
- assign evidence owners and recheck triggers;
- reopen affected dependencies after two sequential material changes; and
- communicate criterion-level readiness without pass/fail, compliance or certification claims.

## Assessment relevance

Evaluate literal separation, boundary control, evidence classification, plausibility review, unsupported-transition control, ownership and transfer, and bounded communication independently. An unsupported blocking criterion or any `stop-required` state cannot be offset by stronger work elsewhere.

## Blocking conditions

Progress is blocked by altered results, concealed contradictions, missing identity or operating-state evidence treated as complete, invented values or procedures, practical retesting directions, continued reasoning beyond the first unsupported transition, unresolved blockers without owners or triggers, or unsupported competency, compliance, verification, certification or technical-approval claims.
