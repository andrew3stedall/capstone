# 12-Week Day 30 - Voltage-Drop Interpretation and Design Iteration

## Purpose

Builds disciplined interpretation and controlled design-iteration skills after a transparent voltage-drop calculation has been completed. The note emphasises complete boundaries, evidence provenance, first-unsupported-transition control and reopening every dependent design gate.

## Core concepts

- **Interpretation:** explaining a result within its stated boundary, method and evidence.
- **Acceptance criterion:** an authorised requirement used to judge a result.
- **Design iteration:** a controlled change followed by recalculation and reopened checks.
- **Sensitivity:** how strongly a result responds to an input change.
- **Evidence provenance:** traceable source identity, revision, location and applicability for a fact, method or criterion.
- **Reopening trigger:** a changed or corrected fact that weakens an earlier conclusion.
- **Competing interpretation:** a plausible alternative retained until evidence distinguishes between alternatives.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest step where evidence no longer supports progression to the next claim.
- **Evidence owner:** the person or role responsible for resolving a gap or contradiction.

## Evidence states

Use these labels for material statements:

- stated fact;
- derived fact;
- supported inference;
- assumption;
- contradiction; and
- evidence gap.

A correct numerical result does not repair an unsupported method, incomplete boundary, inapplicable criterion or unresolved contradiction.

## I-T-E-R-A-T-E workflow

Identify the complete boundary; trace the criterion and method; evaluate the evidence chain; revise one controlled variable; audit every reopened gate; test alternatives consistently; explain the bounded recommendation.

The workflow stops downstream claims at the first unsupported transition and records evidence owners and recheck triggers for unresolved items.

## Practical application

Learners create an interpretation ledger, predict sensitivity, recalculate one changed input, compare three candidates with conflicting records and transfer the workflow after at least two material conditions change.

Repeated calculator entry is not an independent check. Suitable written alternatives include dimensional checking, reverse calculation, estimation and dependency review.

## Assessment relevance

Observable evidence is recorded separately as **secure**, **developing**, **unsupported** or `stop-required` for:

- boundary and operating-case definition;
- method and criterion provenance;
- evidence classification and contradiction handling;
- like-for-like comparison;
- unsupported-transition control;
- reopened design gates;
- trade-off and competing-interpretation reasoning;
- independent checking;
- two-condition transfer; and
- claim restraint.

These states are not combined into an aggregate score and are not official grades or competency decisions.

## Safety boundary

Written training only. No official equation, conductor dataset, limit, acceptance criterion, field measurement or compliance conclusion is supplied. Exact methods and requirements remain `reference_check_required`; no practical electrical work is authorised.

A material evidence, safety or authority failure is blocking and cannot be offset by stronger performance elsewhere.
