# 12-Week Day 41 - Switchboard Inspection Decision Workshop

## Purpose

Builds evidence-controlled, paper-based switchboard inspection reasoning without converting photographs or incomplete records into unsupported defect or compliance conclusions.

## Core method

Use **I-N-S-P-E-C-T**:

1. identify inspection, evidence, authority and decision boundaries;
2. note sourced observations;
3. separate evidence states and preserve contradictions;
4. pinpoint the first unsupported transition and consequence pathway;
5. establish authorised sources, evidence owners and recheck triggers;
6. communicate bounded defect candidates and escalation; and
7. transfer under two material changes, reopening affected claims.

## Core concepts

- **Observation:** directly visible or explicitly stated information tied to a source.
- **Derived fact:** transparent result from supported facts.
- **Supported inference:** bounded interpretation with an adequate evidence chain.
- **Assumption:** unverified proposition not to be disguised as fact.
- **Contradiction:** evidence items that cannot both describe the same condition.
- **Evidence gap:** missing support required for a stronger claim.
- **Defect candidate:** feature requiring authorised checking, not a final classification.
- **First unsupported transition:** earliest unsupported step in the claim ladder.
- **Evidence owner:** person or authorised source expected to resolve a gap.
- **Recheck trigger:** new evidence or change requiring prior reasoning to reopen.
- **Change propagation:** review of every dependent conclusion affected by change.

## Practical applications

- analyse fictional switchboard photographs and records;
- separate visible evidence from hidden-condition claims;
- preserve competing interpretations;
- build consequence pathways;
- write bounded defect-candidate statements;
- assign evidence owners and recheck triggers;
- choose proportionate escalation; and
- retest reasoning after two material scenario changes.

## Assessment relevance

Observable evidence includes boundary control, accurate evidence classification, provenance checking, first-unsupported-transition control, consequence reasoning, bounded communication, change propagation and safe escalation.

Use **secure**, **developing**, **unsupported** and `stop-required` as independent educational planning states. They are not official assessment grades, competency outcomes, defect classifications or technical approvals.

## Blocking conditions

Secure readiness is blocked by:

- treating labels, photographs, blank ways or schedules as proof of hidden connection, capacity or suitability;
- concealing contradictions;
- skipping provenance or applicability;
- promoting a defect candidate into a final conclusion;
- failing to name evidence owners or recheck triggers;
- failing to reopen dependencies;
- testing transfer with fewer than two material changes; or
- proposing unauthorised practical activity.
