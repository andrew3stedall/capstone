## Core method

Use **I-N-S-P-E-C-T**: identify the boundary, note observations, separate inference, prioritise consequence, establish missing evidence, communicate a bounded finding and trigger escalation or re-review.

## Key distinctions

- Observation is directly supported.
- Inference is an interpretation requiring evidence.
- A defect candidate requires further checking.
- A verified conclusion requires applicable authorised evidence and qualified confirmation.

## Practical application

Review a fictional switchboard dossier, produce observations and labelled inferences, rank concerns, request missing evidence and write bounded findings without claiming compliance from appearance.

## Assessment relevance

Strong performance demonstrates source-boundary control, observation quality, consequence ranking, evidence discipline, change propagation and safe escalation.
