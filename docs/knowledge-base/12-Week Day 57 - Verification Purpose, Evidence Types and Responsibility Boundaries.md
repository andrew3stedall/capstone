# 12-Week Day 57 - Verification Purpose, Evidence Types and Responsibility Boundaries

## Purpose

Frame verification as a coordinated evidence and responsibility process rather than a single test or completed form.

## Core workflow

Use **V-E-R-I-F-Y**:

- **V** — Verify the scope.
- **E** — Establish responsibility.
- **R** — Review prerequisites.
- **I** — Inventory evidence streams.
- **F** — Follow dependencies.
- **Y** — Yield a bounded decision.

## Key distinctions

- Design, visual-inspection, test and documentary evidence serve different purposes.
- Evidence collection, interpretation and formal acceptance are separate acts.
- A result without scope, provenance or prerequisites may be valid but insufficient.
- Responsibility depends on role, competence, authority and supervision.
- Missing earlier evidence can invalidate a later conclusion.

## Practical application

Build a verification-planning brief containing scope, role map, prerequisite list, evidence inventory, dependency map, evidence gaps, escalation triggers and a bounded status statement.

## Assessment relevance

The learner should explain verification purpose, evidence limitations, dependency control and responsibility boundaries without inventing procedures, values or authority.

## Safety boundary

This note authorises no access, switching, isolation, proving de-energised, testing, instrument use, alteration, energisation, commissioning, certification or verification.
