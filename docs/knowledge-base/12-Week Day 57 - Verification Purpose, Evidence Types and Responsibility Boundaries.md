# 12-Week Day 57 - Verification Purpose, Evidence Types and Responsibility Boundaries

## Purpose

Frame verification as an evidence-controlled process in which scope, evidence purpose, dependencies, responsibility and decision authority remain explicit.

## Learning module

- [Day 57 — Verification Purpose, Evidence Types and Responsibility Boundaries](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-57-verification-purpose-evidence-types-and-responsibility-boundaries.md)

## Core method

Use **V-E-R-I-F-Y**:

1. verify work, source, operating-state, evidence, role and decision boundaries;
2. establish evidence identity, provenance and state;
3. review prerequisites and responsibility boundaries;
4. inventory purpose-specific evidence streams;
5. follow dependency chains to the first unsupported transition; and
6. yield bounded status, owners, recheck triggers and prohibited claims.

## Core concepts

- **Evidence streams:** design, visual-inspection, test and documentary evidence.
- **Evidence states:** stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction and evidence gap.
- **Provenance:** source, issuer, date or revision, scope and scenario connection.
- **First unsupported transition:** earliest dependency step without adequate evidence or applicability.
- **Responsibility boundary:** limit of assigned role, competence, authority and supervision.
- **Evidence owner:** authorised source or qualified person responsible for resolving a gap.
- **Recheck trigger:** evidence or changed condition requiring dependent reasoning to reopen.

Confidence is recorded separately as guessing, unsure, reasonably confident or certain.

## Key distinctions

- Evidence collection, qualified interpretation, formal acceptance and certification are separate acts.
- Design intent is not proof of installed condition.
- A plausible result may still lack scope, method, provenance or prerequisites.
- A completed form cannot replace missing supporting evidence.
- A changed source, operating state or scope boundary reopens dependent reasoning.
- Educational planning states do not establish compliance, competency or technical acceptance.

## Practical applications

- build a scope and boundary register;
- classify evidence and record provenance;
- separate evidence streams by purpose and limitation;
- map collection, interpretation, acceptance and certification responsibilities;
- trace claims to their first unsupported transition;
- retain contradictions and competing interpretations;
- assign evidence owners and recheck triggers; and
- reopen chains after two sequential material changes.

## Assessment relevance

Evaluate boundary control, evidence discipline, responsibility control, dependency control, resolution planning, two-change transfer and safety communication independently.

Use **secure**, **developing**, **unsupported** and `stop-required` only as educational planning states. They are not official grades, competency decisions, defect classifications, compliance findings or technical approvals.

## Blocking conditions

Progression is blocked by an omitted material boundary, merged evidence purposes, invented authority, hidden contradiction, reasoning beyond the first unsupported transition, unresolved blocker without ownership, cosmetic-only change handling, invented official content or implied practical or compliance authority.
