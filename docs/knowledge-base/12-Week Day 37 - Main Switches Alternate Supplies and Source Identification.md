# 12-Week Day 37 - Main Switches Alternate Supplies and Source Identification

## Purpose

Builds complete source inventories, operating-state matrices and evidence-controlled switching-function maps before any source-identification, main-switch or isolation claim is made.

## Core concepts

- **Installation boundary:** the exact part of the installation covered by the analysis.
- **Source inventory:** the ordinary, alternate, auxiliary and stored-energy sources within that boundary.
- **Operating-state matrix:** a record of which sources and paths may be active, inactive, transferred or unresolved in each state.
- **Switching function:** the purpose a device is claimed to perform; identity or labelling alone does not prove the function.
- **Identification evidence:** information communicating source presence, function or boundary without independently proving isolation.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest evidence failure in the claim ladder; dependent conclusions stop there.
- **Competing interpretations:** plausible explanations retained until current authorised evidence resolves a contradiction.
- **Evidence owner and recheck trigger:** who or what must resolve the gap, and which event requires dependent conclusions to be reopened.

## Workflow

**S-O-U-R-C-E** sets the boundary and authority limit, outlines operating states, uncovers every credible energisation path, relates switching functions to classified evidence, checks the claim ladder for the first unsupported transition, and expresses a bounded conclusion.

The claim sequence is:

`source identified → path identified → operating state supported → device identity supported → switching function supported → controlled boundary supported → identification evidence adequate → qualified technical conclusion`

A failure at one transition prevents every broader dependent claim. A label can support identification evidence but does not establish device capability, source control or isolation.

## Practical applications

- create source inventories for installations with ordinary, embedded, auxiliary and stored-energy sources;
- compare normal, source-loss, transfer and maintenance-preparation states;
- preserve competing interpretations when drawings, photographs, labels and maintenance records conflict;
- assign evidence owners and recheck triggers rather than inventing missing facts;
- reopen switching, protection, identification and documentation conclusions when a source or path changes; and
- transfer the method to scenarios with at least two changed material conditions.

## Assessment relevance

Learners must independently distinguish facts, inferences, assumptions, contradictions and evidence gaps; locate the first unsupported transition; and state a bounded written conclusion. **Secure**, **developing**, **unsupported** and `stop-required` are educational planning states only, not official grades, competency decisions or technical approvals.

Blocking conditions include omitting a credible source, treating a label as proof, resolving contradictions by convenience, claiming isolation, or giving an operating sequence. Stronger performance elsewhere does not offset a blocking condition.

## Safety boundary

Written source-mapping and evidence reasoning only. Never infer isolation from one open device, absent indication, label, drawing or familiar arrangement. No switching, isolation, proving, opening, measurement, testing, adjustment, installation, repair, energisation, commissioning, certification or field verification is authorised.
