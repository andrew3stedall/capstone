# 12-Week Day 59 - Test Purposes, Dependencies and Safe Sequencing Concepts

## Core idea

Verification planning begins with a precise evidence question, not a familiar test name or instrument. A defensible paper plan separates purpose, method, result, interpretation and conclusion, then maps the prerequisites and dependencies that justify any proposed order.

## Workflow

Use **P-U-R-P-O-S-E**:

- **P** — Pose the question.
- **U** — Understand scope and state.
- **R** — Retrieve prerequisites.
- **P** — Pinpoint dependencies.
- **O** — Obtain the authorised method source.
- **S** — Sequence by rationale.
- **E** — Express limits and reopening triggers.

## Key distinctions

- A **test purpose** states the question to be answered.
- A **method** is the authorised process used to obtain evidence.
- A **result** is the recorded observation or value.
- An **interpretation** explains the result within stated conditions.
- A **conclusion** combines applicable evidence within an authorised scope.
- A **dependency** explains why one condition or evidence item must exist before another conclusion is attempted.

## Practical application

Given a fictional dossier, define verification questions, circuit and source boundaries, operating states, prerequisite evidence, dependency links, authorised-source requests, interpretation limits and reopening triggers. Do not write a field procedure or direct instrument use.

## Assessment relevance

The learner should be able to justify why evidence is needed and why an order is proposed, while stopping when source, authority, condition or prerequisite information is incomplete. A memorised sequence without installation-specific rationale is insufficient.

## Safety and review boundary

This note authorises no access, switching, isolation, proving de-energised, testing, measurement, instrument use, alteration, repair, energisation, commissioning, certification or verification. Exact test requirements, sequences, methods and acceptance criteria require current authorised sources, approved procedures and qualified review.
