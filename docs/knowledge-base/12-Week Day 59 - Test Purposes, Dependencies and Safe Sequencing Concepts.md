# 12-Week Day 59 - Test Purposes, Dependencies and Safe Sequencing Concepts

## Purpose

Develop evidence-controlled paper planning that begins with a precise verification question, defines boundaries, classifies evidence and stops at the first unsupported transition before any practical method or conclusion is considered.

## Learning module

- [Day 59 — Test Purposes, Dependencies and Safe Sequencing Concepts](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-59-test-purposes-dependencies-and-safe-sequencing-concepts.md)

## Core method

Use **P-U-R-P-O-S-E** to pose the question, understand boundaries, register evidence, pinpoint dependencies, obtain authorised sources, sequence only by documented rationale, and express a bounded status with reopening triggers.

## Core concepts

- **Purpose:** the precise evidence question.
- **Method:** the authorised process used to obtain evidence.
- **Result:** a recorded observation or value with provenance.
- **Interpretation:** bounded reasoning about what a result may mean.
- **Authorised conclusion:** a decision made within the relevant evidence and role framework.
- **Evidence states:** stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction and evidence gap.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest claim step that lacks adequate applicable evidence.
- **Competing interpretation:** an alternative explanation retained until evidence resolves it.
- **Evidence owner:** the authorised source or qualified person responsible for resolving a blocker.
- **Recheck trigger:** new evidence or change that reopens dependent reasoning.

## Practical applications

- define installation, circuit, source, operating-state, evidence, authority and decision boundaries;
- build provenance and evidence-state registers;
- separate purpose, method, result, interpretation and authorised conclusion;
- map dependencies and stop at unsupported transitions;
- preserve contradictions and competing interpretations;
- assign evidence owners and recheck triggers; and
- reopen dependent reasoning after sequential material changes.

## Assessment relevance

Evaluate purpose and boundary control, evidence classification, dependency reasoning, source and authority control, interpretation limits and change transfer independently. Use **secure**, **developing**, **unsupported** and `stop-required` only as educational planning states. They are not official grades, competency decisions, verification outcomes, compliance findings or technical approvals.

## Blocking conditions

Progress is blocked by practical direction, invented official content, omitted supply paths, stale or ambiguously identified evidence treated as current, reasoning beyond the first unsupported transition, concealed contradictions, unresolved ownership or unsupported compliance claims.
