## Core concepts

- **Retrieval practice:** recalling a concept or process before looking at the answer.
- **Recognition:** identifying familiar material when it is shown; weaker evidence of readiness than recall.
- **Error log:** a record of answer, confidence, error mechanism, correction evidence and varied re-attempt.
- **Transfer:** applying repaired reasoning when the scenario changes.
- **Readiness decision:** ready, ready with one named support, or not ready pending remediation.

## Recovery workflow

Use **R-E-S-T-O-R-E**:

1. Retrieve first.
2. Estimate confidence.
3. Sort errors by mechanism and consequence.
4. Triage one priority.
5. Open the minimum evidence needed.
6. Re-attempt in a changed context.
7. End deliberately.

The time limit is part of the method. Recovery loses value when catch-up becomes open-ended or fatigue encourages guessing.

## Practical application

The learner completes an eight-minute no-notes recall of Days 1–4, classifies weak responses, chooses exactly one high-value correction, checks the minimum relevant learning evidence, completes a changed-scenario re-attempt and records readiness for Day 6. Total study time is capped at 30 minutes.

## Assessment relevance

This block distinguishes material that merely feels familiar from knowledge and processes that can be retrieved independently. It also creates evidence of confidence calibration, error diagnosis, transfer and disciplined stopping—skills needed in later timed and integrated scenarios.

## Safety boundary

The block authorises no switching, isolation, testing, opening equipment, resetting, disconnection, alteration, repair, energisation, verification or practical demonstration.
