## Core concepts

- **Retrieval practice:** reconstructing a concept or process before opening the answer.
- **Recognition:** identifying familiar material after cues are supplied; weaker readiness evidence than recall.
- **Confidence calibration:** comparing confidence recorded before checking with actual answer quality.
- **Error mechanism:** the reason the reasoning failed, not merely the topic marked wrong.
- **Blocking error:** a prerequisite or safety-boundary failure that cannot be averaged away by stronger responses.
- **Transfer:** applying repaired reasoning after material facts, wording or evidence quality change.
- **Evidence owner:** the person or source responsible for resolving an unanswered authority, currency, scope or applicability question.

## Recovery workflow

Use **R-E-S-T-O-R-E**:

1. Retrieve first.
2. Estimate confidence.
3. Separate result from mechanism.
4. Triage the blocking priority.
5. Open minimum evidence.
6. Rebuild in a changed context.
7. End with evidence.

The time limit is part of the method. Recovery loses value when catch-up becomes open-ended or fatigue encourages guessing.

## Readiness model

Readiness is based on four prerequisite domains:

1. authorised-source mapping and currency reasoning;
2. hazard, pathway and consequence reasoning;
3. authority, supervision and stop-boundary reasoning;
4. source navigation, applicability and dependency reasoning.

The learner is not ready when any blocking or `stop-required` error remains. “Ready with one named support” is not a way to conceal a missing safety boundary.

## Practical application

The learner completes an eight-minute no-notes recall of Days 1–4, records confidence before checking, classifies weak responses by mechanism and consequence, chooses exactly one blocking or highest-value correction, checks the minimum relevant learning evidence, completes a materially changed scenario and records criterion-level readiness for Day 6. Total study time is capped at 30 minutes.

## Assessment relevance

This block distinguishes material that merely feels familiar from processes that can be retrieved and explained independently. It also produces evidence of confidence calibration, error diagnosis, transfer, evidence ownership and disciplined stopping. A strong overall result cannot hide one unsafe or blocking misconception.

## Safety boundary

The block authorises no switching, isolation, testing, opening equipment, resetting, disconnection, alteration, repair, energisation, verification or practical demonstration. Unresolved technical claims remain `reference_check_required` and require current authorised evidence or qualified guidance.
