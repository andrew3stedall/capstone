# 12-Week Day 53 - Alternative, Multiple and Embedded Supply Awareness

## Purpose

Builds evidence-controlled source discovery and operating-state reasoning before any claim about switching, isolation, protection, fault conditions, identification, earthing or verification.

## Learning module

- [Day 53 — Alternative, Multiple and Embedded Supply Awareness](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-53-alternative-multiple-and-embedded-supply-awareness.md)

## Core method

Use **S-O-U-R-C-E-S**:

1. set installation, subsystem, operating-state, evidence, authority and decision boundaries;
2. observe source clues with provenance;
3. uncover candidate energy paths without inventing connections;
4. represent operating states separately;
5. connect switching, isolation, protection, fault, identity, neutral, earthing and verification interactions;
6. examine current authorised evidence and retain contradictions; and
7. state a bounded claim, stop at the first unsupported transition, assign owners and schedule rechecks.

## Core concepts

- **Connection boundary:** interface between a source, subsystem or external installation and the analysed scenario.
- **Operating state:** defined combination of source availability, switch positions, controls and load connections.
- **Backfeed possibility:** plausible energisation path requiring evidence, not a confirmed field condition.
- **Provenance:** source, date or revision and scenario linkage of evidence.
- **Competing interpretations:** plausible readings retained until resolved.
- **First unsupported transition:** earliest claim step not supported by evidence.
- **Evidence owner:** authorised source, person or reviewer responsible for a gap.
- **Recheck trigger:** evidence or material change that requires reconsideration.

Evidence states are stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction and evidence gap. Confidence is recorded separately.

## Practical applications

- build source and connection-boundary inventories from conflicting fictional records;
- map normal, backup, islanded, maintenance and automatic-restart states;
- separate source existence from demonstrated connection, isolation and safe-state;
- stop claims at the first unsupported transition;
- assign evidence owners and recheck triggers; and
- reopen dependent conclusions after generator and control-supply disclosures.

## Assessment relevance

Evaluate boundaries, provenance, evidence classification, confidence calibration, source discovery, operating-state mapping, contradiction handling, first-unsupported-transition discipline, evidence ownership and two-change propagation independently.

Use **secure**, **developing**, **unsupported** and `stop-required` only as educational planning states. They are not official grades, competency outcomes, defect classifications, compliance decisions or technical approvals.

## Blocking conditions

Readiness is blocked by an omitted disclosed source, invented connection path, hidden contradiction, paper-only isolation or safe-state claim, reasoning beyond the first unsupported transition, missing ownership, incomplete change propagation or unauthorised practical activity.

## Safety and references

This note authorises no site access, opening, switching, isolation, proving de-energised, testing, measurement, installation, alteration, repair, energisation, commissioning, certification, design approval or field verification.

Exact source classifications, connection arrangements, switching and isolation requirements, protection interactions, neutral and earthing treatment, identification, verification and official assessment expectations require current authorised sources and qualified review.
