# 12-Week Day 45 - Consumer Mains, Submains and Final Subcircuits

Day 45 develops the route reasoning from [[12-Week Day 44 - Environmental Influences, Segregation and Support Concepts]] into source-to-load circuit classification.

## Learning module

- [Day 45 — Consumer Mains, Submains and Final Subcircuits](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-45-consumer-mains-submains-and-final-subcircuits.md)

## Prerequisites

- [[12-Week Day 44 - Environmental Influences, Segregation and Support Concepts]]
- [[Wiring Rules and Design]]
- [[Overcurrent Protection]]

## Core workflow

Use **T-R-A-C-E**: tag every source and distribution point, record start and end boundaries, assign a provisional category from function, check authorised evidence, and explain dependencies and unresolved items.

## Key distinctions

- Consumer mains connect the relevant supply boundary to the main control point; exact jurisdictional wording requires verification.
- A submain supplies another distribution point.
- A final subcircuit supplies utilisation points without another distribution board intervening.
- Circuit identity follows documented function and boundary, not conductor size, route length or appearance.

## Practical application

Learners produce a source-to-load single-line sketch, classify each circuit provisionally, map protection dependencies and reopen the analysis when an alternate source or new downstream board is added.

## Assessment relevance

A strong response defines start and end points, records all sources, uses functional classification, identifies protection dependencies and distinguishes confirmed from unresolved boundaries.

## Misconceptions to track

- Every long circuit is a submain.
- A large conductor must be consumer mains.
- Labels alone prove the point of supply.
- Alternate sources do not affect circuit-boundary analysis.
- Final-subcircuit rules can be applied to every downstream conductor.
