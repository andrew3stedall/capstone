# 12-Week Day 45 - Consumer Mains, Submains and Final Subcircuits

## Purpose

Builds evidence-controlled source-to-load boundary reasoning so circuit categories remain provisional until source identity, operating state, function, start and end points, and relevant authorised evidence are supported.

## Core method

Use **T-R-A-C-E**:

1. take installation, source, evidence, authority and decision boundaries, then tag every credible source;
2. record energisation paths, circuit endpoints, provenance and contradictions;
3. assign provisional functions and circuit categories from function rather than appearance;
4. check current authorised definitions and source, load and protection dependencies; and
5. explain the evidence chain, stop at the first unsupported transition, assign owners and recheck triggers, and reopen affected conclusions after changes.

## Core concepts

- **Source:** identified means by which an installation section may be energised.
- **Operating state:** documented source and switching configuration for the case being analysed.
- **Energisation path:** complete route by which a source can energise the relevant section.
- **Supply boundary:** documented point at which the relevant supply, ownership or responsibility begins for the analysis.
- **Distribution point:** a board or assembly supplying downstream circuits.
- **Utilisation point:** a point at which energy is made available to or used by current-using equipment.
- **Circuit boundary:** stated start and end used for one reasoning task.
- **Protection dependency:** upstream or downstream relationship affecting the validity of a protection claim.
- **Competing interpretations:** plausible explanations retained until stronger evidence resolves them.
- **First unsupported transition:** earliest point where a claim exceeds its evidence; dependent claims stop there.
- **Evidence owner:** person, authorised source, current document set or qualified reviewer expected to resolve a gap.
- **Recheck trigger:** evidence or changed conditions that require earlier conclusions to reopen.

## Key distinctions

- Consumer-mains reasoning concerns the relevant supply boundary to main control point; exact jurisdictional wording requires verification.
- A submain supplies another distribution point.
- A final subcircuit supplies utilisation points without another distribution board intervening.
- Circuit category follows supported function and boundaries, not conductor size, route length, board label or physical appearance.
- A provisional category supports further analysis; it does not prove conductor suitability, protection adequacy, isolation effectiveness or compliance.
- Alternate-source possibility and confirmed present operating state are different evidence claims.

## Evidence states

Classify material statements as stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction or evidence gap. Record confidence separately. Correct guessing and confident fluency are not secure evidence.

## Practical applications

- produce a source-to-load single-line map for a fictional installation;
- define the start, end and load boundary of each circuit;
- classify each circuit provisionally from function;
- retain contradictory drawings, schedules, labels and maintenance records as visible evidence;
- stop dependent claims at the first unsupported transition;
- assign evidence owners and recheck triggers; and
- propagate at least two material changes through source, circuit, load, protection and isolation dependencies.

## Assessment relevance

Evaluate boundary control, source mapping, functional classification, evidence-state discipline, contradiction handling, dependency mapping, first-unsupported-transition control, evidence ownership, change propagation and safety communication independently.

Use **secure**, **developing**, **unsupported** and `stop-required` as educational planning states. They are not official grades, competency outcomes, defect classifications, compliance decisions or technical approvals. No aggregate score or unofficial pass threshold applies.

## Blocking conditions

Secure readiness is blocked by an invented point of supply or operating state; omission of a credible source; classification without defined endpoints; hidden assumptions or contradictions; dependent claims beyond the first unsupported transition; missing owners or triggers; transfer using fewer than two material changes; incomplete reopening of affected conclusions; or unauthorised practical activity.
