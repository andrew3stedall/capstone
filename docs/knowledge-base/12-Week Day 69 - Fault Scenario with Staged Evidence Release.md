# 12-Week Day 69 - Fault Scenario with Staged Evidence Release

## Purpose

Develop disciplined, document-only fault reasoning by preserving literal evidence, competing hypotheses and immutable stage snapshots while fictional evidence is released incrementally.

## Learning module

- [Day 69 — Fault Scenario with Staged Evidence Release](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-69-fault-scenario-with-staged-evidence-release.md)

## Core method

Use **R-E-L-E-A-S-E**: Record literal evidence and nine boundaries; Establish competing hypotheses and falsifiable predictions; Lock the snapshot; Examine one stage for provenance and applicability; Adjust confidence without converting it into proof; Stop at the first unsupported transition; End with a bounded conclusion.

## Core concepts

- **Nine boundaries:** installation, equipment, circuit, source, operating state, time, evidence, authority and decision.
- **Evidence states:** stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction and evidence gap.
- **Diagnostic snapshot:** the locked stage record of hypotheses, predictions, confidence, evidence states and unresolved dependencies.
- **Evidence embargo:** later-stage evidence cannot rewrite an earlier snapshot.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** an expected observation capable of weakening or rejecting a hypothesis.
- **Discriminating evidence:** evidence that separates competing explanations.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest unsupported premise in a claim chain; dependent conclusions remain unsupported.
- **Evidence owner:** authorised source, custodian or qualified person responsible for resolving a blocker.
- **Recheck trigger:** new evidence or a material change that reopens affected reasoning.
- **Confidence calibration:** confidence is recorded separately from correctness and evidence quality.
- **Educational readiness states:** `secure`, `developing`, `unsupported` and `stop-required`; not official grades or technical decisions.

## Practical applications

Complete a staged diagnostic release pack that:

- preserves literal wording and record provenance;
- defines all nine boundaries;
- maintains at least three competing hypotheses with falsifiable predictions;
- locks a snapshot after each stage;
- records confidence, correctness and evidence quality separately;
- stops every claim chain at its first unsupported transition;
- assigns evidence owners and recheck triggers; and
- reopens affected dependencies after two sequential material changes.

## Assessment relevance

Assess boundary control, hypothesis control, stage discipline, evidence control, dependency control, confidence calibration, ownership and transfer, and conclusion/safety independently. There is no aggregate score. A blocking `unsupported` criterion or any `stop-required` state cannot be offset by stronger work elsewhere.

## Blocking conditions

Progress is blocked by invented or altered evidence, later-stage contamination, hidden contradictions, unresolved identity or event-window assumptions, reasoning beyond a first unsupported transition, confidence treated as proof, material changes ignored, root-cause declarations without discriminating evidence, practical troubleshooting directions, or unsupported verification, acceptance, certification, compliance or technical-approval claims.
