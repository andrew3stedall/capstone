# 12-Week Day 38 - Switchboard Functional Areas and Arrangement Principles

## Purpose

Builds an evidence-controlled functional map of a fictional switchboard before any physical-arrangement, capacity, access, isolation or compliance claim is considered.

## Core concepts

- **Board boundary:** the board or represented section included in the analysis.
- **Functional area:** a conceptual grouping by purpose, not proof of compliant physical construction.
- **Incoming-supply function:** the represented source entry or supply path.
- **Switching function:** the attributed control purpose; it does not by itself prove isolation capability.
- **Protection function:** the attributed response role for abnormal conditions; identity does not prove suitability.
- **Distribution function:** the represented division of supply among downstream sections or circuits.
- **Neutral and earthing functions:** represented connection roles that require separate current evidence.
- **Control or auxiliary function:** a supply or dependency that may not follow the main power path.
- **Evidence boundary:** the point beyond which available records cannot support a reliable conclusion.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest unsupported rung in a claim chain; dependent claims must stop there.
- **Prospective change:** a proposed alteration that reopens affected arrangement, protection, capacity, access and documentation questions.
- **Change propagation:** revisiting every conclusion that depends on a changed condition.

## Evidence states

Use these labels explicitly:

- **stated fact**
- **derived fact**
- **supported inference**
- **assumption**
- **contradiction**
- **evidence gap**

For unresolved matters, retain **competing interpretations**, identify an **evidence owner**, and set a **recheck trigger**.

## Workflow

**B-O-A-R-D-S**:

1. **Bound** the board, purpose, authority and evidence.
2. **Outline** every source, path, auxiliary supply and outgoing group.
3. **Assign** shown items to functions without guessing hidden connections.
4. **Relate** switching, protection, distribution, neutral, earthing, control and outgoing functions.
5. **Detect** contradictions, gaps, change consequences and the first unsupported transition.
6. **State** bounded observations, owners, triggers and stop conditions.

## Practical applications

- annotate original fictional schematics by function;
- build an evidence-controlled relationship table;
- retain competing interpretations for conflicting records;
- identify the first unsupported transition;
- propagate at least two material scenario changes through the functional map; and
- write a bounded arrangement review that distinguishes supported observations from prohibited claims.

## Assessment relevance

Observable evidence includes the learner's ability to:

- control board and evidence boundaries;
- map all represented source and functional relationships;
- classify evidence and cite provenance;
- avoid treating proximity or labels as proof;
- reopen affected conclusions after a change;
- identify blocking conditions; and
- stop before unsupported physical, isolation, capacity or compliance claims.

Use **secure**, **developing**, **unsupported** and `stop-required` as independent learning-planning states. They are not official assessment grades, competency decisions or technical approvals.

## Blocking conditions

A secure outcome is blocked by:

- invented source paths, functions, capacities or segregation claims;
- ignored auxiliary supplies or contradictions;
- conclusions carried beyond the first unsupported transition;
- failure to reopen dependencies after material change;
- treating spare physical space as verified capacity; or
- proposing unauthorised access, operation, measurement or testing.

## Safety boundary

Paper-based functional and evidence mapping only. No cover removal, barrier removal, device operation, isolation, live access, measurement, proving, testing, alteration, construction approval, certification or compliance determination is authorised.
