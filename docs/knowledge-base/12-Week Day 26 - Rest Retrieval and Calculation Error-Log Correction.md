# 12-Week Day 26 - Rest Retrieval and Calculation Error-Log Correction

## Purpose

Provides a bounded recovery block for spaced retrieval, confidence calibration, root-error diagnosis and downstream correction without adding new electrical theory.

## Core concepts

- **Error log:** record of error, cause, repair, evidence status, affected work and prevention cue.
- **Error categories:** arithmetic, unit, evidence, method, transcription and conclusion.
- **Root error:** earliest error that creates later symptom errors.
- **First unsupported transition:** earliest reasoning step that exceeds verified evidence.
- **Downstream reopening:** repeating later work affected by an upstream correction.
- **Evidence owner:** authorised person, record or source needed to close a gap or contradiction.
- **Recheck trigger:** new evidence or changed condition that requires reassessment.
- **Confidence calibration:** matching confidence to evidence and reasoning rather than familiarity.
- **Recovery limit:** maximum 30 minutes, no more than three selected errors and one active root repair at a time.

## R-E-P-A-I-R workflow

Retrieve first; expose the earliest error; pinpoint category and evidence status; amend one supported cause; identify downstream effects; record readiness and rest.

## Evidence and learning states

Material inputs and claims are labelled as stated facts, supported inferences, assumptions, contradictions or evidence gaps. Each learning criterion is assessed separately as secure, developing, unsupported or `stop-required`. These are educational planning states, not official grades or competency decisions.

## Practical application

Learners retrieve four recent workflows, record confidence before checking, triage up to three high-value errors, repair one root cause at a time, reopen affected work, identify evidence owners and recheck triggers, complete one changed-context transfer and issue a bounded readiness statement.

## Assessment relevance

Observable evidence includes:

- accurate closed-note retrieval;
- distinction between root and symptom errors;
- transparent evidence labelling;
- identification of the first unsupported transition;
- correct downstream reopening;
- transfer under at least two changed material conditions;
- confidence calibration; and
- fatigue-aware stopping without invented closure.

Strength in one criterion cannot cancel an unsupported or `stop-required` result elsewhere.

Written recovery work only.
