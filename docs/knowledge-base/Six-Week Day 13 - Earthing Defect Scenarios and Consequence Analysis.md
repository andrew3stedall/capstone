# Six-Week Day 13 — Earthing Defect Scenarios and Consequence Analysis

## Purpose

This note supports paper-based analysis of fictional earthing defects. It helps the learner separate supplied observations, provisional defect statements, conditional consequences and verified conclusions without claiming practical authority.

## Core model

Use **D-E-F-E-C-T**:

- **Describe only the supplied evidence** — separate facts, derivations, assumptions and missing information.
- **Establish the intended protective role** — identify the role potentially affected.
- **Form a provisional defect statement** — match wording strength to the evidence.
- **Extend a consequence chain conditionally** — state every dependency explicitly.
- **Check the claim level and required source** — identify what remains `reference_check_required`.
- **Trigger the safe next action** — stop and refer inspection, testing and rectification to appropriately authorised people.

The central rule is:

> An observation may justify concern and escalation, but it does not by itself prove a defect, a hazardous outcome or protective-device behaviour.

## Claim levels

1. **Observation** — a supplied fact in the scenario.
2. **Possible defect** — a provisional mismatch requiring verification.
3. **Supported defect finding** — adequate evidence plus an applicable current requirement.
4. **Verified technical conclusion** — a conclusion within the scope of competent inspection, testing, documentation and authorised review.

Do not skip directly from an observation to a verified conclusion.

## Consequence chain

A defensible chain records:

1. changed condition;
2. protective role potentially affected;
3. path or potential relationship that may change;
4. conditional hazard mechanism;
5. missing evidence; and
6. safe escalation.

Use “if” wording whenever the consequence depends on facts not supplied. Avoid exact fault current, touch voltage, operating time or device behaviour unless verified from current authorised evidence.

## Practical application

For each original fictional scenario, complete seven fields:

| Field | Response |
|---|---|
| Evidence | Supplied facts only |
| Protective role | Intended function potentially affected |
| Possible defect | Bounded provisional wording |
| Changed condition | Path, connection or potential relationship that may differ |
| Conditional consequence | Hazard mechanism with explicit dependencies |
| Missing proof | Source, inspection, test, documentation or context needed |
| Escalation | Safe stop and authorised next step |

Then change one scenario fact and revise only the affected claims. This tests whether reasoning follows evidence rather than a memorised answer.

## Assessment relevance

A strong learner response:

- separates facts from assumptions;
- distinguishes earthing, bonding and fault-path roles;
- writes a provisional statement at the correct claim level;
- develops a conditional consequence chain without inventing values;
- identifies the evidence and current authorised sources needed;
- revises the analysis when a fact changes; and
- applies a clear practical stop boundary.

The module uses a 12-point educational rubric. It is not an official RTO pass mark.

## Common misconceptions

- A drawing omission proves a physical defect.
- Visible damage proves loss of continuity.
- One earthing concern proves every protective layer has failed.
- A possible fault path proves a device will or will not operate.
- Historical verification proves present condition.
- A learner can prescribe rectification from a paper scenario.
- “Safe” or “unsafe” can be concluded from one observation.

## Safety and review boundary

This material authorises no site access, opening, isolation, proving, conductor tracing, continuity testing, resistance or loop measurement, fault creation, resetting, disconnection, reconnection, alteration, repair, energisation, commissioning, certification or verification.

Real suspected defects require qualified assessment. Exact classifications, connection requirements, test methods, values, acceptance criteria, defect findings and jurisdiction-specific duties remain `reference_check_required`.
