# 12-Week Day 72 - Planning a Compliant Design Response and Evidence Trail

## Purpose

Convert the Day 71 controlled scenario brief into a dependency-ordered design-response dossier whose boundaries, evidence states, calculations, options, source questions, hold points, changes and limitations can be reconstructed without implying qualified approval.

## Learning module

- [Day 72 — Planning a Compliant Design Response and Evidence Trail](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-72-planning-a-compliant-design-response-and-evidence-trail.md)

## Core method

Use **D-E-S-I-G-N-E-R**: Define nine boundaries; Extract and classify six evidence states; Sequence claim chains; Identify authorised-source questions and applicability; Generate traceable calculations and competing options; Note first unsupported transitions, owners and triggers; Evaluate interactions and propagate changes; Record independent readiness states and a bounded recommendation.

## Core concepts

- **Nine boundaries:** installation, circuit, load, source, operating state, route and environment, time, evidence and authority.
- **Six evidence states:** stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction and evidence gap.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest unsupported link in a claim chain; dependent conclusions remain unsupported until repaired.
- **Design option:** a candidate retained for comparison, not an accepted design or approval.
- **Design hold point:** a controlled stop caused by a named gap or contradiction.
- **Evidence owner:** the authorised source, custodian or qualified person responsible for resolving a hold point.
- **Recheck trigger:** evidence or a material change that reopens affected reasoning.
- **Change propagation:** reopening every dependent calculation, check and decision after a material input changes.
- **Non-compensatory blocker:** a material weakness that cannot be averaged away by stronger work elsewhere.
- **Educational readiness states:** `secure`, `developing`, `unsupported` and `stop-required`; these are not official grades or competency decisions.

## Practical applications

Prepare a fictional design-response dossier that:

- defines all nine boundaries;
- preserves literal evidence and provenance;
- classifies all six evidence states;
- maps claims to their first unsupported transitions;
- records traceable calculations and competing options;
- names the question and applicability boundary for every authorised-source check;
- assigns owners and recheck triggers to hold points;
- propagates two sequential material changes;
- prepares inspection and verification handover evidence; and
- ends with a bounded recommendation rather than a compliance or approval claim.

## Assessment relevance

Assess boundary control, evidence control, dependency control, source and applicability control, interaction and change control, and decision and conclusion control independently. There is no aggregate score. Any `stop-required` state or an `unsupported` non-compensatory blocker prevents progression regardless of stronger performance elsewhere.

## Blocking conditions

Progress is blocked by invented or altered evidence, hidden assumptions or contradictions, unsupported identity or source transfer, reasoning beyond the first unsupported transition, stale decisions after material changes, confidence treated as proof, unauthorised practical directions or unsupported competency, compliance, verification, acceptance, certification or technical-approval claims.

## Safety and references

This note authorises no site access, opening, switching, isolation, proving de-energised, testing, measurement, instrument use, alteration, repair, energisation, commissioning, acceptance, certification, verification or field fault finding.
