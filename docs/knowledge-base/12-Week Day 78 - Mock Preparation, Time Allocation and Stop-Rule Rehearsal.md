# 12-Week Day 78 - Mock Preparation, Time Allocation and Stop-Rule Rehearsal

## Purpose

Prepare an evidence-controlled mock workflow that separates official instructions from learner-selected pacing, protects review capacity and stops unsupported or unauthorised decisions.

## Learning module

- [Day 78 — Mock Preparation, Time Allocation and Stop-Rule Rehearsal](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-78-mock-preparation-time-allocation-and-stop-rule-rehearsal.md)

## Core method

Use **P-R-E-P-A-R-E**: Pin boundaries; Read literally; Extract deliverables and dependencies; Protect review and transitions; Attach controls; Rehearse and record; Evaluate readiness and reopen after change.

## Core concepts

- **Boundary:** the installation, equipment, circuit, source state, time, evidence, authority and decision limits of the mock.
- **Time gate:** a planned progress decision point.
- **Stop rule:** a condition requiring the affected decision to stop.
- **Deferral rule:** a condition allowing a non-critical item to be bounded and revisited.
- **Completion rule:** the minimum evidence required before a deliverable is treated as complete.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest reasoning step without adequate applicable evidence.
- **Evidence owner:** the authorised person, source or record responsible for closing a gap.
- **Recheck trigger:** the evidence event that permits reconsideration.
- **Material change:** a change that may invalidate earlier planning or evidence.
- **Non-compensatory blocker:** a critical gap that unrelated strengths cannot offset.
- **Secure / developing / unsupported / stop-required:** independent educational planning states, not official grades or competency decisions.

## Practical applications

Create a preparation record that:

- identifies the current mock version and instruction authority;
- separates official, learner-selected and unknown timing conditions;
- maps deliverables, constraints and dependencies;
- protects review and transition capacity;
- assigns time gates, completion rules, deferral rules and stop rules;
- stops dependent conclusions at the first unsupported transition;
- records evidence owners and recheck triggers; and
- reopens affected decisions after material change.

## Assessment relevance

The block reduces avoidable control errors before the staged mocks. Readiness is classified criterion by criterion without a total score. A safety, authority, identity, source-state, fabricated-evidence or unsupported-exactness blocker cannot be offset by stronger timing or presentation elsewhere.

Any duration, permitted-material rule or assessment condition is official only when supplied by the relevant current authorised instruction. Learner-selected pacing controls must be labelled as such.
