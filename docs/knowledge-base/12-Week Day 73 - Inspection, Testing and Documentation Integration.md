# 12-Week Day 73 - Inspection, Testing and Documentation Integration

## Purpose

Integrate bounded design claims, supplied visual observations, test-purpose records and controlled documents into a traceable dossier that exposes unsupported transitions, contradictions, change effects and re-verification triggers without implying qualified acceptance.

## Learning module

- [Day 73 — Inspection, Testing and Documentation Integration](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-73-inspection-testing-and-documentation-integration.md)

## Core method

Use **C-O-V-E-R-A-G-E**: Clarify eight boundaries and claims; Organise literal evidence by purpose; Verify provenance and applicability; Establish claim chains and dependencies; Relate evidence only within its boundary; Audit coverage, contradictions and changes; Give blockers owners and triggers; End with independent readiness states and a bounded summary.

## Core concepts

- **Eight boundaries:** installation, circuit, source, operating state, time, evidence, document and authority.
- **Six evidence states:** stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction and evidence gap.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest unsupported link in a claim chain; every dependent conclusion remains unsupported.
- **Evidence transfer:** use of evidence across a different identity, state, time, revision or purpose; it requires explicit support.
- **Correction claim:** evidence that a change was asserted, not proof that affected claims were subsequently rechecked.
- **Re-verification trigger:** a material change, correction, contradiction or evidence failure that reopens affected claims.
- **Evidence owner:** the authorised custodian, source or qualified person responsible for resolving an open blocker.
- **Change propagation:** reopening every affected claim and record after a material change, including a second sequential change.
- **Non-compensatory blocker:** a weakness that cannot be averaged away by stronger work elsewhere.
- **Educational readiness states:** `secure`, `developing`, `unsupported` and `stop-required`; these are not official grades or findings.

## Practical applications

Prepare a fictional integration pack that:

- defines all eight boundaries;
- preserves literal observations, results, correction claims and document metadata;
- classifies all six evidence states;
- maps claims to their first unsupported transitions;
- records competing interpretations;
- assigns evidence owners and recheck triggers;
- propagates two sequential material changes;
- evaluates readiness independently without aggregate scoring; and
- ends with a bounded summary for qualified review.

## Assessment relevance

Assess boundary control, evidence-state control, claim-chain control, coverage and contradiction control, change and re-verification control, and safety and conclusion control independently. Any `stop-required` state or an `unsupported` non-compensatory blocker prevents progression regardless of stronger work elsewhere.

## Blocking conditions

Progress is blocked by invented or altered evidence, hidden assumptions or contradictions, unsupported circuit or source-state transfer, reasoning beyond the first unsupported transition, stale records after material changes, correction claims treated as successful re-verification, confidence treated as proof, unauthorised practical directions or unsupported compliance, acceptance, certification, competency or technical-approval claims.

## Safety and references

This note authorises no site access, opening, switching, isolation, proving de-energised, testing, measurement, instrument use, alteration, repair, energisation, commissioning, acceptance, certification, verification or field fault finding.
