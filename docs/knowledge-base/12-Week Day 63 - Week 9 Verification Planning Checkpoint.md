# 12-Week Day 63 - Week 9 Verification Planning Checkpoint

## Purpose

Provide a document-only checkpoint for combining verification questions, eight planning boundaries, evidence categories, claim classification, confidence calibration, dependency control, provenance, contradictions, ownership and bounded conclusions without providing practical instructions.

## Learning module

- [Day 63 — Week 9 Verification Planning Checkpoint](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-63-week-9-verification-planning-checkpoint.md)

## Core method

Use **P-L-A-N-N-E-R**: Pin down the question; Locate every boundary; Allocate evidence and classify claims; Name prerequisites and dependencies; Note provenance, completeness and limitations; Examine claim chains and stop at the first unsupported transition; Record ownership, triggers and criterion-level readiness.

## Core concepts

- **Planning boundaries:** installation, circuit, source, operating state, time, evidence, authority and decision.
- **Evidence categories:** design, inspection, result, equipment-system and documentary evidence; none proves the others automatically.
- **Evidence states:** stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction and evidence gap.
- **Confidence calibration:** low, medium or high confidence recorded separately from correctness and evidence quality.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest unsupported arrow in a claim chain; all dependent claims beyond it remain unsupported.
- **Competing interpretations:** plausible explanations retained without promoting either to fact.
- **Evidence owner:** the authorised source, custodian or qualified person responsible for resolving a blocker.
- **Recheck trigger:** a new record or material change that reopens affected reasoning.
- **Educational readiness states:** secure, developing, unsupported and `stop-required`; not official grades or technical decisions.

## Practical applications

For a fictional evidence pack:

- write one bounded verification question;
- define all eight boundaries;
- keep evidence categories separate;
- classify material claims and record confidence independently;
- explain prerequisites and dependencies;
- check identity, provenance, date, version, completeness, consistency and equipment-system limits;
- retain competing interpretations;
- stop at the first unsupported transition;
- assign evidence owners and recheck triggers;
- reopen affected dependencies after two sequential material changes; and
- communicate criterion-level readiness without pass/fail, compliance or certification claims.

## Assessment relevance

Evaluate question and boundary control, evidence separation, claim classification, confidence calibration, dependency reasoning, provenance review, unsupported-transition control, ownership, two-change transfer and authority communication independently. There is no aggregate score. An unsupported blocking criterion or any `stop-required` state cannot be offset by stronger work elsewhere.

## Blocking conditions

Progress is blocked by invented or altered evidence, concealed contradictions, missing identity or source information treated as complete, continued reasoning beyond the first unsupported transition, unresolved blockers without owners or triggers, ignored material changes, practical testing directions, or unsupported competency, verification, compliance, acceptance, certification or technical-approval claims.
