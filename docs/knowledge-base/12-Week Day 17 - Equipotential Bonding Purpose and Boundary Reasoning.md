# 12-Week Day 17 - Equipotential Bonding Purpose and Boundary Reasoning

## Purpose

This note supports written reasoning about equipotential bonding purpose, conductive-part classification and evidence boundaries. It prevents a learner from treating metal, proximity or a visible conductor as proof that bonding is required or effective.

## Core concepts

- **Equipotential bonding:** a protective connection intended to reduce hazardous potential differences between relevant conductive parts.
- **Protective earthing:** a protective relationship for relevant exposed conductive equipment parts within the wider earthing and protection arrangement.
- **Extraneous conductive part:** a conductive item outside the electrical installation that may introduce a potential from elsewhere; metal or proximity alone does not establish the classification.
- **Potential difference:** an electrical difference between points that can drive current if a conductive path exists.
- **Simultaneously accessible:** capable of being touched at the same time under the relevant conditions; exact criteria require authorised verification.
- **Bonding conductor:** a conductor intended to establish a bonding relationship; visibility does not prove endpoints, continuity, condition or suitability.
- **Claim boundary:** the strongest conclusion supported without assumption.
- **Boundary condition:** a location, accessibility, construction, source or supply fact that limits a conclusion.

## B-O-U-N-D-A-R-Y workflow

1. **Bound** the scenario and relevant conditions.
2. **Observe** supplied facts without premature classification.
3. **Understand** each conductive item’s relationship to equipment and the installation.
4. **Name** the possible source of a relevant potential.
5. **Distinguish** bonding from protective earthing and other protective functions.
6. **Audit** identity, requirement, connection, continuity, suitability and outcome separately.
7. **Record** uncertainty and facts that would reopen the conclusion.
8. **Yield** at the authority boundary.

## Practical application

The learner classifies original conductive-item scenarios, completes an evidence ladder for a possible bonding relationship, and reopens conclusions when an insulating section, accessibility change, outdated drawing, alternative supply or equipment-construction change alters the evidence.

## Assessment relevance

Observable evidence includes:

- defining bonding without claiming that it removes all voltage;
- distinguishing protective earthing and bonding by purpose;
- classifying conductive items from equipment, origin, accessibility and potential relationships rather than metal alone;
- separating item presence, classification, requirement, connection, continuity, suitability and protective outcome;
- using conditional language where evidence is incomplete;
- reopening conclusions when boundary conditions change; and
- refusing to access, trace, test, connect, alter or approve beyond authority.
