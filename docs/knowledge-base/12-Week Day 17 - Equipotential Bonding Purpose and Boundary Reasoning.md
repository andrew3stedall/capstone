# 12-Week Day 17 - Equipotential Bonding Purpose and Boundary Reasoning

## Purpose

This note supports written reasoning about equipotential bonding purpose, conductive-part classification, evidence boundaries and bounded conclusions. It prevents metal, proximity, a visible conductor or a conflicting record from being treated as proof that bonding is required or effective.

## Core concepts

- **Equipotential bonding:** a protective connection intended to reduce hazardous potential differences between relevant conductive parts.
- **Protective earthing:** a protective relationship for relevant exposed conductive equipment parts within the wider earthing and protection arrangement.
- **Extraneous conductive part:** a conductive item outside the electrical installation that may introduce a potential from elsewhere; metal or proximity alone does not establish the classification.
- **Claim boundary:** the strongest conclusion supported without assumption.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest point where an argument depends on an assumption, contradiction or unresolved evidence gap.
- **Evidence owner:** the authorised person, record or process responsible for resolving a material gap.
- **Recheck trigger:** a change or new evidence that requires the conclusion to be reopened.

## Evidence labels

- **Stated fact:** supplied directly by the scenario or a current authorised record.
- **Derived fact:** follows transparently from stated facts without an added premise.
- **Supported inference:** a bounded interpretation supported by available evidence.
- **Assumption:** an unstated premise needed to continue the reasoning.
- **Contradiction:** material sources or observations that cannot jointly support the same conclusion without resolution.
- **Evidence gap:** information required for a claim but not available.

## B-O-U-N-D-A-R-Y workflow

1. **Bound** the scenario and material conditions.
2. **Observe** supplied facts and assign evidence labels.
3. **Understand** each conductive item’s relationship to equipment and the installation.
4. **Name** the possible source of a relevant potential.
5. **Distinguish** bonding from protective earthing and other protective functions.
6. **Audit** identity, classification, requirement, connection, continuity, suitability and outcome separately.
7. **Record** the first unsupported transition, evidence owner and recheck trigger.
8. **Yield** at the authority boundary.

## Practical application

The learner classifies original conductive-item scenarios, completes a claim-and-evidence ledger, identifies contradictory records, and rebuilds the reasoning when at least two material conditions change. A previous conclusion is not carried into a changed context without rechecking its premises.

## Assessment relevance

Observable evidence includes:

- defining bonding without claiming that it removes all voltage;
- distinguishing protective earthing and bonding by purpose;
- classifying conductive items from equipment, origin, accessibility and potential relationships rather than metal alone;
- separating presence, identity, classification, requirement, connection, continuity, suitability and protective outcome;
- applying the six evidence labels accurately;
- stopping at the first unsupported transition;
- assigning evidence owners and recheck triggers;
- rebuilding reasoning after at least two material scenario changes; and
- refusing access, tracing, testing, connection, alteration or approval beyond authority.

Criterion states are **secure**, **developing**, **unsupported** and `stop-required`. Strong performance in one criterion cannot cancel an unsupported or `stop-required` result elsewhere. These are educational planning states, not official assessment grades or competency decisions.

## Safety boundary

This is a written reasoning module. It authorises no switching, isolation, opening, proving, tracing, measurement, testing, disconnection, reconnection, installation, alteration, repair, energisation, commissioning, certification or verification. Exact definitions, classifications, connection requirements, conductor criteria, accessibility rules, test methods, acceptance criteria and jurisdiction-specific duties remain `reference_check_required`.
