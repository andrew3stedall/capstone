## Core concept

Day 26 is a deliberate recovery and retrieval block before the full mock examination. It introduces no new electrical requirements. The learner retrieves Week 4 reasoning, identifies one high-confidence misconception, performs the smallest useful correction and preserves concentration for Day 27.

The **P-A-U-S-E** workflow is:

1. **Produce from memory**.
2. **Analyse the gap**.
3. **Use the smallest source return**.
4. **State the correction**.
5. **Examine transfer**.

Unfinished work is classified as **mock-blocking**, **useful after the mock** or **defer**. Confidence and safety consequence determine priority.

## Practical application

Use a maximum 30-minute protocol:

- check attention and choose recovery-only when needed;
- complete ten minutes of closed-note retrieval;
- correct one priority misconception;
- create one original one-page readiness sheet;
- record a Day 27 readiness decision and stop.

The readiness sheet may contain reasoning prompts, evidence categories, stop conditions and source-check reminders. It must not reproduce official tables, clause wording, test sequences, values or acceptance criteria.

## Assessment relevance

This block prepares the learner to enter the full mock with usable retrieval strength rather than recent rereading familiarity. It reinforces evidence classification, test purpose, sequence dependencies, result interpretation, fault-analysis boundaries, confidence calibration and safe uncertainty.

## Safety and review boundary

Day 26 is non-safety-critical as a recovery workflow and is not independently technically reviewed. Any technical answer retrieved from Days 22–25 retains the originating module’s `review-required` and `reference_check_required` status.

The block does not authorise inspection, operation, isolation, energisation, electrical testing, reset, repair, alteration or certification.
