## Core concept

A verification test is useful only when it answers a defined safety or performance question for a known subject and installation state. Test names, readings and completed forms are not self-interpreting evidence.

The **P-U-R-P-O-S-E** workflow is:

1. **Pin down the subject**.
2. **Understand the hazard or property**.
3. **Retrieve the authorised requirement**.
4. **Prove the preconditions**.
5. **Obtain evidence by the approved method**.
6. **Scrutinise context and conflicts**.
7. **Express a bounded conclusion**.

## Practical application

Use a purpose matrix to separate:

- the property that must be demonstrated;
- the evidence family relevant to that property;
- source-state and equipment constraints;
- current authorised method and criterion ownership;
- limitations that prevent a complete conclusion.

The learner should identify invalid substitutions, such as treating continuity as proof of polarity or operation as proof of complete safety.

## Assessment relevance

This block prepares the learner to explain why mandatory tests exist, what distinct evidence each is intended to provide and why results must be interpreted in context. It provides the conceptual foundation for Day 24, where sequence and result interpretation are considered without inventing field procedures.

## Safety and review boundary

This note does not provide an official mandatory-test list, test sequence, instrument setup, connection method, test parameter, acceptance value, energisation instruction or certification procedure. Current authorised sources, approved RTO or workplace procedures and qualified technical review are required.
