# 12-Week Day 74 - Fault Diagnosis, Correction Reasoning and Re-Verification Planning

## Purpose

Build a traceable diagnostic-and-recovery brief that keeps evidence, hypotheses, correction objectives, changes and re-verification needs distinct without implying practical authority or qualified acceptance.

## Learning module

- [Day 74 — Fault Diagnosis, Correction Reasoning and Re-Verification Planning](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-74-fault-diagnosis-correction-reasoning-and-re-verification-planning.md)

## Core method

Use **R-E-C-O-V-E-R-Y**: Record nine boundaries and literal evidence; Establish six evidence states; Construct competing hypotheses; Order evidence by discrimination; Verify the correction objective; Evaluate change impact; Reopen and route dependencies after every change; Yield bounded conclusions, evidence owners and recheck triggers.

## Core concepts

- **Nine boundaries:** installation, equipment, circuit, source, operating state, time, evidence, change and authority.
- **Six evidence states:** stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction and evidence gap.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** an expected observation capable of being contradicted.
- **Discriminating evidence:** evidence that changes relative support between competing hypotheses.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest unsupported link in a claim chain; dependent conclusions remain unsupported.
- **Correction objective:** the condition a change is intended to restore or establish, stated without assuming a field method.
- **Correction claim:** evidence that a change was asserted, not proof of root cause or verified outcome.
- **Change impact:** affected identities, states, design claims, records and dependencies.
- **Evidence owner:** the authorised source responsible for resolving a blocker.
- **Recheck trigger:** the specific evidence event that permits reconsideration.
- **Non-compensatory blocker:** a weakness that cannot be averaged away by stronger work elsewhere.
- **Educational readiness states:** `secure`, `developing`, `unsupported` and `stop-required`; these are not official grades or technical findings.

## Practical applications

Prepare a fictional diagnostic recovery pack that:

- defines all nine boundaries;
- preserves literal symptoms, observations and records;
- classifies all six evidence states;
- compares at least three materially distinct hypotheses using falsifiable predictions;
- stops each claim chain at its first unsupported transition;
- defines a correction objective without guessing a field method;
- propagates two sequential material changes through affected evidence;
- assigns every blocker an evidence owner and recheck trigger;
- records confidence separately from evidence quality; and
- ends with a bounded handover for qualified review.

## Assessment relevance

Assess boundary control, evidence-state control, hypothesis control, correction-objective control, change and re-verification control, and safety and conclusion control independently. Any `stop-required` state, or an `unsupported` non-compensatory blocker, prevents progression regardless of stronger performance elsewhere.

## Blocking conditions

Progress is blocked by invented or silently reconciled evidence, hidden assumptions or contradictions, unsupported identity or source-state transfer, reasoning beyond the first unsupported transition, stale records after material changes, correction claims treated as successful re-verification, confidence treated as proof, unauthorised practical directions or unsupported compliance, acceptance, certification, competency or technical-approval claims.

## Safety and references

This note authorises no site access, opening, switching, isolation, proving de-energised, testing, measurement, instrument use, alteration, repair, energisation, commissioning, acceptance, certification, verification or field fault finding.
