# 12-Week Day 80 - Staged Design and Calculation Mock Assessment

## Purpose

Practise bounded design reasoning, transparent calculation chains, competing interpretations, unsupported-transition control and sequential change propagation in an original staged mock.

## Practical applications

- defining installation, circuit, load, source-state, route, time, evidence, authority and requested-decision boundaries;
- distinguishing evidenced, derived, assumed, contradictory, missing and review-dependent inputs;
- recording method purpose, units, intermediate steps and dependency links;
- comparing materially different interpretations where records conflict;
- stopping dependent conclusions at the first unsupported transition;
- mapping interacting design gates without averaging away blockers;
- reopening affected work after sequential material changes; and
- assigning evidence owners and recheck triggers.

## Assessment relevance

The mock provides educational evidence of independent design reasoning, calculation reproducibility, limitation control and change propagation. Each criterion is reviewed independently as `secure`, `developing`, `unsupported` or `stop-required`; these are not official grades or competency decisions.

## References and review boundary

Exact design criteria, source values, methods, tables, acceptance requirements, assessment conditions and role permissions require current authorised sources and qualified review. The fictional exercise must not be used as field instruction and does not approve a design.
