# 12-Week Day 54 - Rest, Retrieval and Applicability-Check Repair

## Purpose

Recover deliberately, retrieve Week 8 reasoning without notes, classify evidence and error causes, repair no more than three blocking root misconceptions and demonstrate transfer before the mixed scenario workshop.

## Learning module

- [Day 54 — Rest, Retrieval and Applicability-Check Repair](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-54-rest-retrieval-and-applicability-check-repair.md)

## Core method

Use **R-E-S-T-O-R-E**:

1. reduce load and set a 20–30 minute boundary;
2. elicit from memory and record confidence separately;
3. sort evidence states and root-versus-symptom errors;
4. trace each claim to its first unsupported transition;
5. open the smallest useful source;
6. re-attempt after two material changes; and
7. evaluate each readiness criterion independently, assigning owners and recheck triggers where needed.

## Core concepts

- **Applicability check:** confirms that a source, definition, classification and requirement fit the scenario.
- **Root error:** earliest misconception producing downstream mistakes.
- **Symptom error:** later wrong conclusion caused by a root error.
- **First unsupported transition:** earliest claim step without adequate evidence.
- **Provenance:** evidence source, date or revision and scenario linkage.
- **Competing interpretations:** plausible readings retained until resolved.
- **Evidence owner:** authorised source, person or reviewer responsible for a gap.
- **Recheck trigger:** evidence or change requiring the reasoning to reopen.
- **Material change:** condition capable of changing classification, source applicability, protection, isolation or acceptance reasoning.

Evidence states are **stated fact**, **derived fact**, **supported inference**, **assumption**, **contradiction** and **evidence gap**. Confidence is recorded separately as guessing, unsure, reasonably confident or certain.

## Error categories

- applicability-check failure;
- false transfer;
- source omission;
- boundary error;
- evidence-grade error;
- terminology error; and
- fatigue-related performance decline.

A high-confidence error receives priority because it is likely to recur without self-correction. Repair root causes before downstream symptoms.

## Practical applications

- complete an eight-minute closed-note retrieval;
- classify evidence before judging conclusions;
- distinguish root errors from symptoms;
- stop at the first unsupported transition;
- repair no more than three blocking or high-confidence root errors;
- apply two material changes to each repair; and
- record evidence owners, recheck triggers and criterion-level readiness.

## Assessment relevance

Evaluate retrieval and confidence calibration, evidence discipline, causal repair, unsupported-transition control, transfer and recovery discipline independently.

Use **secure**, **developing**, **unsupported** and `stop-required` only as educational planning states. They are not official grades, competency decisions, defect classifications, compliance decisions or technical approvals.

## Blocking conditions

Progression is blocked by an omitted disclosed source or material condition, an assumption presented as fact, a hidden contradiction, reasoning beyond the first unsupported transition, a safety-critical claim from memory, cosmetic-only transfer, missing ownership for an unresolved blocker, unauthorised practical verification or continued study after fatigue prevents careful reasoning.
