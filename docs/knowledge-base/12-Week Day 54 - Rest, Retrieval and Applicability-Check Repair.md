# 12-Week Day 54 - Rest, Retrieval and Applicability-Check Repair

## Purpose

Recover deliberately, retrieve Week 8 reasoning without notes, identify the cause of errors and repair no more than three blocking or high-confidence misconceptions before the mixed scenario workshop.

## Recovery workflow

Use **R-E-S-T-O-R-E**:

- **R** — Reduce load.
- **E** — Elicit from memory.
- **S** — Sort errors.
- **T** — Target three or fewer.
- **O** — Open the smallest source.
- **R** — Re-attempt in variation.
- **E** — Evaluate readiness.

## Error categories

- applicability-check failure;
- false transfer;
- source omission;
- boundary error;
- evidence-grade error;
- terminology error; and
- fatigue-related performance decline.

A high-confidence error receives priority because it is more likely to be repeated without self-correction.

## Practical application

Complete an eight-minute closed-note retrieval, mark confidence, classify every error, repair no more than three, answer one changed-context question for each repair and stop at the time limit.

## Readiness decision

Choose one:

- **ready**;
- **ready with one support**; or
- **not ready—repair first**.

Transfer, not rereading volume, is the evidence of repair.

## Safety and recovery boundary

This note adds no new electrical theory and authorises no practical work. Stop if fatigue, frustration, unavailable authorised material or lack of supervision prevents careful reasoning.
