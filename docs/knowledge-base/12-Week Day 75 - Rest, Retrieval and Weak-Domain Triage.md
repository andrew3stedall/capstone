# 12-Week Day 75 - Rest, Retrieval and Weak-Domain Triage

## Purpose

Protect recovery while using a bounded closed-note sample to identify root weaknesses, repair no more than three high-impact items and record readiness without claiming competency.

## Learning module

- [Day 75 — Rest, Retrieval and Weak-Domain Triage](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-75-rest-retrieval-and-weak-domain-triage.md)

## Core method

Use **T-R-I-A-G-E**: Time-box and check fatigue; Retrieve before reviewing; Identify mechanisms and evidence states; Arrange roots before symptoms; Give up to three minimal repairs; End, escalate and record readiness.

## Core concepts

- **Root weakness:** the earliest misconception or process failure generating downstream errors.
- **Symptom error:** a dependent mistake caused by a root weakness.
- **Non-compensatory blocker:** a safety, identity, source-state, authority or evidence weakness that cannot be averaged away.
- **Repair dose:** the smallest existing explanation plus a fresh changed-context retrieval task.
- **Evidence owner:** the authorised person or source responsible for resolving an open blocker.
- **Recheck trigger:** the evidence event that permits reconsideration.
- **Confidence calibration:** recording confidence separately from correctness and evidence quality.
- **Educational readiness states:** `secure`, `developing`, `unsupported` and `stop-required`; these are not official assessment grades.
- **Recovery boundary:** 30 minutes maximum when steady, 15 minutes when reduced, and immediate stopping when depleted or worsening.

## Practical applications

Prepare a fictional weak-domain triage sheet that:

- preserves literal responses before correction;
- records correctness, confidence and evidence quality separately;
- classifies knowledge, evidence-reading, sequencing, confidence, transcription and fatigue mechanisms;
- links symptom errors to root weaknesses;
- ranks repairs by safety consequence, recurrence and prerequisite reach;
- applies no more than three minimal repair doses;
- uses changed-context transfer checks;
- assigns owners and triggers to unresolved blockers; and
- ends with a bounded Day 76 readiness note.

## Assessment relevance

Assess recovery control, retrieval honesty, mechanism diagnosis, prioritisation, repair quality and readiness boundaries independently. Any `stop-required` state, or an `unsupported` non-compensatory blocker, prevents progression regardless of stronger performance elsewhere.

## Blocking conditions

Progress is blocked by depleted energy, worsening errors, invented exactness, hidden source-state or authority assumptions, confidence treated as proof, symptom-only repair, broad rereading, new theory, missing changed-context checks, unresolved safety-critical misconceptions or unsupported competency and technical-approval claims.
