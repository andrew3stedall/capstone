# Six-Week Day 20 - Complete Cable-Selection Decision Sequence

## Purpose
Integrate load, protection, conductor capacity, route conditions, voltage drop, terminals and equipment constraints into one traceable decision sequence without mistaking a plausible size for a supported selection.

## Core workflow
**S-E-L-E-C-T-I-O-N:** Scope → Establish load → Link roles → Evaluate conditions → Check protection → Test voltage drop → Inspect constraints → Observe interactions → Note bounded conclusion.

## Evidence and claim model
- Evidence grades: **supplied**, **corroborated**, **derived**, **assumed**, **missing or conflicting**.
- Claim grades: **observation**, **provisional reasoning**, **supported paper conclusion**, **authorised technical determination**.
- Selection ledger: claim or gate → evidence grade → dependencies → result → reopening trigger.
- A cable size, margin or passing calculation cannot substitute for another unresolved critical gate.

## Practical application
Build a one-page selection ledger, compare two fictional candidates, assign evidence and claim grades, and trace how changed load, device, grouping, route length or terminals reopen earlier gates.

## Assessment relevance
The learner must preserve evidence traceability across the full selection chain, identify critical dependencies, reopen stale conclusions and state the strongest bounded claim. The 12-point rubric and critical-error gates are educational, not an official RTO threshold.
