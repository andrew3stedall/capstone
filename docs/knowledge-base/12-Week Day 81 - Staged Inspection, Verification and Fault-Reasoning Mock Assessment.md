# 12-Week Day 81 - Staged Inspection, Verification and Fault-Reasoning Mock Assessment

## Purpose

Practise bounded interpretation of fictional inspection observations, verification records and staged fault evidence while preserving evidence identity, competing hypotheses, first-unsupported-transition control and sequential change reopening.

## Practical applications

- defining installation, equipment, circuit, source-state, operating-state, time, evidence, authority and requested-decision boundaries;
- separating observations and recorded results from interpretations, hypotheses and conclusions;
- checking evidence identity, provenance, currency, scope, applicability and limitation;
- maintaining three materially different hypotheses with falsifiable predictions;
- stopping dependent reasoning at the first unsupported transition;
- preserving contradiction and confidence snapshots;
- reopening affected evidence after two sequential material changes; and
- assigning evidence owners and recheck triggers without prescribing practical work.

## Assessment relevance

The original staged mock provides educational evidence of inspection-record interpretation, hypothesis control, limitation handling and change propagation. Each capability is reviewed independently as `secure`, `developing`, `unsupported` or `stop-required`. These are not official grades, competency decisions, technical findings or approvals.

## References and review boundary

Exact inspection, verification, diagnostic, correction, re-verification and acceptance duties, procedures, sequences, instrument requirements, values, criteria, assessment conditions and role permissions require current authorised sources and qualified review. The module authorises no practical work and does not establish compliance or successful correction.
