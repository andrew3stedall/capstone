# Block 11 - Overload and Short-Circuit Concepts

## Core idea
An overload-like condition retains the intended path while demand or operating conditions produce excessive current. A short-circuit-like condition introduces an unintended low-impedance path. Exact definitions and values require authorised verification.

## Retrieval prompts
- What changed from the normal path?
- Is the current using the intended or an unintended route?
- Which facts are observed and which causes are inferred?
- Why must device response not be predicted from a generic sketch?

## Safety boundary
Do not reproduce abnormal-current conditions outside approved, supervised training arrangements.
