# Block 33 — Protection and Conductor Coordination

## Core idea
Coordination requires a traceable relationship among load, conductor, protective device, fault conditions, equipment and installation evidence. A device rating alone is not a complete proof.

## Retrieval prompts
- What load and conductor evidence is confirmed?
- Which protective functions are required?
- How are overload and fault-current checks kept distinct?
- Which missing evidence prevents a full coordination claim?

## Evidence habit
Use a coordination record that separates verified facts, authorised data, assumptions and unresolved contradictions.

## Safety boundary
This note provides no device curves, ratings, fault-current values, disconnection times or compliant combinations. Qualified review against current authorised sources is required.
