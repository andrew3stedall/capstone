# Block 48 - Cumulative Diagnostic Case

## Key idea
A diagnostic conclusion is only as strong as its evidence inventory, competing hypotheses, stop-gate discipline, traceability and bounded communication.

## Retrieval prompts
- What are the eight stages of the cumulative case workflow?
- Why include a non-fault hypothesis?
- What distinguishes discriminating evidence from merely relevant evidence?

## Application cue
Before concluding, identify the strongest disconfirming item, the most consequential assumption and any scope or state mismatch.

## Safety boundary
This is a fictional documentary exercise. It does not authorise access, testing, isolation, energised work, dismantling or repair.
