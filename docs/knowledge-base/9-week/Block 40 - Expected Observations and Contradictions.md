# Block 40 - Expected Observations and Contradictions

## Core idea
Write a bounded expectation before reviewing evidence. Compare it with the observation as agreement, partial agreement, contradiction or indeterminate, then choose authorised discriminating evidence without converting expectation into proof.

## Retrieval prompts
- Why should an expectation be recorded before the result is reviewed?
- What is the difference between a contradiction and a coverage gap?
- What makes evidence discriminating rather than merely additional?

## Safety boundary
This note supplies no prescribed observation, value, acceptance criterion or field action. Exact expectations and responses require current authorised sources and qualified review.
