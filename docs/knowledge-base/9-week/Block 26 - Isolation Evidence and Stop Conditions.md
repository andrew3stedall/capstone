# Block 26 — Isolation Evidence and Stop Conditions

## Core idea
A control action or indication is not, by itself, an isolation conclusion. The conclusion must match a defined boundary and be supported by complete, consistent and appropriately independent evidence.

## Retrieval prompts
- What is the difference between an indication and independent evidence?
- Why must the source-state map be carried forward?
- What kinds of contradiction require stopping?
- How should the strongest justified conclusion be worded?

## Evidence habit
Sort evidence by channel: control action, indication, documentation and authorised verification. Repeated evidence from one channel does not automatically strengthen another.

## Safety boundary
This note does not prescribe a safe-isolation sequence, instrument, proving method or discharge procedure. Those require current authorised information and qualified supervision.
