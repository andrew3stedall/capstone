# 12-Week Day 61 - Rest, Retrieval and Sequence Reconstruction

## Purpose

Provide a bounded, paper-only recovery method for reconstructing Week 9 verification-planning dependencies, correcting a maximum of three root errors and preserving energy for later learning. The note adds no new electrical theory and authorises no practical activity.

## Learning module

- [Day 61 — Rest, Retrieval and Sequence Reconstruction](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-61-rest-retrieval-and-sequence-reconstruction.md)

## Core method

Use **R-E-B-U-I-L-D** to recover and bound the session, elicit learning before checking, build dependency reasons, uncover evidence states, identify the first unsupported transition, limit and link repairs, then double-transfer, decide and stop.

## Core concepts

- **Closed-note retrieval:** recall before source consultation.
- **Dependency reconstruction:** rebuilding a sequence through explicit “depends on because” reasons.
- **Root error:** the earliest misconception or gap causing downstream symptom errors.
- **Evidence states:** stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction and evidence gap.
- **Confidence calibration:** low, medium or high confidence recorded separately from correctness and evidence quality.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest unsupported arrow in a claim chain.
- **Evidence owner:** authorised source or qualified person responsible for resolving a blocker.
- **Recheck trigger:** new evidence or material change requiring dependent reasoning to reopen.
- **Educational states:** secure, developing, unsupported and `stop-required`; these are not official grades or technical decisions.

## Practical applications

Using fictional cards and records within a 15–30 minute limit:

- retrieve workflows and terms before checking;
- record confidence separately;
- reconstruct dependency reasons;
- classify claims by evidence state;
- stop at the first unsupported transition;
- repair no more than three root or high-confidence errors;
- assign owners and triggers to unresolved blockers; and
- reopen affected dependencies after two sequential material changes.

## Assessment relevance

Evaluate recovery control, closed-note retrieval, dependency reconstruction, evidence and confidence control, root-error repair, ownership and transfer, and readiness communication independently. A blocking unsupported or `stop-required` criterion cannot be offset by stronger work elsewhere.

## Blocking conditions

Progress is blocked by fatigue, repeated guessing, practical drift, memorised order without reasons, concealed contradictions, reasoning beyond the first unsupported transition, unresolved blockers without owners or triggers, or unsupported competency, verification, compliance or technical-approval claims.
