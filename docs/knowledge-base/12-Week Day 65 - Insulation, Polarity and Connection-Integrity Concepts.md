# 12-Week Day 65 - Insulation, Polarity and Connection-Integrity Concepts

## Purpose

Provide a document-only method for separating insulation, polarity and connection-integrity evidence while controlling nine boundaries, six evidence states, confidence, provenance, dependencies, competing interpretations, ownership and change propagation.

## Learning module

- [Day 65 — Insulation, Polarity and Connection-Integrity Concepts](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-65-insulation-polarity-and-connection-integrity-concepts.md)

## Core method

Use **S-E-P-A-R-A-T-E**: State the bounded question; Establish all boundaries; Preserve the source record; Assess provenance and applicability; Review dependencies and contradictions; Avoid false transfer; Track ownership and triggers; Express a bounded conclusion.

## Core concepts

- **Distinct questions:** insulation integrity, polarity and connection integrity answer different evidence questions.
- **Nine boundaries:** installation, circuit, conductor, endpoint, state, time, evidence, authority and decision.
- **Evidence states:** stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction and evidence gap.
- **Confidence calibration:** confidence is recorded separately from correctness and evidence quality.
- **False transfer:** using one evidence type to answer a different question automatically.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest unsupported step in a claim chain; downstream claims remain unsupported.
- **Competing interpretations:** plausible explanations retained until evidence resolves them.
- **Evidence owner:** authorised source, custodian or qualified person responsible for resolving a blocker.
- **Recheck trigger:** a new record or material change that reopens affected reasoning.
- **Educational readiness states:** secure, developing, unsupported and `stop-required`; not official grades or technical decisions.

## Practical applications

For a fictional evidence pack:

- define the bounded question and all nine boundaries;
- preserve literal records before interpretation;
- classify claims and record confidence separately;
- check identity, provenance, date, version, exclusions and alterations;
- retain competing interpretations;
- stop at the first unsupported transition;
- assign evidence owners and recheck triggers;
- reopen dependencies after two sequential material changes; and
- communicate a bounded conclusion without pass/fail, compliance, acceptance or certification claims.

## Assessment relevance

Assess evidence-question separation, boundary control, evidence classification, confidence calibration, provenance, dependency reasoning, interpretation limits, ownership, two-change transfer and safety communication independently. There is no aggregate score. An unsupported blocking criterion or any `stop-required` state cannot be offset by stronger work elsewhere.

## Blocking conditions

Progress is blocked by invented or altered evidence, concealed contradictions, mixed evidence questions, historical records treated as current without applicability evidence, continued reasoning beyond the first unsupported transition, unresolved blockers without owners or triggers, ignored material changes, practical testing directions, or unsupported safety, compliance, acceptance, certification or technical-approval claims.
