# 12-Week Day 28 - Week 4 Independent Circuit-Design Checkpoint

## Purpose

Tests whether the learner can coordinate Week 4 circuit-design reasoning independently while controlling evidence provenance, contradictions, changed conditions, claim boundaries and remediation.

## Core concepts

- **Design boundary:** the equipment, circuit extent, route, conditions and decisions included in the fictional task.
- **Evidence provenance:** the source, identity and revision attached to an evidence item.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest point where a claim extends beyond its supporting evidence.
- **Competing interpretations:** plausible readings retained while evidence is insufficient to choose between them.
- **Evidence owner:** the authorised person or source responsible for resolving a gap or contradiction.
- **Recheck trigger:** an event that requires affected reasoning to be reopened.
- **Blocking condition:** a safety, authority, contradiction or evidence failure that prevents progression.
- **Criterion-level state:** secure, developing, unsupported or `stop-required` for one observable capability.
- **Bounded conclusion:** a statement separating what is supported, unresolved and outside authority.
- **Remediation prescription:** one targeted repair tied to observed evidence.

## Evidence labels

Use these labels before relying on an item:

1. stated fact;
2. derived fact;
3. supported inference;
4. assumption;
5. contradiction; and
6. evidence gap.

An assumption or unresolved contradiction cannot support approval, compliance or a strengthened downstream conclusion.

## C-H-E-C-K-P-O-I-N-T workflow

Clarify the boundary; harvest and label evidence; establish load and operating cases; connect current, device and conductor roles; keep route sections distinct; preserve unresolved gates; observe claim boundaries and changes; issue a bounded conclusion; note criterion states and blockers; target remediation and recheck triggers.

## Practical application

Learners complete a fictional independent design response containing conflicting equipment, operating-control and route records. They must:

- retain competing interpretations;
- locate the first unsupported transition;
- propagate two changed material conditions;
- grade ten criteria independently; and
- assign an evidence owner, remediation action and recheck trigger.

The task does not use an aggregate pass score. Strong performance in one criterion cannot cancel an invented technical value, concealed contradiction, authority breach or unresolved safety dependency.

## Assessment relevance

Observable evidence includes:

- scope control;
- source and revision traceability;
- evidence classification;
- contradiction handling;
- load-case construction;
- role separation;
- route segmentation;
- unresolved-gate control;
- changed-condition propagation;
- bounded conclusion writing; and
- evidence-led remediation.

Secure, developing, unsupported and `stop-required` are original educational planning states, not official assessment grades or competency decisions.

## Safety boundary

Desk-based educational work only. It is not design approval and authorises no inspection, switching, isolation, opening, proving, tracing, measurement, testing, disconnection, reconnection, installation, alteration, repair, energisation, commissioning, certification or verification.
