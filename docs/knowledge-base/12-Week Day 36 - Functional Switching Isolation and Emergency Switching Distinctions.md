# 12-Week Day 36 - Functional Switching Isolation and Emergency Switching Distinctions

## Purpose

Separates normal control, isolation and emergency-response purposes before equipment capability is considered.

## Module

- [Day 36 — Functional Switching, Isolation and Emergency Switching Distinctions](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-36-functional-switching-isolation-and-emergency-switching-distinctions.md)

## Core concepts

Functional switching; isolation; emergency switching; purpose statement; switching boundary; operating state; suitability evidence.

## Workflow

**P-U-R-P-O-S-E** identifies the required outcome and operating state, classifies the function, maps sources and verifies evidence before a bounded claim is made.

## Practical application and assessment relevance

Learners classify original scenarios, map source boundaries and explain what evidence is needed when one device is claimed to perform multiple functions. This is preparation only, not an official assessment or practical authority.

## Safety boundary

Written classification only. No switching or isolation procedure, device operation or field action is authorised.
