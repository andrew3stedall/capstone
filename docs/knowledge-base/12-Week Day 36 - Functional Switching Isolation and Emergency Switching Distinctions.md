# 12-Week Day 36 - Functional Switching Isolation and Emergency Switching Distinctions

## Purpose

Separates normal control, isolation and emergency-response purposes before equipment capability is considered, and limits dependent claims at the first unsupported transition.

## Core concepts

- **Functional switching:** start, stop or regulation for normal operation.
- **Isolation:** source separation intended to prevent danger within a defined boundary.
- **Emergency switching:** rapid switching intended to remove or reduce an unexpected danger.
- **Switching boundary:** the equipment, conductors, energy paths and sources included in the claimed function.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest unsupported step in a claim chain; downstream claims cannot be stronger than it.
- **Competing interpretation:** a plausible explanation retained while evidence remains contradictory or incomplete.
- **Evidence owner:** the authorised person or source responsible for resolving a gap.
- **Recheck trigger:** the information or condition that requires affected reasoning to be reopened.

## Workflow

**P-U-R-P-O-S-E**:

1. **Pinpoint** the hazard, requested outcome and decision purpose.
2. **Understand** the operating state and authority boundary.
3. **Resolve** the required function without relying on a device label.
4. **Plot** every relevant source, stored-energy dependency and boundary.
5. **Obtain** current authorised evidence for identity, capability and applicability.
6. **Separate** facts, inferences, assumptions, contradictions and gaps; assign owners and triggers.
7. **Express** a bounded conclusion and reopen dependent claims when upstream evidence changes.

## Evidence discipline

Classify each evidence item as a stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction or evidence gap. Retain competing interpretations when drawings, labels, maintenance records or source information disagree. A visible stop or familiar device name is not evidence that every relevant source is separated.

## Practical application

Learners classify original written scenarios, map source boundaries, document evidence provenance, identify the first unsupported transition and explain what evidence is needed when one arrangement is claimed to perform multiple functions. Transfer requires at least two changed material conditions and explicit reopening of affected reasoning.

## Assessment relevance

Criterion-level educational states are **secure**, **developing**, **unsupported** and `stop-required`; they are not official grades or competency decisions. Operational instructions, omitted possible sources, unsupported suitability claims and treating stopping as proof of isolation are blocking conditions.

## Safety boundary

Written classification and evidence reasoning only. No switching, isolation, opening, proving, measurement, testing, adjustment, installation, repair, energisation, commissioning, certification or field verification is authorised.
