# 12-Week Day 47 - Rest, Retrieval and Installation-Defect Correction

## Purpose

Consolidates [[12-Week Day 43 - Wiring-System Selection and Mechanical Protection]], [[12-Week Day 44 - Environmental Influences, Segregation and Support Concepts]], [[12-Week Day 45 - Consumer Mains, Submains and Final Subcircuits]] and [[12-Week Day 46 - Fixed Appliances and Local Isolation Reasoning]] without adding new electrical theory.

## Learning module

- [Day 47 — Rest, Retrieval and Installation-Defect Correction](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-47-rest-retrieval-and-installation-defect-correction.md)

## Core method

Use **R-E-C-O-V-E-R**:

1. rest before retrieval;
2. elicit closed-note answers and confidence;
3. classify evidence states, root errors and symptom errors;
4. order no more than three blocking or high-confidence root repairs;
5. verify the smallest current authorised explanation and record provenance;
6. exercise transfer across at least two material changes; and
7. reassess each readiness criterion independently and stop when required.

## Core concepts

- **Root error:** the earliest misconception or unsupported assumption causing later mistakes.
- **Symptom error:** a downstream error produced by a root error.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest step where a claim exceeds its evidence; dependent claims stop there.
- **Evidence owner:** the authorised person, source or reviewer responsible for resolving a gap.
- **Recheck trigger:** new evidence or changed conditions that require an earlier conclusion to reopen.
- **Material change:** a change capable of altering a source, route, environment, circuit boundary, device function or installation conclusion.
- **Readiness:** sufficient recall, transfer, concentration and self-monitoring to continue studying; not technical competence or field authority.

Classify evidence as stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction or evidence gap. Record confidence separately because fluent certainty and correct guessing are not secure evidence.

## Practical applications

- reconstruct Week 7 workflows closed-note;
- identify the first unsupported transition rather than repairing only a visible symptom;
- retain conflicting drawings, labels, photographs and service records as competing evidence;
- assign owners and triggers to unresolved blockers;
- reopen dependent conclusions after changed sources, routes, environments or control paths;
- complete two changed-condition transfer attempts; and
- stop at the recovery limit instead of extending catch-up indefinitely.

## Assessment relevance

Evaluate accurate recall, confidence calibration, causal error classification, provenance, contradiction handling, first-unsupported-transition control, evidence ownership, downstream reopening, two-condition transfer and fatigue management independently.

Use **secure**, **developing**, **unsupported** and `stop-required` as educational planning states. They are not official grades, competency outcomes, defect classifications, compliance decisions or technical approvals. No aggregate score or unofficial pass threshold applies.

## Blocking conditions

Readiness is blocked by an omitted credible source or environmental condition; treating “off,” proximity, a label or a drawing as proof; hidden assumptions or contradictions; failure to identify the first unsupported transition; missing evidence owners or recheck triggers; transfer using fewer than two material changes; incomplete reopening of affected conclusions; repeated root error after correction; fatigue; or unauthorised practical activity.
