# 12-Week Day 66 - Fault-Loop and RCD Result Interpretation at Concept Level

## Purpose

Provide a document-only method for interpreting existing fault-loop and RCD evidence while keeping their protective questions separate and controlling nine boundaries, six evidence states, confidence, provenance, dependencies, competing interpretations, ownership and change propagation.

## Learning module

- [Day 66 — Fault-Loop and RCD Result Interpretation at Concept Level](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-66-fault-loop-and-rcd-result-interpretation-at-concept-level.md)

## Core method

Use **P-R-O-T-E-C-T**: Pin down one protective question; Record identities and boundaries; Observe the literal record; Trace dependencies; Examine provenance and contradictions; Constrain interpretation and confidence; Track ownership, triggers and change propagation.

## Core concepts

- **Distinct questions:** fault-loop and RCD evidence address related but different protective questions.
- **Nine boundaries:** installation, circuit, protective device, source, operating state, time, evidence, authority and decision.
- **Evidence states:** stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction and evidence gap.
- **Confidence calibration:** confidence is recorded separately from correctness and evidence quality.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest unsupported step in a claim chain; dependent claims remain unsupported.
- **Competing interpretations:** plausible explanations retained until evidence resolves them.
- **Evidence owner:** authorised source, custodian or qualified person responsible for resolving a blocker.
- **Recheck trigger:** a new record or material change that reopens affected reasoning.
- **Educational readiness states:** secure, developing, unsupported and `stop-required`; not official grades or technical decisions.

## Practical applications

For a fictional evidence pack:

- state separate fault-loop and RCD protective questions;
- define all nine boundaries;
- preserve literal records before interpretation;
- classify claims and record confidence separately;
- check identity, provenance, date, version, alterations and source state;
- retain competing interpretations;
- stop at the first unsupported transition;
- assign evidence owners and recheck triggers;
- reopen dependencies after two sequential material changes; and
- communicate a bounded conclusion without pass/fail, compliance, acceptance or certification claims.

## Assessment relevance

Assess question separation, boundary control, evidence classification, confidence calibration, provenance, dependency reasoning, interpretation limits, ownership, two-change transfer and safety communication independently. There is no aggregate score. An unsupported blocking criterion or any `stop-required` state cannot be offset by stronger work elsewhere.

## Blocking conditions

Progress is blocked by invented or altered evidence, concealed contradictions, merged protective questions, historical records treated as current without applicability evidence, continued reasoning beyond the first unsupported transition, unresolved blockers without owners or triggers, ignored material changes, practical testing directions, or unsupported safety, compliance, acceptance, certification or technical-approval claims.
