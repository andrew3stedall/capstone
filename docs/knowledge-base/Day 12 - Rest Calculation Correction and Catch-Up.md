# Day 12 - Rest Calculation Correction and Catch-Up

Day 12 is a bounded recovery and consolidation block for the Week 2 design sequence. It introduces no new electrical rule. The learner reconstructs the chain from maximum demand through cable selection, installation conditions and voltage-drop interpretation, then repairs one causal error without exceeding the recovery limit.

## R-E-P-A-I-R correction model

1. **Retain** the original attempt and confidence rating.
2. **Establish** the boundary and operating case.
3. **Pinpoint** the earliest boundary, evidence, method, unit/arithmetic or claim error.
4. **Access** the original module and applicable authorised evidence.
5. **Implement** the smallest complete correction and reopen dependent steps.
6. **Retest** with a varied question now and a closed-note check within 48–72 hours.

The model treats error correction as causal diagnosis. A later answer is not repaired while an earlier unsupported assumption remains active.

## Evidence and claim boundary

- Recovery activities are original learning design.
- Technical claims revisited from Days 8–11 inherit those modules' review status.
- Missing authorised data is recorded rather than guessed.
- A successful correction may end without a final number when the evidence gap is correctly exposed.
- No exercise result is a compliance approval or practical authorisation.

## Practical application

The 30-minute protocol contains:

- a two-minute capacity and stop check;
- eight minutes of closed-note dependency retrieval;
- one complete causal correction using R-E-P-A-I-R;
- one changed-context transfer question;
- a readiness statement and delayed-retest date.

Stop earlier when concentration is unreliable, the required authorised source is unavailable, a value would need to be invented, or the task would exceed competence, authority or safe study conditions.

## Assessment relevance

The 12-point rubric measures:

- ordered retrieval of the Week 2 dependency chain;
- identification of the earliest causal error;
- applicable evidence control;
- reopening of every dependent step;
- transfer to a varied scenario; and
- recovery discipline.

Critical errors override the score: invented values, fictional data treated as field evidence, concealed evidence gaps, unverified compliance claims or continued work under unreliable concentration.

## Misconceptions to track

- Correct arithmetic proves the design process is valid.
- The final wrong number is always the first thing to fix.
- Replacing a worksheet is better than preserving and auditing it.
- Convenient online values can substitute for applicable authorised evidence.
- A rest day should clear every unfinished calculation.
- Confidence strengthens an unsupported conclusion.
- A high rubric total cancels a critical error.
