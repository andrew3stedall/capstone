# Day 12 - Rest Calculation Correction and Catch-Up

Day 12 is a bounded recovery and consolidation block for the Week 2 design sequence. It introduces no new electrical rule. The learner retrieves the chain from maximum demand through cable capacity, installation conditions and voltage drop, then audits and completely corrects one high-priority error.

## Correction model

The block uses the **C-H-E-C-K** workflow:

1. **capture the attempt** before editing it;
2. **highlight the earliest unsupported step**;
3. **establish the governing evidence** from the original module and authorised source where required;
4. **correct and recalculate** while retaining units and an audit trail;
5. **keep a fresh test** that varies the context and checks the repaired reasoning.

The audit distinguishes input, method, arithmetic and interpretation errors. It begins with the scenario and evidence chain rather than the calculator result.

## Practical application

Complete the 30-minute protocol in the linked module:

- two-minute capacity check;
- ten-minute closed-note retrieval across Days 8–11;
- eight-minute audit of one prior worksheet;
- eight-minute targeted correction or readiness preparation;
- two-minute readiness statement for Day 13A.

Stop earlier when concentration is unreliable, the required authorised source is unavailable or the correction would exceed competence, authority or safe study conditions.

## Assessment relevance

The block checks whether the learner can:

- reconstruct the Week 2 design sequence without prompts;
- expose assumptions before calculation;
- locate the earliest error in a reasoning chain;
- distinguish an arithmetic mistake from an input, method or interpretation failure;
- preserve source traceability during correction;
- avoid treating fictional teaching values as compliance data;
- state a bounded conclusion and a defensible readiness decision.

## Misconceptions to track

- Correct arithmetic proves the whole design process is valid.
- The final wrong number is always the first thing to fix.
- Replacing a worksheet is better than preserving and auditing the original attempt.
- Convenient online values can substitute for authorised source data.
- A rest day should clear every unfinished calculation.
- Confidence strengthens an unsupported conclusion.
- Fictional worked values can be reused in field design.
