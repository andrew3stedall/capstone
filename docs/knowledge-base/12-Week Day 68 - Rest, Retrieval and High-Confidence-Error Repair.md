# 12-Week Day 68 - Rest, Retrieval and High-Confidence-Error Repair

## Purpose

Provide a bounded recovery method for retrieving Week 10 distinctions, separating confidence from correctness and evidence quality, identifying root misconceptions, repairing no more than three high-value errors and preserving enough attention for the staged-evidence scenario.

## Learning module

- [Day 68 — Rest, Retrieval and High-Confidence-Error Repair](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-68-rest-retrieval-and-high-confidence-error-repair.md)

## Core method

Use **R-E-P-A-I-R**: Retrieve before notes; Estimate confidence and classify evidence; Prioritise root, blocking and high-confidence errors; Analyse the first unsupported transition; Inspect an authorised study source; Rewrite, recheck once and record the owner and trigger.

## Core concepts

- **Confidence calibration:** confidence is recorded separately from correctness and evidence quality.
- **Root misconception:** the underlying mistaken distinction that produces several symptom errors.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest unsupported step in a claim chain; dependent claims remain unsupported.
- **Evidence states:** stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction and evidence gap.
- **Evidence owner:** authorised source, custodian or qualified person responsible for resolving a blocker.
- **Recheck trigger:** new evidence or a material change that reopens affected reasoning.
- **Recovery limits:** stop at three repairs, 30 minutes, the reduced 15-minute fatigue limit or an earlier stop condition.
- **Educational readiness states:** secure, developing, unsupported and `stop-required`; not official grades or technical decisions.

## Practical applications

Complete a three-card root-repair sheet that records the original answer, confidence, correctness, evidence state, linked symptom errors, root misconception, first unsupported transition, authorised source, corrected distinction, boundary, evidence owner, recheck trigger and retrieval cue.

Apply two sequential material changes and reopen every affected boundary and dependency after each change.

## Assessment relevance

Assess retrieval, confidence calibration, root-error control, evidence control, dependency control, recovery control, ownership and transfer independently. There is no aggregate score. An unsupported blocking criterion or any `stop-required` state cannot be offset by stronger work elsewhere.

## Blocking conditions

Progress is blocked by invented or altered evidence, hidden contradictions, confidence treated as proof, symptom-only correction, continued reasoning beyond the first unsupported transition, historical evidence treated as current without applicability, blockers without owners or triggers, ignored material changes, exceeded recovery limits, continued work under unreliable fatigue, practical troubleshooting directions, or unsupported safety, compliance, acceptance, certification or root-cause claims.
