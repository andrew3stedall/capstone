# 12-Week Day 60 - Instrument Suitability, Limitations and Pre-Use Evidence

## Purpose

A paper-based method for deciding whether equipment records support a specific verification-planning question. The note does not teach equipment operation or practical testing.

## Core model

Use **M-E-T-E-R-S**:

- **M**ap the question.
- **E**stablish context.
- **T**race authorised sources.
- **E**xamine records.
- **R**ecord limitations.
- **S**tate the bounded decision.

A complete record separates:

- identity evidence;
- condition evidence;
- capability evidence;
- currency evidence; and
- limitations or unresolved gaps.

Availability alone does not prove suitability. A displayed level of detail does not independently prove accuracy.

## Practical application

Use fictional records to build a complete-system inventory, classify evidence, identify limitations and write a suitable, unsuitable or unresolved paper decision. Reopen the decision whenever the question, context, associated components, configuration or source records change.

## Assessment relevance

A strong response:

1. starts with an exact evidence question;
2. describes the whole measurement system rather than one item;
3. separates evidence categories;
4. connects limitations to the conclusion;
5. identifies stop conditions; and
6. avoids practical authority or compliance claims.

## Safety and review boundary

This note authorises no access, switching, isolation, testing, measurement, equipment operation, alteration, repair, energisation, commissioning, certification or verification. Exact requirements require current authorised sources and qualified technical review.
