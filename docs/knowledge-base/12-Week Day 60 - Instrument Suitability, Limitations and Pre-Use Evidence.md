# 12-Week Day 60 - Instrument Suitability, Limitations and Pre-Use Evidence

## Purpose

Develop evidence-controlled paper reasoning for deciding whether complete measurement-system records support one precise verification-planning question. This note does not teach equipment operation, practical checks, connections, settings, testing or acceptance decisions.

## Learning module

- [Day 60 — Instrument Suitability, Limitations and Pre-Use Evidence](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-60-instrument-suitability-limitations-and-pre-use-evidence.md)

## Core method

Use **M-E-T-E-R-S** to map the question and boundaries, establish provenance and context, trace authorised sources, examine the complete-system record, restrict reasoning at the first unsupported transition, and state or reopen a bounded paper decision.

## Core concepts

- **Complete measurement system:** the main unit, associated components, configuration information and applicable records.
- **Evidence categories:** identity, condition, capability, currency and limitation evidence.
- **Evidence states:** stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction and evidence gap.
- **Confidence calibration:** low, medium or high confidence recorded separately from correctness and evidence quality.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest claim step without adequate applicable evidence.
- **Competing interpretation:** an alternative explanation retained until evidence resolves it.
- **Evidence owner:** the authorised source or qualified person responsible for resolving a blocker.
- **Recheck trigger:** new evidence or a material change requiring dependent reasoning to reopen.
- **Educational states:** secure, developing, unsupported and `stop-required`; these are not official grades or technical approvals.

Availability, familiarity, a status label and displayed resolution do not independently prove suitability or accuracy.

## Practical applications

Using fictional records:

- define the evidence question and all material boundaries;
- inventory the complete measurement system;
- record provenance, configuration and operating state;
- classify evidence and confidence separately;
- preserve contradictions and competing interpretations;
- stop at the first unsupported transition;
- assign evidence owners and recheck triggers; and
- reopen affected dependencies after two sequential material changes.

## Assessment relevance

Evaluate question and boundary control, complete-system inventory, provenance, evidence classification, suitability reasoning, limitations, ownership and change transfer independently. A material unsupported or `stop-required` criterion cannot be offset by stronger work elsewhere.

## Blocking conditions

Progress is blocked by practical direction, invented official content, unidentified associated components treated as suitable, stale or ambiguously applicable records treated as current, concealed contradictions, reasoning beyond the first unsupported transition, unresolved evidence ownership or unsupported compliance claims.
