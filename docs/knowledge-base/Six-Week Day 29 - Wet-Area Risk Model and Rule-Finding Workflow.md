# Six-Week Day 29 - Wet-Area Risk Model and Rule-Finding Workflow

## Purpose

Build a traceable wet-area study conclusion by connecting water sources, exposure pathways, verified geometry, authorised spatial classification, equipment evidence and protective measures without inventing technical limits.

## Core workflow

**W-A-T-E-R-S:** Write scenario and decision → Anchor sources, geometry and datum → Trace exposure pathways → Establish authorised classification → Retrieve equipment and protection evidence → State grades, gaps, reopening triggers and bounded action.

## Evidence and claim model

- Evidence grades: **recalled**, **located**, **supported**, **transferred**, **unresolved**.
- Claim grades: **memory claim**, **provisional interpretation**, **supported study conclusion**, **authorised technical determination**.
- Wet-area ledger: proposed claim → sources → pathways → geometry/datum → authorised query → equipment/protection → evidence grade → dependencies → reopening triggers → claim grade → bounded next action.
- A remembered diagram, marketing term, unscaled image or isolated ingress-protection code is not a complete suitability proof.

## Practical application

For bathroom-like, commercial hand-wash and outdoor washdown scenarios, complete two ledger rows, draft a precise authorised-source query, identify missing dependencies, perform one changed-condition transfer and state a bounded study conclusion.

## Assessment relevance

Tests observable special-location reasoning: source and pathway identification, observed-versus-inferred geometry, datum control, source navigation, equipment/protection interaction, evidence and claim grading, reopening and bounded reporting. The 12-point rubric and critical-error gates are original educational tools, not an official RTO pass mark.

## Reopening triggers

Reopen a conclusion when fixture geometry, water trajectory, datum, dimension, wall or screen, equipment, mounting, supply, protection, room use, washdown condition, jurisdiction or source currency changes or conflicts with later evidence.
