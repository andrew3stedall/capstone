# Six-Week Day 36 - Verification Purpose Evidence and Visual Inspection

## Purpose

Separate visual-inspection evidence, authorised test evidence and unresolved evidence before forming a traceable, bounded verification record.

## Core workflow

**V-E-R-I-F-Y:** Verify scope and source states → Establish authorised questions → Record evidence and grades → Identify dependencies and test boundaries → Form bounded claims → Yield and reopen when conditions change.

## Evidence and claim model

- Evidence grades: **stated**, **indicated**, **corroborated**, **transferred**, **unresolved**.
- Claim grades: **assumption**, **provisional educational conclusion**, **supported educational conclusion**, **authorised technical determination**.
- Verification ledger: scope item → observation or statement → inference → evidence grade → claim grade → traceability → dependency → evidence request → reopening trigger → bounded conclusion.
- Labels, schedules, photographs, drawings and normal operating responses may contribute evidence but do not independently prove testing, isolation, compliance, safety or certification.

## Practical application

Build a fictional visual-inspection record with a controlled scope, document register, graded observations and claims, traceability, evidence gaps, dependencies, reopening triggers, changed-document and changed-scope transfer, and a bounded conclusion.

## Assessment relevance

Tests observable performance in scope control, observation/inference separation, inspection-versus-test distinctions, evidence grading, traceability, selective reopening and refusal to overclaim certification. The six-category 12-point rubric and critical-error gates are original educational tools, not an official RTO pass mark.

## Reopening triggers

Reopen affected ledger rows when the work scope, exclusions, source state, circuit identity, equipment identity, document revision, evidence currency, authorised requirement, jurisdiction, competence or authority changes or conflicts with later evidence.
