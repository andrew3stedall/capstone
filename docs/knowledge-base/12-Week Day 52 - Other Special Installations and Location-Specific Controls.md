# 12-Week Day 52 - Other Special Installations and Location-Specific Controls

## Purpose

Extends [[12-Week Day 50 - Special-Location Method - Classify, Map Zones and Verify Sources]] and [[12-Week Day 51 - Bathrooms, Showers and Other Wet-Area Reasoning]] into evidence-controlled comparison across different special-installation condition families without transferring unverified requirements.

## Learning module

- [Day 52 — Other Special Installations and Location-Specific Controls](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-52-other-special-installations-and-location-specific-controls.md)

## Core method

Use **S-P-E-C-I-A-L**:

1. state installation, activity, user, supply, environment, evidence, authority and decision boundaries;
2. pinpoint dominant conditions from traceable evidence;
3. enumerate candidate control families without asserting exact requirements;
4. consult current authorised sources and test applicability;
5. identify general and location-specific interactions;
6. avoid false transfer by comparing material differences; and
7. limit conclusions at the first unsupported transition, with owners, triggers and downstream reopening.

## Core concepts

- **Dominant condition:** the supported feature that principally changes ordinary assumptions.
- **Control family:** a category of response whose exact application still requires verification.
- **Shared control:** a concept that appears across settings but remains scenario-specific in application.
- **Location-specific control:** a requirement triggered by verified classification, use, geometry, supply or operating condition.
- **False transfer:** moving a rule between superficially similar settings without verifying scope and differences.
- **Provenance:** source, date or revision and scenario linkage for evidence.
- **Competing interpretations:** plausible readings retained until evidence resolves them.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest claim step that exceeds evidence.
- **Evidence owner:** the authorised source, person or reviewer responsible for a gap.
- **Recheck trigger:** the evidence or material change that requires reconsideration.

Evidence states are stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction and evidence gap. Confidence is recorded separately.

## Practical applications

- compare temporary, agricultural and transportable fictional dossiers;
- define all eight boundaries;
- classify evidence and retain contradictions;
- distinguish control families from verified requirements;
- stop at the first unsupported transition;
- assign evidence owners and recheck triggers; and
- reopen affected claims after both access/equipment-record and supply-condition changes.

## Assessment relevance

Evaluate boundary completeness, provenance, evidence classification, confidence calibration, dominant-condition reasoning, source applicability, false-transfer control, contradiction handling, first-unsupported-transition discipline, ownership, change propagation and two-condition transfer independently.

Use **secure**, **developing**, **unsupported** and `stop-required` only as educational planning states. They are not official grades, competency outcomes, defect classifications, compliance decisions or technical approvals.

## Blocking conditions

Readiness is blocked by invented classifications or values, omitted credible conditions, hidden contradictions, control families presented as verified requirements, rule transfer without applicability checks, claims beyond the first unsupported transition, missing owners or triggers, incomplete downstream reopening, inadequate transfer or unauthorised practical activity.

## Safety and references

This note authorises no site classification, approach, measurement, opening, switching, isolation, proving de-energised, testing, installation, alteration, repair, energisation, commissioning, certification, design approval or field verification.

Exact classifications, dimensions, equipment limitations, protection requirements, source treatment, installation methods, exceptions and official assessment expectations require current authorised sources and qualified review.

- Current authorised AS/NZS 3000 material and amendments.
- Applicable manufacturer information and approved current records.
