# 12-Week Day 13 - Protection-Selection Evidence Workflow Using Original Scenarios

## Purpose

This note connects the Week 2 protection concepts to an original evidence-led selection workflow. Day 13 deliberately stops before device sizing or installation approval: the learner frames the hazard, classifies evidence, separates protection functions, identifies authorised source categories and writes a bounded conclusion.

## Core concepts

- **Protected interest:** the person, conductor, equipment, property or continuity objective that a protective measure is intended to safeguard.
- **Protection function:** the specific protective job being considered rather than the name of a device.
- **Selection criterion:** a condition that must be verified before a device or arrangement can be considered suitable.
- **Evidence class:** supplied fact, derived fact, assumption or missing evidence.
- **Evidence chain:** the traceable connection from scenario and applicable source through criterion and evidence to conclusion.
- **Applicability:** whether the source or requirement governs the actual installation context.
- **Completeness:** whether all material protection functions and boundary conditions have been considered.
- **Bounded conclusion:** a finding limited to what the evidence supports, with unresolved matters stated.
- **Reopening trigger:** a changed fact that requires the decision to be checked again.

## S-E-L-E-C-T workflow

1. **State** the scenario, hazard and protected interest.
2. **Extract** and classify supplied, derived, assumed and missing evidence.
3. **List** the separate protection and work-control functions.
4. **Establish** applicable authorised source categories.
5. **Check** criteria, interactions, conflicts and missing evidence.
6. **Tell** a bounded conclusion, reopening triggers and authority limits.

## Practical application

The learner completes original scenarios involving an altered load, changed supply arrangement and incomplete inspection record. Each response must include a protection-evidence record, an evidence matrix, a bounded conclusion, reopening triggers and rejection of an unsafe proposed action.

The workflow is evidence-first. A named device or rating label is not treated as proof of suitability, coverage, condition, correct operation, coordination or safe isolation.

## Assessment relevance

Observable evidence includes:

- accurate scenario framing;
- separation of facts, derivations, assumptions and gaps;
- distinction between overload, short-circuit, fault/disconnection, residual-current and work-control questions;
- mapping of functions to authorised source and criterion categories;
- supported, conditional and unresolved findings;
- recognition of changed conditions that reopen a decision; and
- compliance with safety and authority boundaries.

## Safety boundary

This is a written evidence-reasoning module. It authorises no selection for construction, switching, isolation, opening, proving, measurement, testing, resetting, fault creation, alteration, repair, energisation, commissioning, certification or verification. Exact clauses, values, characteristics, source conditions, test methods and jurisdiction-specific requirements remain `reference_check_required`.
