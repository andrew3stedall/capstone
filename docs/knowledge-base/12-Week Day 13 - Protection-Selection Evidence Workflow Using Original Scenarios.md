# 12-Week Day 13 - Protection-Selection Evidence Workflow Using Original Scenarios

## Purpose

This note connects the Week 2 protection concepts to an original evidence-led selection workflow. Day 13 deliberately stops before device sizing or installation approval: the learner frames the hazard, labels evidence, separates protection functions, identifies authorised source categories and evidence owners, locates the first unsupported transition and writes a bounded conclusion.

## Core concepts

- **Protected interest:** the person, conductor, equipment, property or continuity objective that a protective measure is intended to safeguard.
- **Protection function:** the specific protective job being considered rather than the name of a device.
- **Selection criterion:** a condition that must be verified before a device or arrangement can be considered suitable.
- **Evidence labels:** stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction and evidence gap.
- **Evidence owner:** the authorised person, record or source expected to resolve a named gap.
- **Evidence chain:** the traceable connection from scenario and applicable source through criterion and evidence to conclusion.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest step where reasoning moves from supported material to an unverified claim.
- **Applicability:** whether the source or requirement governs the actual installation context.
- **Completeness:** whether all material protection functions, interactions and boundary conditions have been considered.
- **Bounded conclusion:** a finding limited to what the evidence supports, with unresolved matters stated.
- **Recheck trigger:** a changed fact, source, condition or contradiction requiring the decision to be assessed again.
- **Blocking condition:** a safety, authority, contradiction or evidence failure that prevents progression regardless of strengths elsewhere.

## S-E-L-E-C-T workflow

1. **State** the circuit purpose, boundary, hazard and protected interest.
2. **Extract** and label facts, derivations, supported inferences, assumptions, contradictions and gaps.
3. **List** separate protection and work-control functions.
4. **Establish** applicable authorised source categories and evidence owners.
5. **Check** criteria, competing interpretations, conflicts and the first unsupported transition.
6. **Tell** a supported, conditional, unresolved or `stop-required` conclusion, recheck triggers and authority limits.

## Practical application

The learner completes original scenarios involving an altered load, changed supply arrangement and conflicting inspection records. Each response includes a protection-evidence record, an evidence matrix, a competing interpretation, the first unsupported transition, evidence owners, a bounded conclusion, recheck triggers and rejection of an unsafe proposed action.

The workflow is evidence-first. A named device or rating label is not proof of suitability, coverage, condition, coordination, correct operation or safe isolation. A contradiction cannot be averaged away by otherwise strong evidence.

## Assessment relevance

Observable evidence includes:

- accurate scenario and boundary framing;
- separation of facts, derivations, supported inferences, assumptions, contradictions and gaps;
- distinction between overload, short-circuit, fault/disconnection, residual-current, coordination and work-control questions;
- mapping of criteria to authorised source categories and evidence owners;
- identification of the first unsupported transition;
- supported, conditional, unresolved and `stop-required` findings;
- changed-context transfer using at least two material changes;
- recognition of recheck triggers; and
- compliance with safety and authority boundaries.

Performance is recorded by criterion as **secure**, **developing**, **unsupported** or **`stop-required`**, not as an aggregate score. A `stop-required` result blocks progression, and strong performance elsewhere cannot cancel a material safety, authority, contradiction or evidence failure.

## Safety boundary

This is a written evidence-reasoning module. It authorises no selection for construction, switching, isolation, opening, proving, measurement, testing, resetting, fault creation, alteration, repair, energisation, commissioning, certification or verification. Exact clauses, values, characteristics, source conditions, test methods and jurisdiction-specific requirements remain `reference_check_required`.
