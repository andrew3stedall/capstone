## Purpose

## Core method

Use **U-N-I-F-Y**:

1. unpack installation, source, switchboard, evidence, authority and decision boundaries;
2. name all evidenced sources, operating states and device functions;
3. integrate evidence-state classification, provenance, contradictions and authorised rule-finding;
4. follow dependencies and stop at the first unsupported transition; and
5. yield a bounded result with evidence owners, recheck triggers, escalation and prohibited actions.

## Core concepts

- **Operating-state matrix:** explicit map of source availability, device positions and relevant equipment states.
- **Evidence provenance:** origin, date, described condition and applicability of an evidence item.
- **Stated fact:** information explicitly supplied by a traceable source.
- **Derived fact:** transparent result from supported facts.
- **Supported inference:** bounded interpretation with adequate evidence.
- **Assumption:** unverified proposition not to be presented as fact.
- **Contradiction:** evidence items that cannot both describe the same condition.
- **Evidence gap:** missing support required for a stronger claim.
- **Competing interpretations:** plausible explanations retained until evidence resolves them.
- **First unsupported transition:** earliest point where a claim exceeds its support.
- **Dependency register:** record linking conclusions to the facts, states and interpretations they rely on.
- **Evidence owner:** person, document set or authorised source expected to resolve a gap.
- **Recheck trigger:** new evidence or changed condition requiring earlier reasoning to reopen.
- **Bounded conclusion:** result limited to stated evidence, conditions, authority and unresolved constraints.

## Practical applications

- build a source inventory and operating-state matrix;
- classify device functions without relying on appearance or labels alone;
- map switchboard functional areas without claiming hidden construction;
- separate observations, inferences, assumptions, contradictions and gaps;
- stop dependent claims at the first unsupported transition;
- assign evidence owners and recheck triggers;
- propagate at least two material scenario changes; and
- write a bounded checkpoint result and remediation plan.

## Assessment relevance

Evaluate boundary control, source/state completeness, evidence provenance, function and board reasoning, claim-chain control, change propagation, safety communication and escalation independently.

Use **secure**, **developing**, **unsupported** and `stop-required` as educational planning states. They are not official assessment grades, competency outcomes, defect classifications, compliance decisions or technical approvals. No aggregate score or unofficial pass threshold applies.

## Blocking conditions

Secure readiness is blocked by:

- omitted source paths;
- unsupported complete-isolation claims;
- observations or gaps promoted to verified defects;
- invented hidden construction, connections, ratings or capabilities;
- concealed contradictions;
- failure to stop at the first unsupported transition;
- missing evidence owners or recheck triggers;
- failure to reopen dependencies after change;
- transfer using fewer than two material changes; or
- unauthorised practical activity.
