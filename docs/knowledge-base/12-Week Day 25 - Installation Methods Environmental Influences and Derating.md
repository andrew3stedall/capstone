# 12-Week Day 25 - Installation Methods Environmental Influences and Derating

## Purpose

Supports evidence-based route segmentation, installation classification, source tracing and single application of verified influences without converting incomplete records into design approval.

## Core concepts

- **Route section:** a portion with materially consistent conditions for the check being performed.
- **Section boundary:** the point where a relevant condition changes enough to require separate evidence or assessment.
- **Environmental influence:** a surrounding condition relevant to selection, capacity, protection or durability.
- **Correction factor:** an authorised adjustment tied to a defined condition, source method and scope.
- **Factor provenance:** the traceable record of source, represented condition, applicability, included adjustments and calculation location.
- **Governing section:** the controlling section for one stated check after comparable and complete results are assessed.
- **Double application:** counting the same influence more than once.
- **Competing interpretation:** an alternative route or classification explanation retained while evidence remains incomplete or contradictory.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest point where the next conclusion is not adequately supported.
- **Recheck trigger:** a change that requires earlier classifications or calculations to be reopened.

## Evidence labels

Every material input or claim should be labelled as one of:

- stated fact;
- derived fact;
- supported inference;
- assumption;
- contradiction; or
- evidence gap.

A drawing description is evidence about a record. It is not automatic proof of the actual installation or an authorised classification.

## C-O-N-D-I-T-I-O-N-S workflow

Capture the route; outline sections; name evidence states; determine authorised classification candidates; identify applicable influences; trace source values; implement each adjustment once; order comparable results; note unresolved dependencies; state reopening triggers.

The workflow must stop at the first unsupported transition. A factor must not be applied merely because it appears in a worksheet or has a plausible label.

## Practical application

Learners:

- build a route-section register with evidence labels and boundary bases;
- audit factor provenance and included adjustments;
- retain competing interpretations when records conflict;
- assign evidence owners and recheck triggers;
- change at least two material scenario conditions and rebuild affected reasoning; and
- distinguish unresolved evidence from demonstrated unsuitability.

## Assessment relevance

Observable evidence includes:

- defensible route segmentation;
- separation of physical description from authorised classification;
- source and factor provenance;
- prevention of double application;
- identification of the first unsupported transition;
- governing-case reasoning bounded to a stated check;
- changed-context transfer; and
- explicit safety and authority limits.

Assessment uses criterion-level **secure**, **developing**, **unsupported** and `stop-required` states. There is no aggregate score, and strong work in one criterion cannot cancel a contradiction, evidence failure or safety breach elsewhere.

## Safety boundary

Written educational preparation only. No field access, switching, isolation, opening, proving, tracing, measurement, testing, disconnection, reconnection, installation, alteration, repair, energisation, commissioning, certification or verification is authorised.

Exact installation classifications, factors, capacity methods, applicability conditions and exceptions remain `reference_check_required`. Incomplete educational analysis must not be presented as approval, verification or compliance.
