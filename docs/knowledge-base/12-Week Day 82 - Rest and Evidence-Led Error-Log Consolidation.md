# 12-Week Day 82 - Rest and Evidence-Led Error-Log Consolidation

## Purpose

Use a bounded recovery block to retrieve Week 12 control workflows, trace visible error events to root mechanisms, repair no more than three consequential mechanisms and prepare an evidence-led Day 83 readiness note without adding new electrical theory.

## Core distinctions

- An **error event** is the visible wrong, incomplete or unsupported response.
- An **error mechanism** is the reasoning failure that produced the event.
- A **root error** is the first unsupported transition in a dependency chain.
- A **symptom error** is a later dependent error that may disappear only after the root is repaired.
- Correctness, confidence and evidence quality must be recorded separately.
- `secure`, `developing`, `unsupported` and `stop-required` are educational readiness states, not official grades or competency decisions.

## Practical applications

- preserving untouched Day 79–81 submissions as evidence;
- ranking mechanisms by safety consequence, confidence, dependency reach, recurrence and evidence condition;
- assigning an evidence owner and recheck trigger to unresolved items;
- repairing root mechanisms before downstream symptoms;
- checking transfer with materially varied scenarios;
- limiting remediation to 30 minutes, or 15 minutes under reduced load;
- recording unresolved blockers without concealing or averaging them away.

## Assessment relevance

This block supports recovery and evidence-led preparation for [[12-Week Day 83 - Full Integrated Mock Assessment]]. It improves the reliability of the learner's preparation record but does not add official assessment criteria, confirm technical correctness or establish competence.

## References and review boundary

Technical repairs must use current authorised evidence or remain `reference_check_required`. Exact requirements, values, procedures, acceptance criteria, permissions and official assessment conditions require qualified review. Fatigue, an unresolved safety-critical misconception, unavailable authorised evidence or authority limits require rest, escalation or qualified support rather than forced progression.

This note authorises no site access, switching, isolation, testing, measurement, instrument use, alteration, repair, energisation, commissioning, certification, verification or field fault finding.
