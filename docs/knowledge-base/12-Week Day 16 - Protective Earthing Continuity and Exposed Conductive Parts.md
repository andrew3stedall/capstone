# 12-Week Day 16 - Protective Earthing Continuity and Exposed Conductive Parts

## Purpose

This note supports written reasoning about exposed conductive parts and protective-earthing continuity. It separates presence, identity, connection, continuity, suitability and a verified protective outcome, and requires the learner to stop at the first unsupported transition.

## Core concepts

- **Protective-earthing continuity:** an evidence-dependent condition in which the required protective path is electrically continuous between relevant endpoints.
- **Exposed conductive part:** a touchable conductive equipment part that is not normally live but may become live under a fault; exact classification remains source-dependent.
- **Endpoint:** a named start or finish point for the path being claimed.
- **Intermediate link:** a stated termination, conductor, joint, terminal or bar between endpoints.
- **Described connection:** a connection shown or stated in a record, label or drawing; it does not automatically prove current physical condition.
- **Continuous path:** an electrically unbroken path between identified endpoints, established through authorised evidence.
- **Suitable protective path:** a continuous path whose arrangement and characteristics also satisfy applicable requirements.
- **Verified protective outcome:** a conclusion requiring the complete arrangement, path, interacting-protection and verification evidence.
- **Parallel path:** another conductive route that may affect a result or its interpretation.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest step where a claim depends on an unproved premise.
- **Evidence owner:** the authorised person, document source or process responsible for resolving a material gap.
- **Recheck trigger:** the event that requires the conclusion to be reopened.

## Evidence labels

Use six explicit labels:

1. stated fact;
2. derived fact;
3. supported inference;
4. assumption;
5. contradiction; and
6. evidence gap.

A contradiction or gap prevents downstream claims from remaining unconditional.

## C-O-N-T-I-N-U-E workflow

1. **Classify** the conductive part and name defining and missing facts.
2. **Outline** endpoints and intermediate links.
3. **Name** the six evidence labels.
4. **Test** presence, identity, connection, continuity, suitability and outcome separately.
5. **Identify** the first unsupported transition.
6. **Nominate** an evidence owner and recheck trigger.
7. **Use** bounded supported, conditional or unresolved language.
8. **End** at the authority boundary.

## Practical application

The learner classifies original conductive-part scenarios, constructs a claim-dependency ledger, keeps competing interpretations open, and rebuilds the reasoning when at least two material conditions change. Transfer conditions include outdated drawings, unidentified endpoints, ambiguous continuity records, possible parallel paths, changed enclosure construction and alternative supplies.

## Assessment relevance

Observable evidence includes:

- classification based on equipment relationship, accessibility and fault possibility rather than metal alone;
- separate identification of endpoints and intermediate links;
- distinction between presence, identity, connection, continuity, suitability and protective outcome;
- explicit evidence labels for every material claim;
- identification of the first unsupported transition;
- bounded consequence reasoning without unsupported diagnosis;
- evidence owners and recheck triggers;
- changed-context transfer using at least two material changes; and
- refusal to access, trace, test, alter, energise or approve beyond authority.

Criterion states are **secure**, **developing**, **unsupported** and `stop-required`. These are educational planning states, not official grades or competency decisions. Strength in one criterion cannot cancel an unsupported or `stop-required` result elsewhere.

## Safety boundary

This is a written reasoning module. It authorises no switching, isolation, opening, proving, tracing, measurement, testing, disconnection, reconnection, alteration, repair, energisation, commissioning, certification or verification.
