# 12-Week Day 16 - Protective Earthing Continuity and Exposed Conductive Parts

## Purpose

This note supports written reasoning about exposed conductive parts and protective-earthing continuity. It separates a visible or described connection from continuity, suitability and a verified protective outcome.

## Core concepts

- **Protective-earthing continuity:** an evidence-dependent condition in which the required protective path is electrically continuous between relevant endpoints.
- **Exposed conductive part:** a touchable conductive equipment part that is not normally live but may become live under a fault; exact classification remains source-dependent.
- **Described connection:** a connection shown or stated in a record, label or drawing.
- **Continuous path:** an electrically unbroken path between stated endpoints, established through authorised evidence rather than appearance.
- **Suitable protective path:** a continuous path whose construction and characteristics also meet applicable protective requirements.
- **Verified protective outcome:** a conclusion requiring all relevant arrangement, path, protection and verification evidence.
- **Discontinuity:** an interruption or ineffective link in a path; incomplete records do not identify its location or cause.
- **Claim boundary:** the strongest conclusion supported without assumption.

## C-O-N-T-I-N-U-E workflow

1. **Classify** the conductive part.
2. **Outline** the intended path and endpoints.
3. **Note** supplied, derived, assumed and missing evidence.
4. **Test** presence, connection, continuity, suitability and outcome separately.
5. **Identify** possible consequences of a break without diagnosing it.
6. **Name** the unresolved authorised requirement or evidence.
7. **Use** bounded supported, conditional or unresolved language.
8. **End** at the authority boundary.

## Practical application

The learner classifies original conductive-part scenarios, builds an evidence ladder for each path claim, and reopens conclusions when drawings are outdated, endpoints are undocumented, records are incomplete, enclosure construction changes or another supply is introduced.

## Assessment relevance

Observable evidence includes:

- classification based on equipment relationship, accessibility and fault possibility rather than metal alone;
- separate identification of endpoints and intermediate links;
- distinction between presence, connection, continuity, suitability and protective outcome;
- explicit identification of assumptions and missing evidence;
- bounded consequence reasoning without unsupported diagnosis;
- changed-condition transfer; and
- refusal to access, trace, test, alter or approve beyond authority.
