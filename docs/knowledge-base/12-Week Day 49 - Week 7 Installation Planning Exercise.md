# 12-Week Day 49 - Week 7 Installation Planning Exercise

## Purpose

Integrates [[12-Week Day 43 - Wiring-System Selection and Mechanical Protection]], [[12-Week Day 44 - Environmental Influences, Segregation and Support Concepts]], [[12-Week Day 45 - Consumer Mains, Submains and Final Subcircuits]], [[12-Week Day 46 - Fixed Appliances and Local Isolation Reasoning]], [[12-Week Day 47 - Rest, Retrieval and Installation-Defect Correction]] and [[12-Week Day 48 - Motors, Associated Protection and Control Boundaries]] into one evidence-controlled paper plan.

## Learning module

- [Day 49 — Week 7 Installation Planning Exercise](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-49-week-7-installation-planning-exercise.md)

## Core method

Use **P-L-A-N-I-T**:

1. place installation, task, source, evidence, authority and decision boundaries;
2. list route conditions, operating states, evidence provenance, confidence and contradictions;
3. apply the relevant Week 7 workflows without carrying forward stale conclusions;
4. name dependencies and stop at the first unsupported transition;
5. integrate supported, provisional and unresolved claims into a bounded plan; and
6. test transfer under at least two material changes, reopening affected dependencies.

## Core concepts

- **Design basis:** the supported and explicitly assumed inputs on which the plan depends.
- **Dependency:** a conclusion that may change when an upstream condition changes.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest step where a claim exceeds evidence.
- **Evidence owner:** the authorised source, person or reviewer responsible for resolving a gap.
- **Recheck trigger:** evidence or change that requires a conclusion to reopen.
- **Material change:** a change capable of altering a source, route, operating state, protective function, isolation boundary or planning conclusion.
- **Bounded plan:** an educational proposal limited to available evidence and authority.

Evidence states are stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction and evidence gap. Confidence is recorded separately because certainty does not establish correctness or provenance.

## Practical applications

- map normal, alternate and control paths for a fictional workshop upgrade;
- segment routes and classify conditions;
- build an evidence ledger with provenance, contradictions, owners and triggers;
- identify at least six cross-domain dependencies;
- stop dependent claims at the first unsupported transition;
- revise the plan after two material changes; and
- justify both reopened and unaffected conclusions.

## Assessment relevance

Evaluate boundary completeness, source accounting, evidence classification, dependency visibility, contradiction handling, first-unsupported-transition control, evidence ownership, change propagation and two-condition transfer independently.

Use **secure**, **developing**, **unsupported** and `stop-required` as educational planning states. They are not official grades, competency outcomes, defect classifications, compliance decisions or technical approvals. No aggregate score or unofficial pass threshold applies.

## Blocking conditions

Readiness is blocked by an omitted credible energy source; invented values, functions or conditions; hidden contradictions; claims beyond the first unsupported transition; missing evidence owners or triggers; incomplete downstream reopening; fewer than two material transfer changes; fatigue; or unauthorised practical activity.
