# 12-Week Day 51 - Bathrooms, Showers and Other Wet-Area Reasoning

## Purpose

Applies [[12-Week Day 50 - Special-Location Method - Classify, Map Zones and Verify Sources]] to bathrooms, showers and other wet-area scenarios through evidence-controlled source, exposure and equipment reasoning without reproducing official zones or dimensions.

## Learning module

- [Day 51 — Bathrooms, Showers and Other Wet-Area Reasoning](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-51-bathrooms-showers-and-other-wet-area-reasoning.md)

## Core method

Use **W-A-T-E-R** after **Z-O-N-E-S**:

1. identify water sources, operating states and explicit boundaries;
2. create original plan and elevation maps with evidence provenance;
3. test current source applicability without importing remembered dimensions;
4. separate equipment position, suitability, supply, additional protection, switching and isolation; and
5. record the first unsupported transition, evidence owner, recheck trigger and downstream reopening.

## Core concepts

- **Source state:** a supported fixed, movable, cleaning or other operating arrangement relevant to exposure.
- **Original source-and-reach map:** a learner-created plan and elevation; it is not an official zone diagram.
- **Equipment position:** the supported location and orientation of an item.
- **Equipment suitability:** evidence that the item is appropriate for the actual environment and intended use.
- **Additional protection:** a supplementary layer that does not replace all other applicable controls.
- **Provenance:** source, date or revision and scenario linkage for evidence.
- **Competing interpretations:** plausible readings retained until evidence resolves them.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest point at which a claim exceeds evidence.
- **Evidence owner:** the authorised source, person or reviewer responsible for a gap.
- **Recheck trigger:** the evidence or material change that requires reconsideration.

Evidence states are stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction and evidence gap. Confidence is recorded separately.

## Practical applications

- define location, activity, source, exposure, equipment, evidence, authority and decision boundaries;
- map fixed, movable and cleaning water-source states on original plan and elevation views;
- classify evidence and retain contradictions;
- separate position, suitability and interacting-control claims;
- stop at the first unsupported transition;
- assign evidence owners and recheck triggers; and
- reopen affected conclusions after both a longer movable source and changed screen or equipment geometry.

## Assessment relevance

Evaluate boundary completeness, source-state inventory, spatial mapping, evidence classification, provenance, applicability, equipment/control separation, contradiction handling, first-unsupported-transition control, ownership, change propagation and two-condition transfer independently.

Use **secure**, **developing**, **unsupported** and `stop-required` only as educational planning states. They are not official grades, competency outcomes, defect classifications, compliance decisions or technical approvals.

## Blocking conditions

Readiness is blocked by omitted credible sources, an original map presented as an official zone diagram, invented dimensions or equipment permissions, hidden contradictions, position treated as proof of suitability, missing owners or triggers, incomplete downstream reopening, inadequate transfer or unauthorised practical activity.

## Safety and references

This note authorises no site classification, approach, measurement, opening, switching, isolation, proving de-energised, testing, installation, alteration, repair, energisation, commissioning, certification, design approval or field verification.

Exact zone definitions, dimensions, equipment restrictions, protection requirements, source treatment, installation methods, manufacturer requirements and official assessment expectations require current authorised sources and qualified review.
