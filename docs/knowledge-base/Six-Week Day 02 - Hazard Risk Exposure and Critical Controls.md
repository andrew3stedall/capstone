# Six-Week Day 02 — Hazard, Risk, Exposure and Critical Controls

## Purpose

This note connects the six-week program's opening source hierarchy to safety reasoning. The learner must separate what is observed from what is assumed, map how a hazard could reach a person or asset, identify critical controls, and stop when those controls or the learner's authority cannot be verified.

## Core model

Use **S-C-O-P-E**:

- **State the scenario** — identify the task, people, equipment, environment, energy sources and observed facts.
- **Choose the controlling source family** — locate the current legislation, regulator guidance, authorised standard, network requirement, manufacturer information, RTO direction or approved workplace procedure that governs the decision.
- **Outline the exposure chain** — connect hazard, exposure pathway and plausible consequence.
- **Prove the controls** — identify critical controls and the evidence needed to show they are present and effective.
- **Escalate uncertainty** — stop when authority, equipment state, isolation status, control integrity or source applicability is unresolved.

The reusable reasoning chain is:

**hazard → exposure pathway → consequence → controls → verification → residual uncertainty**

## Practical application

For each paper-based scenario, record:

1. observed facts;
2. hazard source;
3. exposure pathway;
4. plausible consequence;
5. controls and their intended functions;
6. critical controls;
7. evidence of control effectiveness;
8. authority and supervision boundary;
9. unresolved assumptions;
10. decision to continue in simulation, obtain evidence, or stop and escalate.

A changed-scenario re-attempt should alter an environmental condition, exposed person or possible energy source so the learner must transfer the method rather than copy corrections.

## Assessment relevance

This reasoning supports safe explanation, scenario analysis, inspection thinking and evidence-based decisions. It does not define an official RTO marking scheme or practical procedure. Current assessment arrangements and technical requirements must be confirmed through authorised sources.
