## Core concepts

- Functional switching changes normal equipment operation.
- Isolation establishes a defined separation from all relevant electrical-energy sources under an authorised safe-work system.
- Emergency switching, protective operation and isolation have different purposes and must not be treated as interchangeable.
- A main-switch label does not prove that every conductor and every alternate source is disconnected.
- An isolation boundary must identify the equipment or board, sources, conductors, devices, stored energy and verification point.
- The **S-A-F-E** workflow is: Survey every source; Assign the boundary; Find authorised requirements; Establish evidence or stop.

## Practical application

Use a paper single-line diagram to classify each switching device by apparent function, map normal and alternate sources, identify possible backfeed paths, draft exact isolation-boundary statements and list evidence needed before any field conclusion.

## Assessment relevance

The learner should be able to distinguish switching from isolation, reason from source arrangements rather than labels, identify missing evidence and stop without inventing a field procedure. Exact device, pole, neutral, accessibility, labelling, securing and verification requirements remain `reference_check_required`.
