# 12-Week Day 77 - Week 11 Competency Conference and Targeted Remediation

## Purpose

Interpret the untouched Day 76 submission as evidence, classify criterion-level performance without averaging away blockers, and create a maximum-three-target remediation plan before Week 12.

## Learning module

- [Day 77 — Week 11 Competency Conference and Targeted Remediation](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-77-week-11-competency-conference-and-targeted-remediation.md)

## Core method

Use **C-O-N-F-E-R**: Capture boundaries; Observe literally; Name criterion states; Find first unsupported transitions; Engineer bounded repairs; Record and reopen after change.

## Core concepts

- **Evidence boundary:** the fixed submitted artefacts, timing records and source records available for review.
- **Secure:** independent, consistent and traceable evidence with no unresolved blocker.
- **Developing:** relevant capability is present but inconsistent, incomplete or prompt-dependent.
- **Unsupported:** evidence is absent, conflicting, stale, inapplicable or insufficient.
- **Stop-required:** a safety, authority, identity, source-state or fabricated-evidence issue blocks the affected decision.
- **Non-compensatory blocker:** a weakness that stronger unrelated performance cannot offset.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest unsupported step in a reasoning chain; dependent conclusions remain blocked.
- **Root weakness:** the earliest process, knowledge or evidence failure causing several later errors.
- **Transfer check:** a changed-context task demonstrating whether a repair generalises.
- **Evidence owner:** the authorised person, source or record responsible for closing a gap.
- **Recheck trigger:** the new evidence event that permits reconsideration.
- **Conditional readiness:** progression under named learning and review conditions, not competency or technical approval.

## Practical applications

Conduct an educational evidence conference that:

- freezes the untouched Day 76 submission;
- defines installation, equipment, circuit, source, operating-state, time, evidence, authority and requested-decision boundaries;
- preserves literal evidence and provenance before interpretation;
- classifies evidence as verified, supported but limited, conflicting, stale or superseded, not applicable or missing;
- records `secure`, `developing`, `unsupported` and `stop-required` independently;
- separates correctness, confidence and evidence quality;
- locates first unsupported transitions and root weaknesses;
- selects no more than three remediation targets;
- assigns evidence owners, recheck triggers and changed-context transfer checks; and
- reopens dependencies after material changes.

## Assessment relevance

This conference converts one timed attempt into a bounded Week 12 preparation plan. There is no aggregate score and no unofficial pass threshold. A non-compensatory blocker prevents a readiness recommendation for the affected decision regardless of stronger performance elsewhere.

The 45–60 minute structure is an original educational pacing control, not an official RTO, regulator or licensing assessment duration.

## Blocking conditions

Progress is blocked by unresolved safety-critical identity or source-state boundaries, fabricated or silently transferred evidence, stale evidence treated as current after change, conclusions beyond the first unsupported transition, invented exact requirements or procedures, practical-authority overreach, and competency, compliance or technical-approval claims without qualified review.

## Safety and references

This note authorises no site access, opening, switching, isolation, proving de-energised, testing, measurement, instrument use, alteration, repair, energisation, commissioning, acceptance, certification, verification or field fault finding.

Exact duties, procedures, values, limits, test methods, instrument requirements, acceptance criteria, role permissions and official assessment expectations require current authorised sources and qualified review.
