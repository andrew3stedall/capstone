# 12-Week Day 70 - Week 10 Verification and Fault-Diagnosis Checkpoint

## Purpose

Integrate Week 10 verification-evidence interpretation and systematic fault-diagnosis reasoning while preserving boundaries, literal evidence, competing hypotheses, unsupported transitions and authority limits.

## Learning module

- [Day 70 — Week 10 Verification and Fault-Diagnosis Checkpoint](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-70-week-10-verification-and-fault-diagnosis-checkpoint.md)

## Core method

Use **I-N-T-E-G-R-A-T-E**: Identify ten boundaries; Name literal evidence and its state; Trace provenance and applicability; Establish dependencies and first unsupported transitions; Generate competing hypotheses; Review discrimination and contradiction; Assign owners and triggers; Test two-change transfer; End with independent readiness states and bounded conclusions.

## Core concepts

- **Ten boundaries:** installation, equipment, circuit, protective device, source, operating state, time, evidence, authority and decision.
- **Six evidence states:** stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction and evidence gap.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest unsupported step in a claim chain; all dependent claims remain unsupported.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** an expected observation capable of weakening or rejecting a hypothesis.
- **Discriminating evidence:** evidence that changes the relative credibility of competing explanations.
- **Evidence owner:** the authorised source, custodian or qualified person responsible for resolving a blocker.
- **Recheck trigger:** new evidence or a material change that reopens affected reasoning.
- **Confidence calibration:** confidence remains separate from correctness and evidence quality.
- **Non-compensatory blocker:** a serious weakness that cannot be averaged away.
- **Educational readiness states:** `secure`, `developing`, `unsupported` and `stop-required`; these are not official grades, verification outcomes or competency decisions.

## Practical applications

Complete a fictional checkpoint dossier that:

- defines all ten boundaries;
- classifies every item into one of six evidence states;
- stops each claim chain at its first unsupported transition;
- maintains at least three hypotheses with falsifiable predictions;
- separates confidence, correctness and evidence quality;
- assigns owners and recheck triggers;
- treats events separately when coverage differs; and
- reopens affected dependencies after two sequential material changes.

## Assessment relevance

Assess boundary control, evidence control, dependency control, hypothesis control, change control, ownership and calibration, and conclusion/safety independently. There is no aggregate score. A blocking `unsupported` criterion or any `stop-required` state cannot be offset by stronger work elsewhere.

## Blocking conditions

Progress is blocked by invented or altered evidence, hidden contradictions, unsupported cross-purpose transfer, reasoning beyond a first unsupported transition, confidence treated as proof, event coverage overgeneralised, material changes ignored, root-cause declarations without discriminating evidence, practical directions or unsupported verification, acceptance, certification, compliance or technical-approval claims.

## Safety and references
