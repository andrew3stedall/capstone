## Core concepts

- A switchboard is an engineered assembly whose enclosure, devices, conductors, supports, barriers, markings and environment interact.
- Functional-zone mapping separates incoming supply, switching, protection, distribution, neutral, protective-earthing, control and alternate-supply functions.
- Barrier, separation and segregation are related but not interchangeable concepts.
- Physical spare space does not prove electrical, thermal, fault or manufacturer-approved capacity.
- The **B-O-A-R-D** workflow is: Bound the assembly; Organise by function; Assess interactions; Retrieve authorised evidence; Decide within evidence.
- A paper review must distinguish confirmed, assumed and missing evidence.

## Practical application

Use drawings, schedules and manufacturer information to map a fictional board, trace sources and terminations, review barriers and access, identify thermal and fault questions, and prepare a bounded evidence request for a proposed circuit extension.

## Assessment relevance

The learner should be able to reason about the board as an assembly rather than judge isolated components. Exact enclosure, barrier, access, segregation, conductor, neutral, earth, thermal, fault-rating, identification and compatibility requirements remain `reference_check_required`.
