# 12-Week Day 67 - Systematic Fault-Finding Workflow and Hypothesis Control

## Purpose

Provide a document-only diagnostic method for separating symptoms from observations, controlling nine boundaries, maintaining competing hypotheses, using falsifiable predictions and discriminating evidence, stopping at unsupported transitions and reopening reasoning after material changes.

## Learning module

- [Day 67 — Systematic Fault-Finding Workflow and Hypothesis Control](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-67-systematic-fault-finding-workflow-and-hypothesis-control.md)

## Core method

Use **H-Y-P-O-T-H-E-S-I-S**: Hold symptom apart from observation; Yield nine boundaries; Preserve literal evidence; Offer competing hypotheses; Tell falsifiable predictions; Hunt for discriminating evidence; Evaluate provenance and contradictions; Shift ranking provisionally; Identify owners and triggers; State the bounded conclusion.

## Core concepts

- **Nine boundaries:** installation, circuit, equipment, source, operating state, time, evidence, authority and decision.
- **Evidence states:** stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction and evidence gap.
- **Falsifiable prediction:** a specific expectation that authorised evidence could contradict.
- **Discriminating evidence:** evidence that changes the relative credibility of alternatives.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest unsupported step in a claim chain; dependent claims remain unsupported.
- **Confidence calibration:** confidence is recorded separately from correctness and evidence quality.
- **Competing interpretations:** plausible explanations retained without promotion to fact.
- **Evidence owner:** authorised source, custodian or qualified person responsible for resolving a blocker.
- **Recheck trigger:** a new record, clarification or material change that reopens affected reasoning.
- **Educational readiness states:** secure, developing, unsupported and `stop-required`; not official grades or technical decisions.

## Practical applications

For a fictional dossier:

- preserve the exact symptom separately from verified observations;
- define all nine boundaries;
- classify material claims and record provenance;
- generate at least three hypotheses;
- state a falsifiable prediction and discriminator for each;
- locate the first unsupported transition;
- retain competing interpretations;
- assign evidence owners and recheck triggers;
- reopen dependencies after two sequential material changes; and
- communicate a bounded conclusion without root-cause, compliance, acceptance or certification claims.

## Assessment relevance

Assess symptom separation, boundary control, hypothesis breadth, prediction quality, provenance, dependency control, change propagation, ownership and safety communication independently. There is no aggregate score. A blocking unsupported criterion or any `stop-required` state cannot be offset by stronger work elsewhere.

## Blocking conditions

Progress is blocked by invented or altered evidence, hidden contradictions, first-guess diagnosis, ignored high-consequence alternatives, historical evidence treated as current without applicability, continued reasoning beyond the first unsupported transition, blockers without owners or triggers, ignored material changes, practical troubleshooting directions, or unsupported root-cause, safety, compliance, acceptance, certification or technical-approval claims.
