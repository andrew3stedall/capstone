# 12-Week Day 50 - Special-Location Method - Classify, Map Zones and Verify Sources

## Purpose

Extends [[12-Week Day 49 - Week 7 Installation Planning Exercise]] with an evidence-controlled method for classifying special locations before selecting location-specific requirements.

## Learning module

- [Day 50 — Special-Location Method: Classify, Map Zones and Verify Sources](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-50-special-location-method-classify-map-zones-and-verify-sources.md)

## Core method

Use **Z-O-N-E-S**:

1. zero in on location, activity, exposure, evidence, authority and decision boundaries;
2. outline an original exposure map without inventing official zones;
3. name candidate general, special-location, manufacturer, regulator and supply sources;
4. establish currency, scope, definitions, exceptions, scenario match and provenance; and
5. stop at the first unsupported transition, state bounded conclusions and schedule rechecks.

## Core concepts

- **Classification fact:** supported information that may determine the applicable rule set.
- **Exposure source:** the origin of the relevant condition.
- **Exposure path:** the supported way the condition may reach a person or installation part.
- **Learner-created exposure map:** an original educational sketch, not an official zone diagram.
- **Applicability:** whether a source governs the stated jurisdiction, edition and scenario.
- **Competing interpretations:** plausible classifications retained until evidence resolves them.
- **First unsupported transition:** the earliest step where a claim exceeds evidence.
- **Evidence owner:** the authorised source, person or reviewer responsible for a gap.
- **Recheck trigger:** the evidence or change that requires reconsideration.
- **Material change:** a change capable of altering classification, exposure or an interacting control.

Evidence states are stated fact, derived fact, supported inference, assumption, contradiction and evidence gap. Confidence is recorded separately.

## Practical applications

- classify from facts rather than room names;
- build original exposure maps;
- maintain competing interpretations;
- verify source applicability and provenance;
- identify the first unsupported transition;
- assign evidence owners and recheck triggers; and
- reopen affected conclusions after two material scenario changes.

## Assessment relevance

Evaluate boundary completeness, evidence classification, exposure mapping, source applicability, contradiction handling, first-unsupported-transition control, ownership, change propagation and transfer independently.

Use **secure**, **developing**, **unsupported** and `stop-required` only as educational planning states. They are not official grades, competency outcomes, defect classifications, compliance decisions or technical approvals.

## Blocking conditions

Readiness is blocked by invented official boundaries or values, an omitted credible source, hidden contradictions, claims beyond available evidence or authority, missing owners or triggers, incomplete downstream reopening, inadequate transfer or unauthorised practical activity.
