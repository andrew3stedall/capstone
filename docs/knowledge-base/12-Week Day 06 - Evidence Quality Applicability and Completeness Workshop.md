## Core concepts

- **Evidence quality:** authority, currency, traceability, integrity and independence of an evidence item.
- **Applicability:** whether valid evidence governs the actual jurisdiction, installation, supply, circuit, equipment, location and authority conditions.
- **Completeness:** whether every material premise required for the proposed conclusion is supported.
- **Corroboration:** independent support that genuinely strengthens a claim rather than repeating the same weak source.
- **Contradiction:** applicable evidence supporting incompatible conclusions, requiring hierarchy and scope checks rather than averaging.
- **Bounded conclusion:** an outcome no stronger than the evidence: supported, conditionally supported, insufficient evidence or outside authority.

## C-L-E-A-R workflow

1. **Claim** — state the exact conclusion being tested.
2. **Locate evidence** — identify scenario facts and candidate controlling sources.
3. **Evaluate quality** — check authority, currency, traceability, integrity and independence.
4. **Apply conditions** — map definitions, scope, exceptions, dependencies and authority limits.
5. **Review completeness** — identify missing premises or contradictions and state a bounded outcome.

Passing one evidence test does not guarantee the others. A current authoritative source may still be irrelevant to the scenario or insufficient for the exact conclusion.

## Practical application

The learner sorts fictional evidence cards, maps scenario conditions, audits the premises behind a proposed protective-arrangement conclusion and then changes one material condition. The final answer must show which evidence remains valid, which becomes inapplicable and whether the conclusion is supported, conditional, insufficient or outside authority.

## Assessment relevance

Day 6 assesses whether the learner can resist keyword confirmation, silent assumptions and unsupported binary compliance claims. Observable evidence includes an exact claim, source-quality checks, an applicability map, a completeness audit, a changed-scenario revision and a bounded conclusion.

## Safety boundary

This is a written evidence workshop. It authorises no switching, isolation, testing, opening equipment, resetting, disconnection, alteration, repair, energisation, commissioning, verification or practical demonstration. Exact clauses, limits, values, device characteristics, procedures and official assessment rules remain `reference_check_required`.
