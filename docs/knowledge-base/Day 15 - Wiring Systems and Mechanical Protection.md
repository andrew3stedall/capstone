# Day 15 - Wiring Systems and Mechanical Protection

Day 15 extends cable selection into complete-route reasoning. A suitable conductor is only one part of a wiring system; supports, enclosures, fittings, entries, transitions and foreseeable activities must remain coherent across the route.

## R-O-U-T-E workflow

1. **Record** the complete route and transition points.
2. **Observe** each exposure profile and foreseeable activity.
3. **Use** current authorised sources and manufacturer information.
4. **Test** each control strategy, boundary and transition.
5. **Evidence** the bounded selection, gaps and reopening triggers.

## Core concepts

- A **wiring system** includes conductors, supports, containment, protection, fittings, entries, joints and terminations.
- An **exposure zone** is a route segment with a distinct hazard profile.
- A **transition point** is where environment, support, enclosure, direction, movement or termination changes.
- A **foreseeable activity** includes installation, use, cleaning, maintenance, fixing, excavation, pests, equipment movement and other trades.
- Control strategies include route avoidance, position, construction, enclosure or guarding, and warning of residual risk.

## Practical application

For a fictional workshop route:

1. map every segment and transition;
2. identify mechanical, environmental, thermal, fire, access and service-interaction hazards;
3. compare route change, cable construction and added physical protection;
4. test continuity through supports, bends, entries, joints and terminations;
5. record missing substrate, route and compatibility evidence;
6. write a bounded selection;
7. reopen affected zones after a route, equipment or activity change.

## Assessment relevance

The module uses a six-category, 12-point educational rubric covering route mapping, hazard mechanisms, control comparison, transition continuity, evidence discipline and safety communication. Critical errors override the score. This is not an official assessment rule.

## Misconceptions to track

- Current-carrying capacity alone establishes suitability.
- Concealed automatically means mechanically protected.
- One enclosure choice applies unchanged to the entire route.
- Protecting the straight section is enough.
- A product label proves compatibility with every fitting and environment.
- A warning replaces physical suitability.
- A route change affects only the changed segment.
