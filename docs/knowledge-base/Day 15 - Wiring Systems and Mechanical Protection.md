## Core idea

A cable is only one component of a wiring system. Selection must account for the complete route, supports, enclosures, fittings, entries, joints and terminations, and must remain suitable for foreseeable mechanical, environmental, thermal, fire and maintenance exposures.

The module uses the **R-O-U-T-E** workflow:

1. **Record** the complete route and transitions.
2. **Observe** each exposure profile.
3. **Use** current authorised source families.
4. **Test** each proposed protection method and its boundaries.
5. **Evidence** a bounded selection and unresolved review needs.

## Practical application

The learner divides a fictional workshop route into exposure zones, compares route change, cable construction and added physical protection, then writes a bounded recommendation without inventing official dimensions or classifications.

## Assessment relevance

The topic develops route interpretation, wiring-system selection, mechanical-protection reasoning, evidence discipline and the ability to recognise when a physically plausible proposal is not sufficiently supported.

## Common misconceptions

- current-carrying capacity alone establishes suitability;
- concealed automatically means mechanically protected;
- one enclosure choice applies unchanged to the entire route;
- protecting the straight section is enough even when entries and transitions remain vulnerable;
- a product label replaces compatibility and installation evidence.

## Safety boundary

This note does not authorise opening, drilling, excavation, testing, live access, repair or alteration. Exact wiring-system classifications, permitted arrangements, dimensions, fire requirements, segregation rules and jurisdiction-specific obligations require current authorised sources and qualified review.
