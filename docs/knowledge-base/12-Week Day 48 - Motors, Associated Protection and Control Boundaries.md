# 12-Week Day 48 - Motors, Associated Protection and Control Boundaries

Day 48 extends [[12-Week Day 46 - Fixed Appliances and Local Isolation Reasoning]] into motor power, control, protection and driven-equipment boundaries.

## Learning module

- [Day 48 — Motors, Associated Protection and Control Boundaries](../learning-plans/12-week/modules/day-48-motors-associated-protection-and-control-boundaries.md)

## Core workflow

Use **M-O-T-O-R-S**: map the system, outline operating states, tag documented functions, obtain authorised evidence, relate protection and control interactions, then state a bounded conclusion.

## Key distinctions

- A power circuit supplies the motor; a control circuit commands operation.
- Starting, overload and fault conditions are different reasoning cases.
- A stop command does not by itself prove isolation.
- The driven machine and automatic restart conditions remain part of the system boundary.

## Practical application

Learners map a fictional exhaust-fan motor, identify power and control sources, distinguish protection functions, review operating states and reopen the analysis when a variable-speed controller or alternate supply is introduced.

## Assessment relevance

A strong response maps all sources and states, distinguishes documented functions, connects protection with conductors and driven equipment, and refuses unsupported safe-state or setting claims.

## Safety boundary

This note authorises no switching, isolation, testing, setting adjustment, opening, mechanical intervention, energisation, commissioning or verification.
