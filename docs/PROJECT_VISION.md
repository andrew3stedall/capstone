# Project Vision

## Problem

Electrical apprentices often prepare for capstone assessment from fragmented class notes, workplace experience, standards, handouts and informal advice. Notes are commonly incomplete, difficult to organise and poorly suited to repeated revision. The assessment requires candidates to connect theory, installation practice, safe isolation, verification, fault finding and documentation under pressure.

## Vision

Capstone Ready will be a comprehensive online study companion that helps apprentices move from **reference material** to **retained knowledge** and then to **safe practical application**.

It should eventually provide:

- a structured encyclopaedia of capstone-relevant concepts;
- clear separation between theory and practical learning;
- interactive calculations, diagrams, wiring and test simulations;
- fault-finding scenarios;
- personalised highlights, notes and revision collections;
- editable flashcards and spaced repetition;
- progress, mastery and weak-area analysis;
- realistic practice and mock-assessment modes;
- jurisdiction, RTO and standards-version context.

## Primary user

An Australian electrical apprentice approaching final capstone assessment who:

- has practical trade experience but inconsistent notes;
- studies mainly on a phone, tablet or laptop;
- needs concise explanations during short study sessions;
- also needs deeper reference material when a topic is unclear;
- benefits from visual and practical learning;
- must remember safety-critical sequences accurately.

## Secondary users

- earlier-stage apprentices building foundational knowledge;
- trainers recommending supplementary revision;
- recently licensed electricians refreshing knowledge;
- employers supporting apprentice development.

## Product principles

### Readability before decoration

This is a technical study tool used for long reading sessions and under cognitive load. Typography, contrast, spacing and navigation must take priority over fashionable visual effects.

### Reference depth without forced linear reading

The library may become encyclopaedic, but learners should not need to read every page. Summaries, highlights, saved notes, retrieval practice and recommendations should reduce cognitive overload.

### Theory connected to practice

Every major rule or concept should connect to at least one of:

- a scenario;
- a calculation;
- a diagram;
- a test procedure;
- a fault;
- a simulator exercise;
- an assessment-style question.

### Safety over gamification

Gamification may encourage practice, but it must never reward unsafe shortcuts, speed over correct process, or confidence unsupported by competence.

### Evidence and version awareness

Learning content should identify its source category, applicable jurisdiction, standards edition and review date. Statements that vary by RTO should not be presented as universal facts.

## Core learning domains

1. Capstone orientation and assessment readiness
2. Safety and electrical risk
3. Electrical fundamentals and calculations
4. Wiring rules and installation design
5. Conductors and wiring systems
6. Control, switching and protection
7. Electrical equipment and accessories
8. Earthing, bonding and MEN
9. Special installations and locations
10. Inspection, testing and verification
11. Fault finding and commissioning
12. Motors, transformers and energy systems
13. Practical installation rehearsal
14. Mock assessment and remediation

## Long-term outcome

The product should become a trusted learning and rehearsal layer around the formal apprenticeship curriculum and current official sources—not a substitute for them.
