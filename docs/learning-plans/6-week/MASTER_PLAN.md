# Six-Week Capstone Learning Plan

This is the canonical sequence for the six-week program. It is designed for a learner with prior trade-school and workplace exposure who needs more spacing, retrieval and application than the intensive four-week stream provides.

The sequence is not a stretched copy of the four-week plan. Concepts are separated into smaller learning decisions, revisited in varied contexts and integrated progressively through design, inspection, verification, fault-finding and mock-assessment work.

## Study rhythm

- Monday–Thursday: 45–60 minutes
- Friday: deliberate rest, retrieval or catch-up only, maximum 30 minutes
- Saturday: 90–120 minutes
- Sunday: 45–75 minutes
- Weekly target: approximately 6–8 hours

## Module production standard

Every scheduled block, including rest, retrieval and catch-up days, has a dedicated eight-beat educational module: Outcome and entry check; Why it matters; Core concepts and terminology; Rule-finding workflow; Visual model or worked example; Practical application; Common errors and safety checkpoint; Retrieval and next links.

Every module includes compatible YAML metadata, explicit prerequisites, related notes, a source/currency notice and a review status. Exact clause numbers, limits, test values, jurisdiction-specific claims and safety-critical procedures require verification against authorised current material before technical approval.

## Cumulative learning design

The sequence uses spaced retrieval, interleaving, worked-example fading, error-driven remediation, confidence calibration and cumulative application checkpoints.
