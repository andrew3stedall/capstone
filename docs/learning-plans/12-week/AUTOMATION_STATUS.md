## Day 3 design decisions

- Day 3 separates role, competence, task authority, scope, supervision and stop conditions before any practical-procedure content.
- The A-U-T-H-O-R-I-T-Y workflow requires exact activity verbs, a valid authority source, defined oversight, explicit exclusions and re-authorisation triggers.
- The authority-envelope model treats permission as conditional on overlapping role, competence, method, supervision and site/task boundaries.
- Applications remain written or trainer-approved scenarios and deliberately stop before technical interaction with equipment.
- The module uses original learner-centred organisation, scenarios, diagrams and wording rather than reproducing legal, standards or RTO source structures.

## Review flags

- Day 3 remains `review-required` and `reference_check_required`.
- Licensing boundaries, legal duties, supervision requirements, RTO conditions, workplace responsibilities, approved methods and task authority require verification against current authorised sources.
- The module states no universal supervision ratio, licence rule, safe-work method, technical value, practical test sequence or official assessment threshold.
- The module does not authorise switching, isolation, testing, opening equipment, resetting, disconnection, alteration, repair, rescue, energisation or verification.
- Automated content is not `technically-reviewed`.
