## Day 4 design decisions

- Day 4 teaches source navigation as a reproducible evidence process rather than clause-number recall.
- The T-R-A-C-E workflow separates scenario translation, source routing, context analysis, dependency checking and evidence recording.
- The three-round drill fades support while preserving source-identity, currency, applicability and stop checks.
- No exact clause, technical limit, standards table, figure or source passage is reproduced.
- Practical authority remains separate from the ability to locate technical material.

## Review flags

- Day 4 remains `review-required` and `reference_check_required`.
- The authorised source, edition, amendment status, normative status, applicability and dependencies require verification for the relevant jurisdiction and assessment context.
- The module states no technical limit, practical test sequence, official assessment threshold or universal source hierarchy.
- The module does not authorise switching, isolation, testing, opening equipment, resetting, disconnection, alteration, repair, energisation or verification.
- Automated content is not `technically-reviewed`.
