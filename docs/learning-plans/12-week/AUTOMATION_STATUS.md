## Day 14 design decisions

- Day 14 integrates Week 2 concepts rather than adding unnecessary new electrical theory.
- Current condition, current path, protection function, evidence and claim level remain separate reasoning layers.
- The checkpoint tests overload, short-circuit, earth-fault, residual-current and work-control distinctions without supplying technical values.
- Device names never substitute for identifying the hazard, protected interest or required function.
- A 20-point rubric requires no zero in current-path reasoning, evidence control or safety boundary.
- Learners below threshold complete one varied remediation attempt before progressing.

## Review flags

- Day 14 remains `review-required`, `reference_check_required`, safety-critical and not `technically-reviewed`.
- Exact clauses, limits, device characteristics, fault levels, operating times, supply arrangements, test methods and jurisdiction-specific requirements require qualified checking.
- I-N-T-E-G-R-A-T-E, fictional scenarios, diagrams, matrices and rubric are original educational constructs.
- No standards table, figure, device curve, systematic clause sequence, exact technical value or official assessment material was reproduced.
- The module authorises no switching, isolation, opening, proving, measurement, testing, resetting, fault creation, alteration, repair, energisation, commissioning, certification or verification.
