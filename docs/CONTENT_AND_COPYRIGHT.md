# Content, Standards and Copyright Policy

## Purpose

Capstone Ready will rely on current electrical standards and official guidance as source material, while publishing original educational content. This document sets the working rules for content creation.

This is a product policy, not legal advice. Obtain Australian legal advice and any necessary licences before commercial release.

## AS/NZS 3000 source status

AS/NZS 3000:2018 is a copyrighted joint Australian/New Zealand Standard. The uploaded copy states that reproduction or copying requires permission unless otherwise permitted by applicable copyright legislation.

The repository must therefore not contain:

- the complete PDF or scans unless the project has explicit distribution rights;
- systematic verbatim extraction of clauses;
- copied tables, figures or diagrams;
- a near-complete substitute for purchasing or accessing the Standard;
- lightly edited clause-by-clause paraphrases that preserve the original structure and expression too closely.

A private repository does not automatically remove copyright risk.

## Permitted working approach

The product may safely be designed around:

- section, clause and topic references;
- original explanations of underlying ideas;
- independently created examples and diagrams;
- scenarios that teach application;
- original quizzes and simulations;
- links or directions to authorised official access;
- short quotations only where legally justified, necessary and attributed;
- public regulator and safety guidance used under its applicable terms.

Facts, principles and methods are not protected in the same way as the Standard's particular wording, selection, arrangement, tables and illustrations. However, transformation must be genuine.

## Transformation test

Before publishing material derived from a Standard, ask:

1. Is the wording original?
2. Is the organisation driven by learner needs rather than the Standard's clause sequence?
3. Are examples, diagrams and tables independently created?
4. Does it add explanation, comparison, scenario practice or interaction?
5. Could a user reconstruct a substantial part of the Standard from the product?
6. Would this content reduce the need to obtain authorised access to the Standard?

A “yes” to questions 5 or 6 indicates elevated risk and requires redesign or licensing review.

## Safe module pattern

Do not produce a clause substitute. Use this structure:

```text
Learning objective
Why the topic matters
Plain-language concept
Original example
Decision process
Common mistakes
Practical scenario
Question or simulation
Reference to official clause/source
Currency notice
```

## Tables

High-risk approach:

- copying the same rows, columns, values, headings and notes;
- changing fonts or colours while retaining the table;
- transcribing a table into JSON or a calculator that exposes the full dataset.

Lower-risk approach:

- explain how a practitioner selects the relevant official table;
- teach what variables affect a decision;
- use a small original worked example with independently selected values;
- create a decision tree that directs the learner to consult the authorised source;
- licence data where the application requires full table functionality.

A disclaimer does not cure unauthorised copying.

## Figures and diagrams

Do not trace or restyle copyrighted figures. Create diagrams from first principles using original layout, labels and pedagogical purpose. Preserve technical accuracy but not the original composition.

## Section 2 transformation example

Section 2 of AS/NZS 3000 concerns general arrangement, control and protection, including circuits, maximum demand, conductors, control and isolation, fault and overcurrent protection, RCDs, voltage conditions, arc-fault considerations and switchboards.

Rather than mirroring its clauses, Capstone Ready should organise this material into learner tasks:

1. **Plan an installation** — divide loads into circuits and consider demand.
2. **Choose control points** — distinguish functional switching, isolation and emergency action.
3. **Protect conductors and people** — compare overload, short-circuit, fault and additional protection.
4. **Coordinate devices** — understand why protection must work with conductors and upstream/downstream devices.
5. **Build and inspect a switchboard** — identify functional areas, labelling, accessibility and common unsafe arrangements.

Each task should use original scenarios, diagrams, questions and simulator exercises and then point the learner to the relevant official clauses.

## Source metadata

Every learning item should record:

- source type;
- source title;
- edition/version;
- clause or page reference where applicable;
- jurisdiction;
- date accessed;
- content author;
- technical reviewer;
- last reviewed date;
- whether the item contains licensed material.

## Content statuses

- `draft-unverified`
- `review-required`
- `technically-reviewed`
- `legally-reviewed`
- `published`
- `superseded`

Safety-critical material must not move directly from AI-generated draft to published.

## Uploaded PDF handling

The uploaded PDF may be used in this working session to create an index and plan original modules. It should not be committed to the repository unless the owner confirms that repository storage and redistribution are authorised by the licence.

Instead, the repository contains:

- a bibliographic source record;
- a topic and clause index;
- content coverage status;
- module plans;
- links to authorised access paths.

## Product disclaimer

Suggested baseline wording:

> Capstone Ready is an independent educational resource. It is not affiliated with or endorsed by Standards Australia, Standards New Zealand, an electrical regulator or a registered training organisation. Always use current authorised standards, legislation, regulator guidance and your RTO's approved procedures.

This disclaimer is necessary for clarity but does not replace copyright compliance, technical review or licensing.
